TRANSLATION AND LOCALIZATION SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Translation and Localization Services Agreement (the "Agreement"), bearing
reference number TLS-2025-0089, is entered into as of March 1, 2025 (the
"Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the global media landscape has undergone a profound transformation in
recent decades, driven by the proliferation of digital distribution platforms,
the exponential growth of streaming services, and the increasing demand for
locally relevant content across diverse linguistic and cultural markets. The
international entertainment and media industry now generates in excess of two
trillion dollars annually, with non-English-language markets representing the
fastest-growing segment of global content consumption, fueled by expanding
internet penetration, mobile device adoption, and evolving viewer preferences
that increasingly favor content in their native languages;

WHEREAS, the challenges of effective translation and localization extend far
beyond mere linguistic conversion, encompassing cultural adaptation, tonal
fidelity, regulatory compliance across jurisdictions, synchronization with
audiovisual media, and the preservation of creative intent across fundamentally
different linguistic structures, idiomatic expressions, and cultural reference
frameworks. Studies have consistently demonstrated that viewers are
significantly more likely to engage with and complete viewing of content
presented in their native language, with localized content achieving engagement
rates up to three times higher than subtitled-only alternatives in certain
markets;

WHEREAS, the localization industry has evolved from a relatively narrow
discipline focused on software interface translation into a sophisticated,
technology-enabled ecosystem that integrates computer-assisted translation (CAT)
tools, machine translation (MT) engines, terminology management systems,
translation memory (TM) databases, quality assurance (QA) frameworks, and
project management platforms to deliver high-volume, high-quality translations
across dozens of target languages simultaneously. The integration of neural
machine translation (NMT) and large language models has further accelerated
throughput while simultaneously raising new questions about quality
consistency, stylistic control, and the appropriate role of human post-editing
in the translation workflow;

WHEREAS, GlobalReach Media Corp. has established itself as a leading
international content distributor with operations spanning broadcast television,
streaming platforms, theatrical distribution, and digital media across more
than forty countries, and has identified a strategic need to engage a
specialized language services provider capable of delivering comprehensive
translation and localization services at the scale, quality, and speed required
to support its ambitious international content strategy;

WHEREAS, LinguaGlobal Translations Inc. brings over fifteen years of
specialized experience in media and entertainment localization, with proven
expertise in subtitling, dubbing script adaptation, voice-over script
preparation, marketing material transcreation, and multilingual content
management, supported by a network of over two thousand qualified linguists
across the target languages specified in this Agreement;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to establish a comprehensive framework for the
provision of translation and localization services that balances quality
excellence with operational efficiency, leverages technology to optimize costs
and turnaround times, and provides the flexibility to accommodate fluctuations
in content volume and priority across GlobalReach Media Corp.'s diverse content
portfolio;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the
receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as
follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "CAT Tool" means Computer-Assisted Translation tool, any software
application that supports the translation process by providing access to
translation memories, terminology databases, and linguistic resources,
including but not limited to SDL Trados, memoQ, Phrase (formerly Memsource),
and XTM Cloud.

2.2 "Dubbing" means the process of replacing the original audio track of a
video or film production with a translated audio track performed by voice
actors in the target language, including lip-sync adaptation, timing
adjustments, and audio mixing to ensure synchronization with on-screen action.

2.3 "LQA" means Linguistic Quality Assurance, the systematic process of
evaluating the quality of translated content against established quality
standards, including error identification, categorization, and severity
classification using standardized scoring methodologies.

2.4 "Localization" means the comprehensive process of adapting content,
products, or services to meet the linguistic, cultural, technical, and
regulatory requirements of a specific target market or locale, encompassing
not only translation but also cultural adaptation, format conversion, and
regulatory compliance.

2.5 "Machine Translation" or "MT" means automated translation performed by
software systems, including rule-based machine translation (RBMT), statistical
machine translation (SMT), and neural machine translation (NMT), without
direct human intervention in the initial translation process.

2.6 "Post-Editing" means the review and correction of machine-translated
content by a qualified human linguist to achieve the quality standards
specified for the applicable content type and target language, classified as
light post-editing (focused on intelligibility) or full post-editing (focused
on achieving quality comparable to human translation).

2.7 "Source File" means any document, audio file, video file, subtitle file,
script, marketing material, website content, social media post, or other
content provided by the Client to the Service Provider for translation or
localization under this Agreement.

2.8 "Style Guide" means the comprehensive set of guidelines governing tone,
register, terminology, formatting, and stylistic conventions for translations
produced under this Agreement, developed jointly by the parties for each
target language and updated periodically as needed.

2.9 "Subtitle" means on-screen text that provides a written translation of
dialogue, narration, or other audio content in a video or film production,
formatted in accordance with industry-standard subtitling specifications
including character limits per line, reading speed thresholds, and timing
synchronization requirements.

2.10 "Target Language" means any language into which Source Files are to be
translated or localized under this Agreement, as specified in Section 3 and
the applicable Statement of Work or project instructions.

2.11 "Translation Memory" or "TM" means a linguistic database that stores
previously translated text segments (source-target pairs) for reuse in
subsequent translation projects, enabling consistency across translations and
reducing costs for repetitive or similar content.

2.12 "Transcreation" means the creative adaptation of marketing, advertising,
or promotional content for a target language market, going beyond literal
translation to preserve the emotional impact, cultural relevance, and
persuasive intent of the original content.

2.13 "Voice-Over" means an audio production technique in which a translated
narration or dialogue is recorded and laid over the original audio track of
a video or film production, without lip-sync adaptation, commonly used for
documentary, corporate, and educational content.

2.14 "TEP" means the Translation, Editing, and Proofreading process, the
standard three-step quality workflow in which content is (i) translated by a
qualified translator, (ii) edited by a second qualified linguist who compares
the translation against the source for accuracy and completeness, and (iii)
proofread by a third linguist who reviews the final target-language text for
fluency, formatting, and adherence to the Style Guide.

2.15 "Glossary" means a structured repository of approved terms, phrases,
and expressions for each target language, including definitions, context
guidelines, and usage restrictions, maintained by the Service Provider and
approved by the Client.

2.16 "TMS" means Translation Management System, a software platform used to
manage the end-to-end localization workflow, including project creation,
resource assignment, deadline tracking, quality management, and delivery
coordination.

2.17 "Rush Project" means any project or delivery request for which the
Client has requested turnaround in fewer than forty-eight (48) hours from
receipt of Source Files by the Service Provider.

2.18 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed
by either party to the other in connection with this Agreement, including but
not limited to unreleased media content, scripts, marketing strategies,
distribution plans, financial data, audience metrics, and proprietary
processes, whether disclosed orally, in writing, by electronic transmission,
or by inspection.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive translation
and localization services for the Client's media content portfolio, including
but not limited to the following content types and service categories:

    (a) Subtitling. Creation of time-coded subtitles for video content
    including theatrical releases, television series, streaming originals,
    documentaries, and short-form content, formatted in accordance with
    Client-specified technical specifications and platform requirements.

    (b) Dubbing Scripts. Adaptation of original scripts for dubbed audio
    production, including lip-sync adaptation, dialogue timing, and cultural
    adjustment for theatrical and broadcast distribution.

    (c) Voice-Over Scripts. Preparation of voice-over scripts for documentary,
    corporate, educational, and promotional content, including narration
    adaptation and timing alignment.

    (d) Marketing Materials. Translation and transcreation of marketing
    collateral including posters, key art, trailers, promotional videos,
    social media campaigns, press kits, and advertising copy.

    (e) Press Releases. Translation and cultural adaptation of press releases,
    media advisories, and communications materials for international media
    distribution.

    (f) Website Localization. Full localization of Client's websites and web
    applications, including user interface text, navigation elements, metadata,
    SEO content, and multimedia assets.

    (g) Social Media Content. Translation and adaptation of social media
    posts, captions, hashtags, and engagement materials for platforms
    including but not limited to Instagram, Twitter/X, Facebook, TikTok,
    YouTube, and LinkedIn.

3.2 Target Languages. The Service Provider shall provide translation and
localization services into the following fourteen (14) target languages:

    (a) Tier 1 Languages: Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Italian,
    Dutch, and Swedish.

    (b) Tier 2 Languages: Japanese, Korean, and Mandarin Chinese.

    (c) Tier 3 Languages: Arabic, Hindi, Thai, and Turkish.

3.3 Additional Languages. Either party may propose the addition of target
languages beyond the fourteen (14) languages specified in Section 3.2. Any
such addition shall require mutual written agreement and shall be documented
in an amendment to this Agreement or a supplemental Statement of Work,
including the applicable pricing tier classification for the added language.

4. TRANSLATION PROCESS

4.1 Source File Preparation. Upon receipt of Source Files from the Client,
the Service Provider shall perform initial file analysis including word count
verification, file format validation, reference material review, and
complexity assessment. The Service Provider shall notify the Client within
twenty-four (24) hours of receipt of any Source Files that are incomplete,
corrupted, or otherwise unsuitable for processing.

4.2 TEP Workflow. All translations performed under this Agreement shall
follow the standard TEP (Translation, Editing, Proofreading) workflow unless
otherwise specified in a project-specific instruction. The Service Provider
shall assign a minimum of three (3) qualified linguists to each translation
project, with no linguist performing more than one step of the TEP process
for the same project.

4.3 Machine Translation. The Service Provider may utilize machine translation
as part of the translation workflow, subject to the following conditions:
(a) MT shall not be used for creative content, marketing materials, or any
content classified as requiring transcreation without the Client's prior
written approval; (b) all MT output shall undergo human post-editing in
accordance with the quality standards specified for the applicable content
type; (c) the Service Provider shall disclose the use of MT in any project
summary or quality report upon the Client's request.

5. QUALITY ASSURANCE

5.1 LQA Scoring. The Service Provider shall implement a Linguistic Quality
Assurance (LQA) scoring framework based on the Multidimensional Quality
Metrics (MQM) model or an equivalent industry-standard methodology. LQA
scores shall be calculated for each delivered project on a scale of zero to
one hundred (0-100), with the following minimum thresholds:

    (a) Subtitles and Dubbing Scripts: Minimum LQA score of 95.
    (b) Marketing Materials and Transcreation: Minimum LQA score of 93.
    (c) Website Localization: Minimum LQA score of 92.
    (d) Social Media Content: Minimum LQA score of 90.
    (e) General Translation (press releases, corporate communications): 94.

5.2 Error Categorization. Errors identified during LQA review shall be
categorized by type (accuracy, fluency, terminology, style, formatting) and
severity (critical, major, minor, neutral). Critical errors — including
mistranslations that alter meaning, offensive content, regulatory
non-compliance, and factual errors — shall trigger mandatory rework at no
additional cost to the Client.

5.3 Sampling Rates. For projects exceeding fifty thousand (50,000) words,
the Service Provider shall perform LQA review on a random sample of not less
than ten percent (10%) of the delivered content, with the sampling methodology
designed to ensure representative coverage of all content types, linguists,
and language pairs within the project.

6. LINGUIST QUALIFICATIONS

6.1 Native Speaker Requirement. All translators, editors, and proofreaders
assigned to projects under this Agreement shall be native speakers of the
applicable target language, with professional-level proficiency in the source
language (English).

6.2 Domain Expertise. Linguists assigned to media content projects shall
demonstrate a minimum of three (3) years of professional experience in media
and entertainment localization, with verifiable credits or references for at
least two (2) projects of comparable scope and complexity.

6.3 Certifications. Linguists shall hold, at minimum, one of the following
qualifications: (a) a degree in translation, linguistics, or a related field
from an accredited institution; (b) certification from a recognized
professional body (e.g., ATA, ITI, CIOL); or (c) equivalent professional
credentials as approved by the Client.

7. TECHNOLOGY STACK

7.1 CAT Tools. The Service Provider shall utilize industry-standard CAT tools
for all translation projects, ensuring compatibility with the Client's
preferred file formats and workflow requirements. The Service Provider shall
maintain current licenses and subscriptions for all required CAT tools.

7.2 MT Engines. The Service Provider may deploy proprietary or third-party
machine translation engines as part of the translation workflow, subject to
the restrictions set forth in Section 4.3. The Service Provider shall not
use Client content to train or fine-tune any MT engine without the Client's
prior written consent.

7.3 TMS Integration. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with
access to a web-based Translation Management System (TMS) portal for project
submission, status tracking, delivery download, and quality report review.
The TMS shall generate automated notifications at key project milestones.

8. CONTENT TYPES AND HANDLING

8.1 Video Content. Source Files for video content shall be provided in
industry-standard formats including MP4, MOV, MXF, or ProRes, accompanied by
dialogue lists, time-coded scripts, or bilingual transcripts as available.
The Service Provider shall confirm receipt and technical suitability of video
Source Files within forty-eight (48) hours.

8.2 Audio Content. Voice-over and dubbing-related translations shall be
performed from audio Source Files provided in WAV, AIFF, or MP3 format, or
from transcripts prepared by the Client. The Service Provider shall flag any
audio quality issues that may impede accurate transcription or translation.

8.3 Print and Digital Marketing. Marketing materials shall be provided in
editable source formats (InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop, Figma, or
equivalent) to enable accurate text replacement and layout preservation. The
Service Provider shall not be responsible for design modifications beyond
text replacement unless specifically instructed.

8.4 Social Media. Social media content translations shall account for
platform-specific character limits, hashtag conventions, emoji usage, and
cultural sensitivities. The Service Provider shall propose culturally
appropriate alternatives when direct translation of social media content would
be ineffective or inappropriate in the target market.

9. CULTURAL ADAPTATION

9.1 Transcreation. For marketing materials, advertising copy, and creative
content, the Service Provider shall provide transcreation services that go
beyond literal translation to preserve the emotional impact, brand voice, and
cultural relevance of the original content. Transcreation deliverables may
include alternative messaging concepts, adapted taglines, and culturally
localized imagery recommendations.

9.2 Sensitivity Review. The Service Provider shall conduct cultural
sensitivity reviews for all content destined for markets with known cultural,
religious, political, or regulatory sensitivities. The sensitivity review
shall identify potentially offensive, inappropriate, or non-compliant content
and recommend appropriate modifications prior to delivery.

10. PROJECT MANAGEMENT

10.1 Turnaround Times. Standard turnaround times shall be determined based on
content type, word count, and language tier, as specified in the applicable
project instructions or as mutually agreed. Unless otherwise agreed, the
Service Provider shall acknowledge receipt of all project requests within four
(4) business hours and provide an estimated delivery date within eight (8)
business hours.

10.2 Communication Protocols. The Service Provider shall designate a dedicated
Project Manager as the primary point of contact for the Client. The Project
Manager shall be available during the Client's business hours (9:00 AM to
6:00 PM Eastern Time, Monday through Friday) and shall respond to all
communications within two (2) business hours.

10.3 Status Reporting. The Service Provider shall provide weekly status
reports summarizing active projects, upcoming deadlines, resource allocation,
and any issues or risks requiring the Client's attention.

11. REVISION POLICY

11.1 Rework Limits. The Service Provider shall perform one (1) round of
rework at no additional cost for any delivered project that does not meet the
applicable LQA score threshold specified in Section 5.1, provided that the
Client's feedback is submitted within fifteen (15) business days of delivery
and is specific, actionable, and consistent with the agreed Style Guide.

11.2 Change Requests. Revisions arising from changes to the Source Files,
modifications to the Style Guide, or alterations to project scope or
requirements initiated by the Client after work has commenced shall be
treated as change requests and shall be subject to additional fees at the
applicable rates.

13. COMPENSATION

13.1 Per-Word Pricing — Tier 1 Languages. For translations into Tier 1
Languages (Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Italian, Dutch, and Swedish),
the Client shall pay the Service Provider a rate of twelve cents ($0.12) per
source word for standard TEP services. Tier 1 rates apply to content in
languages using Latin-based scripts with established localization industry
infrastructure and mature translation memory coverage.

13.2 Per-Word Pricing — Tier 2 Languages. For translations into Tier 2
Languages (Japanese, Korean, and Mandarin Chinese), the Client shall pay the
Service Provider a rate of eighteen cents ($0.18) per source word for standard
TEP services. Tier 2 rates reflect the additional complexity of CJK character
sets, the smaller pool of qualified media localization linguists, and the
heightened quality requirements for East Asian market content distribution.

13.3 Per-Word Pricing — Tier 3 Languages. For translations into Tier 3
Languages (Arabic, Hindi, Thai, and Turkish), the Client shall pay the
Service Provider a rate of fifteen cents ($0.15) per source word for standard
TEP services. Tier 3 rates account for script complexity (including
right-to-left rendering for Arabic), limited TM leverage due to morphological
richness, and the specialized expertise required for accurate localization.

13.4 Rush Surcharge. For any Rush Project (as defined in Section 2.17), the
Service Provider shall apply a surcharge of fifty percent (50%) to the
applicable per-word rate for the relevant language tier. The Service Provider
shall confirm its capacity to accept a Rush Project within two (2) business
hours of receiving the request.

13.5 Minimum Monthly Engagement. The Client agrees to a minimum monthly
engagement of eight thousand dollars ($8,000.00). In any calendar month during
which the total fees for services rendered are less than the minimum monthly
engagement, the Client shall pay the difference between the actual fees and
the minimum monthly engagement amount. The minimum monthly engagement shall
apply from the first full calendar month following the Effective Date.

13.6 Payment Terms. Invoices shall be submitted monthly and shall be payable
by the Client within forty-five (45) calendar days of receipt. Invoices shall
include a detailed breakdown of services rendered by content type, language,
and word count.

13.7 Transcreation and Creative Services. Rates for transcreation and creative
adaptation services shall be quoted on a per-project basis and documented in
the applicable project instructions or Statement of Work.

Agreement, the Service Provider shall deliver monthly volume reports summarizing

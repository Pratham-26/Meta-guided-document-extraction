MARKETING SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Marketing Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of September 15, 2024
(reference number MSA-2024-7710), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the outdoor retail industry has experienced significant transformation over the past
decade, driven by the convergence of e-commerce proliferation, shifting consumer preferences
toward experiential outdoor lifestyles, and the accelerating digitalization of retail marketing
channels. The global outdoor gear market, valued at over sixty billion dollars annually, continues
to see sustained growth as consumers increasingly prioritize health, wellness, and outdoor
recreation activities ranging from casual day hiking to technical mountaineering expeditions;

WHEREAS, digital transformation within the outdoor retail sector has fundamentally altered how
consumers discover, evaluate, and purchase outdoor equipment and apparel. Social media platforms,
influencer partnerships, content-driven storytelling, and data-driven advertising have replaced
traditional print catalogs and trade show marketing as the primary channels through which outdoor
brands engage with their target demographics. Brands that fail to establish a compelling and
coherent digital presence risk losing market share to more digitally nimble competitors;

WHEREAS, effective brand positioning in the outdoor industry requires a nuanced understanding of
the values, aspirations, and purchasing behaviors of outdoor enthusiasts. These consumers tend
to be discerning, brand-loyal, and influenced by authenticity narratives, user-generated content,
and community engagement rather than conventional advertising approaches. The intersection of
performance-driven product messaging and aspirational lifestyle storytelling represents a critical
success factor for outdoor brands seeking to differentiate themselves in an increasingly crowded
marketplace;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to engage in a strategic marketing partnership that leverages modern
digital marketing methodologies, data analytics, and creative content production to enhance the
Client's market position, drive measurable business outcomes, and build long-term brand equity
within the outdoor recreation community;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set forth
herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are
hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1. PARTIES

1.1 Service Provider. Velocity Creative Agency, LLC, a limited liability company duly organized
and existing under the laws of the State of Colorado, with its principal offices located at
1600 Blake Street, Suite 400, Denver, CO 80202 (hereinafter referred to as "Agency" or
"Service Provider").

1.2 Client. Peak Outdoor Gear Co., a corporation duly organized and existing under the laws
of the State of Colorado, with its principal place of business at 890 Mountain View Road,
Boulder, CO 80301 (hereinafter referred to as "Brand," "Client," or "Company").

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents that they have the authority to bind
their respective organization to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

2. DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

2.1 "Approved Messaging" means all copy, taglines, value propositions, and textual content
that have been reviewed and explicitly approved in writing by Brand for use in marketing
materials, social media posts, advertisements, email campaigns, and any other public-facing
communications produced under this Agreement.

2.2 "Brand Guidelines" means the comprehensive set of rules, standards, and specifications
governing the visual and verbal identity of Brand, including but not limited to logo usage,
color palettes, typography, imagery style, tone of voice, and messaging frameworks, as set
forth in Exhibit A attached hereto and incorporated by reference.

2.3 "Campaign Calendar" means the strategic schedule of planned marketing campaigns,
initiatives, and activations developed by Agency in coordination with Brand, detailing timing,
channels, objectives, budgets, and responsible parties for each campaign element.

2.4 "Content Calendar" means the operational editorial schedule specifying individual content
pieces, publication dates, distribution channels, content types, assigned creators, and
approval status for all marketing content produced under this Agreement.

2.5 "Conversion Rate" means the percentage of users who complete a desired action (such as
a purchase, form submission, or account creation) out of the total number of visitors or
impressions for a given marketing asset, campaign, or channel during a specified period.

2.6 "CTR" (Click-Through Rate) means the ratio of users who click on a specific link,
advertisement, or call-to-action to the total number of users who view that link, ad, or
call-to-action, expressed as a percentage.

2.7 "Engagement Rate" means a composite metric measuring the level of interaction that
content receives from its audience, calculated as the sum of likes, comments, shares, saves,
and other meaningful interactions divided by total impressions or reach, expressed as a
percentage.

2.8 "Impression" means a single instance of a marketing asset or advertisement being displayed
on a user's screen, regardless of whether the user interacted with or even noticed the asset.

2.9 "KPI" (Key Performance Indicator) means a quantifiable metric used to evaluate the success
of marketing activities against predetermined targets, as specified in Section 8 of this
Agreement.

2.10 "Media Buy" means the purchase of advertising space or placement across digital channels,
including but not limited to search engines, social media platforms, display networks, and
programmatic advertising exchanges.

2.11 "ROI" (Return on Investment) means a performance metric calculated as the net profit
generated by marketing activities divided by the total cost of those activities, expressed
as a percentage or ratio.

2.12 "SEO" (Search Engine Optimization) means the practice of optimizing website content,
technical structure, and off-site signals to improve organic visibility and ranking in search
engine results pages for targeted keywords and search queries.

2.13 "Social Media Platform" means any internet-based service that enables users to create,
share, and interact with content, including but not limited to Instagram, TikTok, YouTube,
Facebook, Pinterest, LinkedIn, X (formerly Twitter), and any successor or similar platforms.

2.14 "Target Audience" means the specific demographic, psychographic, and behavioral segments
identified by Brand and Agency as the primary recipients of marketing communications and
campaigns, as further described in Exhibit B attached hereto.

2.15 "Work Product" means all materials, content, strategies, reports, data analyses,
campaigns, and deliverables created by Agency in the course of performing services under
this Agreement, whether individually or jointly authored.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 Overview. Agency shall provide comprehensive digital marketing services including social
media management, content creation, email marketing campaigns, SEO optimization, and paid
digital advertising for Brand's outdoor equipment product lines. Services shall be performed
in accordance with the standards, timelines, and specifications set forth in this Agreement
and any applicable exhibits, statements of work, or written directives from Brand.

3.2 Social Media Management. Agency shall manage Brand's presence across designated Social
Media Platforms, including but not limited to content posting, community engagement, comment
moderation, direct message management, story creation, and hashtag strategy. Agency shall
develop platform-specific content strategies tailored to the unique audience demographics,
content formats, and algorithmic preferences of each platform, as further described in
Section 6.

3.3 Content Creation. Agency shall produce original marketing content including but not
limited to blog articles, social media posts and captions, video scripts, email copy,
landing page copy, ad copy, infographic content, product descriptions, and long-form
editorial content. All content shall comply with Brand Guidelines and be subject to the
approval process described in Section 5.

3.4 Email Marketing Campaigns. Agency shall design, write, build, test, deploy, and analyze
email marketing campaigns on behalf of Brand, including promotional campaigns, newsletters,
product launches, abandoned cart sequences, welcome series, re-engagement campaigns, and
transactional email optimization. Email marketing activities shall comply with all applicable
laws and regulations, including the CAN-SPAM Act and the General Data Protection Regulation
(GDPR), as applicable.

3.5 SEO Optimization. Agency shall perform ongoing search engine optimization activities
including keyword research and strategy, on-page optimization (meta titles, meta descriptions,
header tags, internal linking, image alt text), technical SEO audits and recommendations,
content optimization, local SEO management, backlink strategy development, and competitive
analysis. Agency shall provide monthly SEO performance reports and quarterly strategy
adjustments based on ranking data and algorithm updates.

3.6 Paid Digital Advertising. Agency shall plan, create, manage, and optimize paid advertising
campaigns across digital channels including search engine marketing (SEM), social media
advertising, display advertising, retargeting campaigns, and programmatic media buys.
Advertising activities shall be governed by the budget parameters set forth in Section 9
and the media buying standards described in Section 7.

4. BRAND GUIDELINES AND COMPLIANCE

4.1 Tone of Voice. All content produced by Agency under this Agreement shall adhere to Brand's
established tone of voice, which conveys authenticity, expertise, adventure, and
environmental stewardship. Content should inspire confidence in Brand's products while
maintaining an approachable, community-oriented voice that resonates with outdoor enthusiasts
of varying experience levels. Slang, overly promotional language, and corporate jargon should
be avoided unless specifically approved in writing by Brand.

4.2 Visual Identity. All visual assets produced by Agency shall conform to Brand's visual
identity standards, including the approved color palette, typography specifications, logo
usage rules, photography style guidelines, and video production standards as detailed in the
Brand Style Guide (Exhibit A). No visual asset shall be published or distributed without
first receiving written approval from Brand's designated brand manager or marketing director.

4.3 Approved Messaging. Agency shall not publish, distribute, or authorize the publication of
any content that makes claims about Brand's products, sustainability practices, corporate
initiatives, or competitive positioning that have not been pre-approved in writing by Brand.
Agency shall maintain a current library of Approved Messaging for reference across all team
members working on Brand's account.

4.4 Regulatory Compliance. Agency shall ensure that all marketing materials comply with
applicable advertising laws, regulations, and industry self-regulatory standards, including
but not limited to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) guidelines on endorsements and
testimonials, the CAN-SPAM Act, platform-specific advertising policies, and any
industry-specific regulations applicable to outdoor equipment marketing.

5. CAMPAIGN PLANNING AND APPROVAL

5.1 Quarterly Planning Cycles. Agency and Brand shall engage in quarterly strategic planning
sessions to review performance data, assess market conditions, identify opportunities and
challenges, and develop campaign strategies for the upcoming quarter. These sessions shall
occur no later than fifteen (15) business days before the start of each calendar quarter and
shall involve senior representatives from both organizations.

5.2 Campaign Briefs. For each major campaign, Agency shall prepare a written campaign brief
that outlines the campaign objectives, target audience segments, key messages, creative
concepts, channel strategy, timeline, budget allocation, and proposed KPIs. Campaign briefs
shall be submitted to Brand for review and approval no later than twenty (20) business days
before the intended campaign launch date.

5.3 Approval Workflow. All creative assets, copy, and campaign materials shall be submitted
through Brand's designated approval workflow. Brand shall provide initial feedback within five
(5) business days of receiving materials. Agency shall incorporate feedback and resubmit
revised materials within three (3) business days. Brand shall render final approval or
rejection within three (3) business days of receiving revised materials.

5.4 Revision Limits. Each deliverable shall be entitled to up to two (2) rounds of revisions
at no additional cost. Any revisions beyond the second round shall be subject to additional
fees at Agency's standard hourly rate, which shall be communicated to Brand in writing prior
to commencement of such additional work.

6. CONTENT CREATION STANDARDS

6.1 Quality Requirements. All content produced by Agency shall be original, grammatically
correct, factually accurate, and free of spelling errors. Long-form content (blog articles,
white papers, case studies) shall be a minimum of one thousand (1,000) words unless otherwise
agreed. Video content shall meet minimum production quality standards as defined in the Brand
Style Guide.

6.2 Plagiarism and Originality. Agency warrants that all content delivered under this Agreement
shall be original work product and shall not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of
any third party. Agency shall not plagiarize, scrape, or substantially replicate content from
competitor websites, other brands, or third-party publications. Agency may use AI-assisted
tools in the content creation process provided that all final deliverables are reviewed, edited,
and approved by a human editor prior to submission to Brand.

6.3 Image and Media Licensing. Agency shall ensure that all stock images, graphics, music,
video clips, fonts, and other third-party media elements used in marketing materials are
properly licensed for commercial use. Agency shall maintain records of all licenses and shall
indemnify Brand against any claims arising from unlicensed use of third-party intellectual
property.

6.4 Content Archival. Agency shall maintain an organized archive of all content produced
under this Agreement, including source files, published versions, approval records, and
licensing documentation, for the duration of the Agreement and for two (2) years following
termination.

7. SOCIAL MEDIA MANAGEMENT

7.1 Platform-Specific Strategy. Agency shall develop and execute tailored strategies for each
Social Media Platform on which Brand maintains an active presence, recognizing that each
platform has distinct audience demographics, content format preferences, algorithmic
priorities, and engagement patterns.

7.2 Instagram. Agency shall manage Brand's Instagram presence including feed posts, Stories,
Reels, and Guides. Content strategy shall emphasize high-quality outdoor photography, short-
form video showcasing product use in real-world environments, user-generated content
features, behind-the-scenes brand stories, and interactive elements such as polls, quizzes,
and question stickers. Agency shall develop and implement a hashtag strategy optimized for
discoverability within the outdoor recreation community.

7.3 TikTok. Agency shall manage Brand's TikTok account with a focus on authentic, trend-aware
short-form video content that aligns with platform culture while reinforcing Brand identity.
Content shall include product demonstrations, educational content (e.g., gear selection tips,
outdoor safety), trending audio participation, duet and stitch collaborations with relevant
creators, and behind-the-scenes brand content. Agency shall monitor TikTok trends daily and
propose timely content opportunities to Brand.

7.4 YouTube. Agency shall manage Brand's YouTube channel, including long-form product review
videos, tutorial content, brand documentary-style content, and YouTube Shorts. Agency shall
optimize video titles, descriptions, tags, thumbnails, and end screens for discoverability
and engagement. Agency shall develop a YouTube content calendar aligned with seasonal outdoor
activity trends and product launch schedules.

7.5 Facebook. Agency shall manage Brand's Facebook presence including page posts, Facebook
Groups strategy, Facebook Live sessions, and Facebook Shops integration. Given the platform's
older demographic skew relative to other social channels, Agency shall tailor content
strategy to emphasize product reliability, value propositions, customer testimonials, and
community-building initiatives.

7.6 Pinterest. Agency shall manage Brand's Pinterest account with a focus on inspirational
and educational content that drives referral traffic to Brand's website. Content shall include
product pins, outdoor adventure inspiration boards, seasonal gift guides, how-to and
tutorial pins, and curated boards aligned with trending outdoor lifestyle topics. Agency
shall optimize pins for Pinterest's visual search functionality.

8. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS

8.1 Performance Targets. Agency shall use commercially reasonable efforts to achieve the
following key performance indicators within the first six (6) months of the initial term:

(a) Increase website organic traffic by thirty-five percent (35%) as measured against the
baseline organic traffic average for the six-month period preceding the Effective Date;

(b) Achieve social media follower growth of twenty percent (20%) across all managed
platforms, measured on a combined basis;

(c) Maintain email campaign open rate at or above twenty-two percent (22%);

(d) Reduce customer acquisition cost (CAC) by fifteen percent (15%) as measured against
the baseline CAC for the six-month period preceding the Effective Date.

8.2 Measurement Methodology. KPIs shall be measured using industry-standard analytics tools
including Google Analytics, platform-native analytics dashboards, and Agency's proprietary
reporting tools. Baseline measurements shall be established within the first thirty (30) days
of the initial term using data from the immediately preceding six-month period. Disputes
regarding KPI measurement methodology shall be resolved through good-faith negotiation
between the parties.

8.3 Quarterly Performance Reviews. Agency and Brand shall conduct formal quarterly performance
reviews to assess progress against KPI targets, identify areas requiring strategic adjustment,
and agree on corrective actions where necessary. Performance review meetings shall be
scheduled no later than fifteen (15) business days following the end of each calendar quarter.

9. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT TERMS

9.1 Monthly Retainer. Brand shall pay Agency a monthly retainer fee of eight thousand five
hundred dollars ($8,500.00) for the services described in Section 3. The retainer fee shall
be invoiced monthly in advance on the first business day of each calendar month during the
term of this Agreement.

9.2 Advertising Spend Budget. In addition to the monthly retainer, Brand shall provide Agency
with a monthly advertising spend budget of fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00) to be used
exclusively for paid digital advertising as described in Section 3.6. The advertising spend
budget shall be subject to quarterly review and adjustment by mutual written agreement of
the parties. Any unused advertising spend from a given month may be carried forward to the
subsequent month within the same calendar quarter but shall not carry forward beyond the
end of the calendar quarter.

9.3 Total Annual Value. The total annual value of this Agreement, excluding advertising
spend, is estimated at two hundred seventy-six thousand dollars ($276,000.00), based on the
monthly retainer fee multiplied by twelve (12) months. This estimate does not include costs
for additional services outside the scope of Section 3, expenses for third-party tools or
subscriptions, or any overages described in Section 11.

9.4 Payment Terms. All invoices shall be paid by Brand within twenty-one (21) days of the
invoice date. Payments shall be made by wire transfer to Agency's designated bank account or
by such other method as the parties may agree in writing. Late payments shall accrue interest
at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month, or the maximum rate permitted by
applicable law, whichever is less.

10. TERM AND RENEWAL

10.1 Initial Term. The initial term of this Agreement shall be twelve (12) months, commencing
on October 1, 2024 (the "Effective Date" for purposes of service delivery, notwithstanding
the date of execution set forth above).

10.2 Automatic Renewal. Upon expiration of the initial term, this Agreement shall automatically
renew for successive periods of six (6) months each, unless either party provides written
notice of non-renewal to the other party at least sixty (60) days prior to the expiration of
the then-current term.

10.3 Effect of Non-Renewal. Non-renewal of this Agreement shall not affect any obligations
of either party that accrued prior to the effective date of non-renewal, including without
limitation payment obligations, confidentiality obligations, intellectual property
obligations, and indemnification obligations.

11. BUDGET MANAGEMENT

11.1 Spend Tracking. Agency shall maintain detailed records of all advertising expenditures
made on behalf of Brand, including platform, campaign, date, amount, and performance data
associated with each expenditure. Agency shall provide Brand with monthly spend reports
detailing all advertising expenditures and reconciling actual spend against the approved
budget.

11.2 Overage Approval. In the event that advertising expenditures for any calendar month are
projected to exceed the approved monthly advertising spend budget by more than ten percent
(10%), Agency shall obtain Brand's prior written approval before incurring such excess
expenditures. Brand shall respond to overage approval requests within two (2) business days;
absent a response, the request shall be deemed denied.

11.3 Cost Efficiency. Agency shall use commercially reasonable efforts to optimize advertising
spend for maximum efficiency and performance. Agency shall not mark up media costs, platform
fees, or third-party vendor costs unless such markup is disclosed in writing and approved
by Brand in advance.

12. ADVERTISING AND MEDIA BUYING

12.1 Ad Platforms. Agency shall manage paid advertising campaigns across platforms including
but not limited to Google Ads (Search, Display, YouTube, Shopping), Meta Ads (Facebook,
Instagram), TikTok Ads, Pinterest Ads, and programmatic display networks. The selection of
advertising platforms for specific campaigns shall be based on strategic alignment with
campaign objectives, target audience demographics, and budget efficiency considerations.

12.2 Audience Targeting. Agency shall develop detailed audience targeting strategies for each
advertising campaign, utilizing demographic, geographic, psychographic, behavioral, and
contextual targeting parameters. Agency shall regularly refine targeting based on performance
data and audience insights. All audience targeting strategies shall be documented and made
available to Brand upon request.

12.3 A/B Testing. Agency shall implement systematic A/B testing protocols for advertising
campaigns, testing variables including but not limited to ad copy, creative assets, landing
pages, call-to-action elements, audience segments, bid strategies, and campaign settings.
Test results shall be documented and shared with Brand, and winning variations shall be
implemented to improve overall campaign performance.

13. EMAIL MARKETING

13.1 List Segmentation. Agency shall develop and maintain email list segmentation strategies
based on subscriber demographics, purchase history, engagement behavior, geographic location,
and other relevant data points. Segmentation strategies shall be reviewed and updated
quarterly to ensure relevance and effectiveness.

13.2 Compliance. All email marketing activities shall comply with the CAN-SPAM Act, GDPR
(where applicable to EU-based subscribers), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA),
and applicable platform-specific email delivery policies. Agency shall ensure that all
commercial emails include a clear and conspicuous unsubscribe mechanism, accurate header
information, and a valid physical mailing address for Brand.

13.3 Sending Frequency. Agency shall propose an email sending frequency schedule for Brand's
approval, taking into account audience engagement data, industry best practices, list health
metrics, and campaign objectives. Brand may adjust the approved sending frequency at any
time upon written notice to Agency.

14. INFLUENCER AND PARTNERSHIP MANAGEMENT

14.1 Influencer Vetting. Where Brand authorizes Agency to engage influencers or content
creators on Brand's behalf, Agency shall conduct reasonable due diligence on all prospective
influencer partners, including review of audience demographics, engagement authenticity,
content quality, brand alignment, and any history of controversial or problematic content.
Agency shall present vetted influencer recommendations to Brand for final approval prior
to engagement.

14.2 FTC Compliance. Agency shall ensure that all influencer partnerships and sponsored
content arrangements comply with the Federal Trade Commission's Endorsement Guides,
including proper disclosure of material connections between Brand and influencers. Agency
shall provide influencers with clear disclosure guidelines and shall monitor compliance.

14.3 Partnership Terms. The terms of any influencer or partnership engagement, including
compensation, content requirements, usage rights, exclusivity provisions, and termination
provisions, shall be documented in a separate agreement between Brand (or Agency on Brand's
behalf) and the influencer or partner. Agency shall not commit Brand to any partnership
without Brand's prior written approval.

15. REPORTING AND ANALYTICS

15.1 Monthly Performance Reports. Agency shall provide Brand with comprehensive monthly
performance reports within ten (10) business days following the end of each calendar month.
Monthly reports shall include, at a minimum: a summary of activities performed; performance
metrics for each active channel and campaign compared against KPI targets; spending
breakdown and reconciliation; top-performing content analysis; audience growth and
engagement trends; and recommended adjustments for the following month.

15.2 Quarterly Strategy Reports. In conjunction with the quarterly performance review
described in Section 8.3, Agency shall prepare a detailed quarterly strategy report that
includes comprehensive analysis of quarterly performance, competitive landscape assessment,
market trend analysis, and a strategic plan for the upcoming quarter.

15.3 Dashboard Access. Agency shall grant Brand access to Agency's proprietary reporting
dashboard for the duration of this Agreement. The dashboard shall provide real-time or
near-real-time visibility into key marketing metrics across all active channels. This
license to access the dashboard is non-exclusive, non-transferable, and limited to
authorized representatives of Brand.

15.4 Meeting Cadence. Agency and Brand shall hold regularly scheduled status meetings, the
frequency and format of which shall be agreed upon at the commencement of the initial term.
In no event shall the frequency of status meetings be less than one (1) meeting per month.
Additional ad hoc meetings may be scheduled as needed by mutual agreement.

16. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

16.1 Ownership of Work Product. Subject to Section 16.2, all content, creative assets,
campaigns, strategies, and marketing materials created specifically for Brand under this
Agreement shall become the exclusive property of Brand upon full payment for the services
giving rise to such Work Product. Agency hereby assigns to Brand all right, title, and
interest in and to such Work Product.

16.2 Agency Proprietary Materials. Agency's proprietary tools, software, analytics platforms,
methodologies, frameworks, templates, and process documentation shall remain the sole and
exclusive property of Agency. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as transferring
ownership of Agency's proprietary materials to Brand.

16.3 License Grant. Agency grants Brand a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-
free license to use Agency's proprietary reporting dashboard during the term of this
Agreement for the sole purpose of monitoring marketing performance under this Agreement.
This license shall terminate automatically upon expiration or termination of this Agreement.

16.4 Third-Party Rights. Agency shall not incorporate third-party intellectual property into
Work Product without first securing appropriate licenses or permissions for Brand's use of
such intellectual property in connection with Brand's marketing activities.

17. NON-SOLICITATION

17.1 Employee Non-Solicitation. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve
(12) months following the termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason, Brand
shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, or encourage any employee or
independent contractor of Agency to leave Agency's employ or engagement, or hire any such
person who has left Agency's employ or engagement within six (6) months prior to such
hiring.

17.2 Client Non-Solicitation. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve
(12) months following termination, Agency shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit,
recruit, or provide marketing services to any direct competitor of Brand in the outdoor
equipment retail sector without Brand's prior written consent.

18. CONFIDENTIALITY

18.1 Confidential Information. Each party acknowledges that in the course of performing its
obligations under this Agreement, it may receive or have access to confidential and
proprietary information of the other party, including but not limited to business plans,
financial data, customer lists, marketing strategies, product roadmaps, trade secrets, and
other sensitive business information (collectively, "Confidential Information").

18.2 Non-Disclosure. Each party agrees to hold all Confidential Information of the other
party in strict confidence and shall not disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal such
Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the
disclosing party, except as required by law or as necessary to perform its obligations
under this Agreement.

18.3 Survival. The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Section 18 shall survive the
expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

19. TERMINATION

19.1 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for any reason or
no reason upon forty-five (45) days' prior written notice to the other party.

19.2 Termination for Cause. Brand may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice
to Agency if Agency fails to meet the minimum KPI targets established in Section 8.1 for
two (2) consecutive calendar quarters, provided that Brand has given Agency thirty (30)
days' prior written notice of such failure and Agency has failed to cure the deficiency
within such thirty-day cure period.

19.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (a) Agency
shall promptly deliver to Brand all completed and partially completed Work Product for
which Brand has made payment; (b) Agency shall cease all use of Brand's trademarks, logos,
and other intellectual property; (c) Brand shall pay all outstanding invoices for services
duly rendered through the effective date of termination; and (d) the provisions of Sections
16, 17, 18, 20, and 22 through 30 shall survive termination.

IT STAFF AUGMENTATION SERVICES AGREEMENT

This IT Staff Augmentation Services Agreement (the "Agreement"), bearing
reference number ITA-2025-0455, is entered into as of April 1, 2025
(the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1
below.

WHEREAS, the global information technology industry has experienced
unprecedented demand for skilled technology professionals over the past
decade, driven by accelerating digital transformation initiatives, the
proliferation of cloud computing and artificial intelligence platforms,
increasing cybersecurity threats, and the growing complexity of enterprise
software ecosystems;

WHEREAS, the financial technology sector in particular has faced acute
challenges in recruiting and retaining qualified technology talent, with
industry surveys indicating that fintech companies experience average
time-to-fill periods exceeding ninety (90) days for senior software
engineering positions and annual voluntary turnover rates among technology
professionals approaching twenty-five percent (25%);

WHEREAS, the staff augmentation model has emerged as a proven and flexible
approach to addressing short-term and medium-term technology staffing needs,
enabling organizations to rapidly scale their development teams, access
specialized technical expertise not available internally, and manage workforce
capacity fluctuations without the long-term commitments and overhead costs
associated with direct hiring;

WHEREAS, Quantum Financial Technologies Inc., a leading provider of digital
banking and payment processing solutions headquartered in Stamford, Connecticut,
has identified a strategic need to supplement its existing technology
workforce with contracted IT professionals to support the development and
deployment of its next-generation financial services platform;

WHEREAS, TalentBridge IT Staffing Corp. is a nationally recognized provider of
IT staff augmentation services with demonstrated expertise in sourcing,
screening, and placing technology professionals across a broad range of
technical disciplines, including software engineering, cloud infrastructure,
DevOps, quality assurance, and project management, and has been selected by
the Client to provide contracted technology professionals;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration,
the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree
as follows:

1. PARTIES

1.1 Service Provider. TalentBridge IT Staffing Corp., a corporation duly
organized and existing under the laws of the State of New Jersey, with its
principal offices located at 100 Headquarters Plaza, Suite 600, Morristown, NJ
07960 (hereinafter referred to as "Service Provider," "TalentBridge," or
"Provider").

1.2 Client. Quantum Financial Technologies Inc., a corporation duly organized
and existing under the laws of the State of Connecticut, with its principal
offices located at 900 Stamford Place, Stamford, CT 06902 (hereinafter
referred to as "Client," "Company," or "Quantum").

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents and warrants that
they have the full legal authority to bind their respective organization to the
terms and conditions of this Agreement, and that the execution of this
Agreement has been duly authorized by all necessary corporate approvals.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Bill Rate" means the hourly rate charged by the Service Provider to the
Client for each Contractor's services, as set forth in the applicable Rate
Card attached hereto as Exhibit A.

2.2 "Bench Time" means any period during which a Contractor is engaged under
this Agreement but is not assigned to perform billable work for the Client
due to reasons other than the Contractor's own unavailability.

2.3 "Co-employment" means a situation in which both the Service Provider and
the Client may be perceived as joint employers of a Contractor, which the
parties expressly intend to avoid through the structural provisions of this
Agreement.

2.4 "Contractor" means any individual technology professional engaged by the
Service Provider to perform services for the Client under this Agreement.
Contractors are employees of the Service Provider and are not employees of
the Client.

2.5 "Mark-up" means the percentage or fixed amount added to a Contractor's
cost to the Service Provider to arrive at the Bill Rate charged to the Client.

2.6 "Off-boarding" means the process of transitioning a Contractor out of the
Client's work environment, including the return of equipment, revocation of
system access, completion of knowledge transfer, and final performance
documentation.

2.7 "On-boarding" means the process of integrating a newly engaged Contractor
into the Client's work environment, including orientation, equipment
provisioning, system access setup, and introduction to the assigned team.

2.8 "Requisition" means a formal request submitted by the Client to the
Service Provider describing a specific staffing need, including required
skills, experience level, start date, and estimated duration of engagement.

2.9 "Skill Matrix" means a documented inventory of the technical competencies,
certifications, and experience levels required for each role or position
specified in a Requisition.

2.10 "Statement of Work" or "SOW" means a document describing specific
services, deliverables, timelines, and resource requirements for a particular
engagement under this Agreement.

2.11 "Time and Materials" or "T&M" means a billing arrangement under which the
Client is billed based on the actual hours worked by each Contractor at the
applicable Bill Rate, plus any approved out-of-pocket expenses.

2.12 "Timesheet" means a document, whether electronic or paper, recording the
hours worked by each Contractor during a given billing period, to be approved
by an authorized representative of the Client.

2.13 "Utilization Rate" means the percentage of a Contractor's available
working hours that are spent performing billable work for the Client during
a given period.

2.14 "Work Week" means a period of seven (7) consecutive calendar days
beginning on Monday at 12:00 a.m. and ending on Sunday at 11:59 p.m.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide IT staff augmentation
services to the Client by supplying contracted technology professionals
(Contractors) to supplement and support the Client's existing software
development and technology operations teams. All Contractors shall perform
services on-site at the Client's principal offices located at 900 Stamford
Place, Stamford, CT 06902, under the day-to-day direction and supervision of
the Client's engineering management.

3.2 Initial Staffing. The initial engagement under this Agreement shall
include the following contracted positions:

    (a) Eight (8) Senior Software Engineers responsible for the design,
        development, and maintenance of the Client's core financial services
        platform, including backend microservices, API development, database
        design, and integration with third-party payment processors;

    (b) Four (4) DevOps Engineers responsible for the design, implementation,
        and maintenance of CI/CD pipelines, cloud infrastructure management,
        container orchestration, monitoring and alerting systems, and
        infrastructure-as-code deployments across the Client's multi-cloud
        environment;

    (c) Two (2) Quality Assurance Leads responsible for the development and
        execution of comprehensive test strategies, including automated
        testing frameworks, performance testing, security testing, and
        regression testing for the Client's financial services applications;

    (d) One (1) Project Manager responsible for coordinating the activities
        of the augmented team, managing project timelines and deliverables,
        facilitating communication between Contractors and Client personnel,
        and reporting project status to the Client's engineering leadership.

3.3 Role Adjustments. The specific roles, headcount, and skill requirements
of Contractors may be adjusted from time to time by mutual written agreement
of the parties. Either party may request a change to the staffing composition
by submitting a written Requisition to the other party. Any increase or
decrease in headcount shall require thirty (30) calendar days' prior written
notice and shall be subject to the availability of qualified candidates. Role
adjustments shall be reviewed on a quarterly basis.

3.4 Direction and Supervision. The Client shall provide day-to-day direction,
supervision, and task assignment to all Contractors engaged under this
Agreement. The Service Provider shall remain the employer of record for all
Contractors and shall be responsible for all employment-related obligations,
including payroll, benefits, workers' compensation, unemployment insurance,
and tax withholdings. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create
an employment relationship between the Client and any Contractor.

4. CONTRACTOR QUALIFICATIONS

4.1 Minimum Experience. All Contractors engaged under this Agreement shall
meet the minimum experience and qualification requirements specified in the
applicable Role Descriptions attached hereto as Exhibit B. In general,
Contractors shall possess a minimum of five (5) years of relevant professional
experience in their assigned discipline and a bachelor's degree in computer
science, information technology, or a related field from an accredited
institution.

4.2 Certifications. Where applicable, Contractors shall hold current and
valid professional certifications relevant to their assigned roles, including
but not limited to AWS Certified Solutions Architect, Microsoft Certified:
Azure Solutions Architect, Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA), ISTQB
Certified Tester, and Project Management Professional (PMP).

4.3 Background Checks. Prior to the commencement of any engagement, the
Service Provider shall conduct comprehensive background checks on all proposed
Contractors, including verification of employment history, educational
credentials, professional references, criminal history, and credit history
(where permissible by applicable law). The results of all background checks
shall be shared with the Client prior to the start of any engagement, and the
Client reserves the right to reject any Contractor based on the results of
such background checks.

4.4 Drug Screening. The Service Provider shall ensure that all Contractors
pass a pre-employment drug screening test in accordance with the Client's
substance abuse policy, a copy of which is attached hereto as Exhibit C. The
Service Provider shall also conduct random drug screening of Contractors at
the Client's request, at no additional cost to the Client.

5. RECRUITMENT PROCESS

5.1 Sourcing. Upon receipt of a Requisition from the Client, the Service
Provider shall utilize its internal recruitment databases, professional
networks, job boards, and other sourcing channels to identify qualified
candidates meeting the requirements specified in the Requisition and the
applicable Skill Matrix.

5.2 Screening. The Service Provider shall conduct initial screening of all
candidates, including review of resumes and cover letters, preliminary phone
interviews, skills assessments, and technical evaluations appropriate to the
candidate's discipline and experience level.

5.3 Technical Interviews. Candidates who successfully pass the initial
screening process shall be presented to the Client for technical interviews.
The Service Provider shall coordinate scheduling of technical interviews
between candidates and the Client's engineering team and shall provide the
Client with candidate profiles, assessment results, and availability
information prior to each interview.

5.4 Reference Checks. The Service Provider shall conduct a minimum of three
(3) professional reference checks for each candidate presented to the Client,
including verification of the candidate's technical skills, work quality,
communication abilities, and reliability.

5.5 Candidate Presentation Timeline. The Service Provider shall present
qualified candidates to the Client within fifteen (15) business days of
receipt of a Requisition. If the Service Provider is unable to present
qualified candidates within this timeframe, the Service Provider shall notify
the Client in writing and provide an estimated timeline for candidate
presentation.

6. ON-BOARDING AND OFF-BOARDING

6.1 On-boarding. Upon selection of a Contractor by the Client, the Service
Provider shall complete the on-boarding process within five (5) business days.
The on-boarding process shall include completion of all required employment
documentation, execution of confidentiality and intellectual property
assignment agreements, coordination of equipment provisioning with the Client,
setup of system and network access, and orientation to the Client's workplace
policies and project requirements.

6.2 Equipment. The Client shall provide all necessary hardware, software,
development tools, and workspace accommodations required by Contractors to
perform their assigned duties. The Service Provider shall be responsible for
providing any specialized tools, software licenses, or equipment that are
unique to the Service Provider's standard operating environment and not
otherwise available from the Client.

6.3 Off-boarding. Upon termination of a Contractor's engagement for any
reason, the Service Provider shall coordinate with the Client to complete the
off-boarding process within three (3) business days. The off-boarding process
shall include the return of all Client-provided equipment and access
credentials, completion of knowledge transfer documentation, submission of
final timesheets, and a debrief meeting between the Service Provider and the
Client to discuss the engagement and any lessons learned.

6.4 Knowledge Transfer. The Service Provider shall ensure that each Contractor
completes adequate knowledge transfer documentation prior to the conclusion of
their engagement, including documentation of work in progress, pending issues,
system configurations, and any other information necessary to ensure a smooth
transition of responsibilities.

7. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

7.1 Supervision. While performing services under this Agreement, each
Contractor shall operate under the day-to-day direction and supervision of
the Client's designated project leads and engineering managers. The Service
Provider shall assign a dedicated account manager to serve as the primary
point of contact between the Service Provider and the Client for all matters
relating to Contractor performance, engagement status, and administrative
issues.

7.2 Feedback. The Client shall provide regular feedback to the Service
Provider regarding the performance of each Contractor, including participation
in quarterly performance reviews. The Service Provider shall use such feedback
to ensure that Contractors continue to meet the Client's expectations and to
identify any performance issues requiring corrective action.

7.3 Performance Improvement. If the Client identifies performance deficiencies
in any Contractor, the Client shall notify the Service Provider in writing,
specifying the nature of the deficiencies and the expected improvements. The
Service Provider shall work with the Contractor to develop a performance
improvement plan and shall implement corrective measures within fifteen (15)
business days of receiving the Client's notice.

7.4 Replacement. If a Contractor fails to meet the performance standards
specified in the performance improvement plan, or if the Client determines
that a Contractor is unsuitable for any reason, the Service Provider shall
replace the affected Contractor with a qualified substitute within fifteen
(15) business days of receiving the Client's written request for replacement.
The Service Provider shall bear all costs associated with the replacement
process, including recruitment, screening, and on-boarding of the substitute
Contractor, and the Client shall not be charged for any bench time incurred
during the replacement period.

8. TIME TRACKING AND BILLING

8.1 Timesheet Submission. Each Contractor shall submit a weekly Timesheet
documenting the hours worked during the preceding Work Week. Timesheets shall
be submitted electronically through the Client's designated time tracking
system no later than the close of business on the Friday of each Work Week.

8.2 Timesheet Approval. An authorized representative of the Client shall
review and approve or reject each submitted Timesheet within five (5) business
days of submission. Approved Timesheets shall serve as the basis for invoicing.
Any disputed hours shall be documented in writing and resolved between the
parties within ten (10) business days.

8.3 Invoicing. The Service Provider shall submit monthly invoices to the
Client, aggregating all approved Timesheets for the preceding calendar month.
Invoices shall be submitted no later than the fifth (5th) day of each month
and shall include a detailed breakdown of hours worked by each Contractor,
the applicable Bill Rate, and the total amount due.

8.4 Minimum Billable Hours. Each Contractor shall be available to work a
minimum of forty (40) hours per Work Week. The Client shall be billed for a
minimum of forty (40) hours per Contractor per Work Week, regardless of
whether the full forty (40) hours are worked, except during periods of
Client-approved absence (vacation, holiday, illness) or bench time caused
solely by the Client's failure to provide work assignments.

8.5 Overtime. Any hours worked by a Contractor in excess of forty (40) hours
in a Work Week shall be billed at the applicable overtime rate of one and
one-half (1.5) times the Contractor's standard Bill Rate. Overtime work shall
require the prior written approval of the Client's authorized representative,
except in cases of emergency or critical production incidents requiring
immediate attention.

8.6 Rate Adjustments. The Bill Rates set forth in Exhibit A (Rate Card) shall
remain in effect for the initial twelve (12) months of the Term. Thereafter,
the Service Provider may propose annual rate adjustments, not to exceed five
percent (5%) per annum, by providing the Client with sixty (60) calendar days'
prior written notice. Any rate adjustment shall require the Client's written
consent before taking effect.

8.7 Audit Rights. The Client shall have the right to audit the Service
Provider's timekeeping records, payroll records, and other documentation
reasonably related to the billing of services under this Agreement, upon
thirty (30) calendar days' prior written notice. Such audits shall be
conducted during normal business hours and at the Client's expense, unless an
audit reveals a billing discrepancy of more than five percent (5%), in which
case the Service Provider shall bear the cost of the audit.

9. WORKPLACE POLICIES

9.1 Code of Conduct. All Contractors shall comply with the Client's code of
conduct and professional standards of behavior while performing services on
the Client's premises. The Service Provider shall ensure that all Contractors
are informed of and agree to comply with the Client's code of conduct prior
to the commencement of any engagement.

9.2 Working Hours. Contractors shall generally work during the Client's
standard business hours, which are 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. Eastern Time,
Monday through Friday, unless otherwise agreed upon by the parties. The
Client may authorize alternative work schedules on a case-by-case basis.

9.3 Holiday Schedule. Contractors shall observe the Client's designated
holiday schedule. The Client shall provide the Service Provider with the
annual holiday schedule no later than December 1 of each year for the
following calendar year. Contractors shall not be billed for time not worked
due to Client-designated holidays.

10. TERM

10.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and
shall continue for a period of twenty-four (24) months, concluding on March
31, 2027 (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with
Section 16.

10.2 Auto-Renewal. Upon expiration of the Initial Term and any subsequent
renewal term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve
(12) month periods (each a "Renewal Term"), unless either party provides the
other party with at least ninety (90) calendar days' prior written notice of
its intent not to renew.

10.3 Survival. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall not affect
any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination,
including without limitation Sections 12 (Intellectual Property and Invention
Assignment), 13 (Confidentiality), 14 (Anti-Poaching), 15 (Data Security),
17 (Liability), 16 (Termination), 18 (Insurance), 19 (Dispute Resolution),
and 21 (Governing Law).

11. COMPENSATION

11.1 Bill Rates. The Client shall pay the Service Provider for services
rendered by Contractors at the following rates:

    (a) Blended Rate: Ninety-five dollars ($95.00) per hour for standard
        contractor positions;

    (b) Senior Specialist Rate: One hundred twenty-five dollars ($125.00) per
        hour for Contractors classified as senior specialists with ten (10) or
        more years of relevant experience;

    (c) Project Manager Rate: One hundred thirty-five dollars ($135.00) per
        hour for Contractors engaged in project management roles.

11.2 Overtime Rate. Hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours per Work Week
shall be billed at one and one-half (1.5) times the applicable standard Bill
Rate.

11.3 Payment Terms. The Client shall pay all undisputed invoices within
forty-five (45) days of receipt of the invoice from the Service Provider.
Invoices shall be submitted by the Service Provider no later than the fifth
(5th) calendar day of each month for services rendered during the preceding
calendar month.

11.4 Late Payments. Invoices not paid within forty-five (45) days of receipt
shall be subject to interest at a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%)
per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less,
calculated from the due date until the date of actual payment.

12. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND INVENTION ASSIGNMENT

12.1 Client Ownership. All work product, inventions, discoveries, designs,
software code, documentation, technical specifications, and other intellectual
property created, conceived, or reduced to practice by any Contractor in the
course of performing services under this Agreement shall be the exclusive
property of the Client (the "Client Work Product"). Each Contractor shall be
required to execute an Invention Assignment Agreement in a form acceptable to
the Client, assigning to the Client all rights, title, and interest in and to
any Client Work Product.

12.2 Service Provider Property. The Service Provider shall retain all right,
title, and interest in its pre-existing intellectual property, including but
not limited to its recruitment databases, candidate assessment methodologies,
screening tools, training materials, and proprietary placement processes. The
Service Provider grants the Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable license
to use such pre-existing intellectual property solely in connection with the
services provided under this Agreement.

12.3 Moral Rights. To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, each
Contractor hereby waives all moral rights, rights of attribution, and rights
of integrity that may exist in any Client Work Product.

13. CONFIDENTIALITY

13.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all
Confidential Information of the other party disclosed in connection with this
Agreement. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, the Client's
business plans, financial data, customer information, software source code,
system architectures, security protocols, and proprietary algorithms, as well
as the Service Provider's recruitment methodologies, candidate databases,
pricing structures, and proprietary screening processes.

13.2 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section 13
shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of
five (5) years.

14. ANTI-POACHING

14.1 Restriction. During the Term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve
(12) months following the expiration or termination of this Agreement (the
"Restricted Period"), the Client shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit,
recruit, hire, or engage as an employee, independent contractor, or consultant
any individual who was engaged as a Contractor under this Agreement, or who
was presented to the Client as a candidate by the Service Provider during the
preceding six (6) months, without the prior written consent of the Service
Provider.

14.2 Buy-Out Provision. Notwithstanding the foregoing restriction, the Client
may hire a Contractor directly during the Restricted Period upon payment to
the Service Provider of a buy-out fee equal to twenty percent (20%) of the
Contractor's projected annual compensation (based on the applicable Bill Rate
times two thousand eighty (2,080) hours), payable within thirty (30) days of
the Contractor's start date as a Client employee.

15. DATA SECURITY

15.1 Security Requirements. The Service Provider shall ensure that all
Contractors comply with the Client's information security policies and
procedures, including requirements for password management, data encryption,
access control, and the handling of sensitive financial data. Each Contractor
shall execute the Client's standard Non-Disclosure Agreement prior to being
granted access to any Client systems or data.

15.2 Device Management. All devices used by Contractors to access Client
systems or data shall be managed in accordance with the Client's device
management policies, including installation of required security software,
remote wipe capabilities, and regular security updates.

16. TERMINATION

16.1 Individual Contractor Termination. Either party may terminate the
engagement of an individual Contractor upon thirty (30) calendar days' prior
written notice to the other party. The Service Provider shall be entitled to
bill for all hours worked and expenses incurred by the terminated Contractor
through the effective date of termination.

16.2 Full Agreement Termination. Either party may terminate this Agreement
in its entirety upon sixty (60) calendar days' prior written notice to the
other party. Upon termination of the full Agreement, the Service Provider
shall be entitled to payment for all services satisfactorily performed and
expenses incurred through the effective date of termination.

16.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement or the
engagement of any individual Contractor for cause if the other party commits
a material breach of any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such
breach within ten (10) calendar days after receiving written notice specifying
the nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

16.4 Payment Upon Termination. Upon termination of the full Agreement for any
reason, the Client shall pay all undisputed invoices for services rendered
through the effective date of termination within forty-five (45) days of such
effective date.

17. LIABILITY

17.1 Limitation of Liability. TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE
LAW, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT,
INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING
BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF DATA, BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, OR
LOSS OF OPPORTUNITY, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE
PERFORMANCE OF SERVICES HEREUNDER, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES ARE
BASED ON CONTRACT, TORT, STRICT LIABILITY, ANY OTHER LEGAL THEORY, AND EVEN
IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

17.2 Liability Cap. The Service Provider's total aggregate liability for all
claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement, whether in contract,
tort, or otherwise, shall not exceed an amount equal to three (3) months of
fees for the affected Contractor(s) that gave rise to the claim. For purposes
of calculating this cap, fees shall be calculated based on the applicable Bill
Rate multiplied by the minimum billable hours per week (40) multiplied by
thirteen (13) weeks, or such shorter period applicable to the affected
Contractor(s).

18. INSURANCE

18.1 Professional Liability. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the
Service Provider shall maintain professional liability (errors and omissions)
insurance in an amount not less than one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) per
claim, covering claims arising from negligent acts, errors, or omissions in
the provision of staffing services under this Agreement.

18.2 General Liability. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the Service
Provider shall maintain commercial general liability insurance in an amount not
less than one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) per occurrence, covering bodily
injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury arising from the
Service Provider's operations in connection with this Agreement.

18.3 Workers' Compensation. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the
Service Provider shall maintain workers' compensation insurance in accordance
with the requirements of the State of New Jersey and the State of Connecticut,
with employer's liability coverage of not less than five hundred thousand
dollars ($500,000.00).

18.4 Evidence of Coverage. The Service Provider shall provide certificates of
insurance evidencing the required coverage to the Client upon execution of this
Agreement and upon each renewal of such policies.

19. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

19.1 Negotiation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to
this Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve such dispute
through good faith negotiations between senior executives of each party for a
period of not less than thirty (30) calendar days.

19.2 Arbitration. If the parties are unable to resolve a dispute through
negotiation, such dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration in
Stamford, Connecticut, in accordance with the commercial arbitration rules of
the American Arbitration Association (AAA) then in effect. The decision of
the arbitrator shall be final and binding, and judgment upon the award may be
entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

20. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of
its obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such failure or delay
is caused by circumstances beyond the reasonable control of the affected
party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics,
government actions, labor disputes, civil unrest, war, terrorism, power or
telecommunications failures, and public health emergencies. The affected party
shall notify the other party promptly in writing of the force majeure event
and its expected duration, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to
mitigate its impact on the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws
of the State of Connecticut, without giving effect to any choice-of-law or
conflict-of-law provisions that would require the application of the laws of

23. GENERAL PROVISIONS

23.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, including all exhibits attached hereto,
constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the
subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements,
understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written, between
the parties relating to such subject matter.

23.2 Amendments. This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented
except by a written instrument signed by both parties.

23.3 Assignment. Neither party may assign or transfer any of its rights or
obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other
party, except that either party may assign this Agreement to a successor entity
in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all
of its assets.

23.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid,
illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision
shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and
enforceable, and the remaining provisions of this Agreement shall continue in
full force and effect.

23.5 Waiver. The failure of either party to enforce any provision of this
Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such party's right to enforce such
provision or any other provision in the future.

23.6 Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be
in writing and shall be deemed delivered when (a) delivered personally, (b)
sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, (c) sent by nationally
recognized overnight courier, or (d) sent by email with confirmation of
receipt, to the addresses set forth in Section 1 or such other address as
either party may designate in writing.

23.7 Independent Contractor. The Service Provider is an independent contractor
and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Client. Nothing
in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship,
partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties. The
parties expressly acknowledge that the Service Provider is the sole employer
of all Contractors and that the Client's direction and supervision of
Contractors does not create an employment or co-employment relationship
between the Client and any Contractor.

23.8 Non-Exclusivity. This Agreement does not grant the Service Provider an
exclusive right to provide staffing services to the Client. The Client may
engage other staffing providers or hire employees directly to perform services
similar to those provided under this Agreement, subject to the anti-poaching
restrictions set forth in Section 14.

LOGISTICS AND WAREHOUSE MANAGEMENT SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Logistics and Warehouse Management Services Agreement (the "Agreement"), bearing
reference number LOG-2024-3301, is entered into as of June 1, 2024 (the "Effective Date"),
by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the home furniture manufacturing industry has undergone a profound transformation
in recent years, driven by the rapid expansion of e-commerce furniture sales, shifting consumer
preferences toward direct-to-consumer purchasing models, and the increasing complexity of
last-mile delivery logistics for oversized and fragile merchandise. The United States home
furniture market, estimated at over one hundred twenty billion dollars in annual revenue,
continues to evolve as manufacturers and retailers alike seek to optimize their supply chain
operations, reduce warehousing costs, and improve order fulfillment speed and accuracy to meet
rising consumer expectations for rapid delivery and hassle-free returns;

WHEREAS, the proliferation of online furniture sales has introduced significant logistical
challenges that traditional brick-and-mortar distribution networks were not designed to address.
Furniture items present unique warehousing and shipping requirements due to their variable
dimensions, weight ranges, susceptibility to damage during handling and transit, and the
consumer expectation of white-glove delivery options. The transition from regional distribution
to national fulfillment operations has compelled furniture manufacturers to reevaluate their
approach to inventory management, warehouse operations, and carrier relationships;

WHEREAS, supply chain disruptions experienced in recent years, including port congestion,
container shortages, raw material scarcity, and labor market volatility, have underscored the
critical importance of resilient and adaptable warehouse management systems. Manufacturers
who have invested in modern warehouse management technology, including real-time inventory
tracking, automated picking systems, and integrated shipping coordination platforms, have
demonstrated significantly greater operational flexibility and customer satisfaction compared
to those relying on legacy warehouse management approaches;

WHEREAS, the seasonal nature of furniture demand, characterized by pronounced peaks during
spring and fall home improvement seasons, holiday promotional periods, and year-end clearance
events, requires warehouse operations capable of scaling capacity rapidly in response to
fluctuating order volumes. The ability to accommodate seasonal surges without compromising
order accuracy or delivery timelines represents a critical competitive advantage in the
furniture retail landscape;

WHEREAS, the growing importance of reverse logistics in furniture e-commerce, including
product returns, exchanges, damage claims, and refurbishment processing, has created demand
for warehouse management partners capable of efficiently managing the full product lifecycle
from inbound receiving through final-mile delivery and, when necessary, return-to-vendor or
refurbishment workflows;

WHEREAS, regulatory requirements governing warehouse operations, including Occupational Safety
and Health Administration (OSHA) standards for material handling, fire protection codes
established by the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA), and environmental regulations
pertaining to packaging waste and hazardous material storage, impose substantial compliance
obligations on warehouse operators that require specialized expertise and dedicated compliance
management resources;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to enter into a comprehensive warehouse management arrangement
that addresses the foregoing considerations and establishes a framework for the efficient,
compliant, and scalable management of the Client's furniture inventory and fulfillment
operations;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set forth
herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which
are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

2.10 "Inventory Snapshot" means a point-in-time record of all Client Products stored at the

2.12 "Monthly Service Fee" means the fixed monthly retainer payment described in Section 4.1.

2.15 "Returns Merchandise Authorization" or "RMA" means the authorization issued by the
Client or Service Provider for the return of Client Products from the end customer.

2.16 "Service Level Agreement" or "SLA" means the performance standards and metrics set

2.17 "Shipping Volume" means the total number of Client Product units shipped from the

2.18 "Surcharge" means the additional per-unit fee applicable when Shipping Volume exceeds

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Services. The Service Provider shall provide end-to-end warehouse management
services for the Client's home furniture products, including without limitation the following
core operational functions:

    (a) Receiving. The Service Provider shall receive inbound shipments of Client Products
    at the Facility, including unloading of delivery vehicles, inspection of inbound
    merchandise for quantity verification and damage assessment, documentation of
    discrepancies, and proper staging of received goods for putaway.

    (b) Inventory Management. The Service Provider shall manage all aspects of Client
    Product inventory at the Facility, including putaway to designated storage locations,
    cycle counting, physical inventory audits, inventory reconciliation, lot and serial
    number tracking, FIFO (first-in-first-out) rotation management, and real-time
    inventory visibility through the WMS platform.

    (c) Pick-and-Pack Operations. The Service Provider shall fulfill Client orders through
    a systematic pick-and-pack process, including order receipt and processing, wave
    planning and batch optimization, picking from designated storage locations, quality
    inspection of picked items, appropriate packaging selection based on product type and
    shipping method, label generation, and staging of packed orders for carrier pickup.

    (d) Shipping Coordination. The Service Provider shall coordinate all outbound shipments
    of Client Products from the Facility, including carrier scheduling, bill of lading
    preparation, freight classification, shipment manifesting, tracking number generation
    and communication, and proof of delivery confirmation.

    (e) Returns Processing. The Service Provider shall manage the receipt, inspection,
    grading, and disposition of returned Client Products, including RMA verification,
    damage assessment, restocking of sellable returns, segregation of damaged or
    unsellable merchandise, and disposition reporting to the Client.

    (f) Real-Time Inventory Tracking. The Service Provider shall maintain continuous
    real-time visibility of all Client Products stored at the Facility through the WMS
    platform, providing the Client with access to inventory data including on-hand
    quantities, allocated quantities, available-to-promise quantities, in-transit
    quantities, and location-level inventory detail.

3.2 Additional Services. The Service Provider may, upon written request from the Client
and subject to mutually agreed terms and additional compensation, provide supplementary
services including but not limited to kitting and assembly services, value-added services
such as furniture assembly prior to shipment, gift wrapping, promotional insert placement,
and custom labeling.

4. COMPENSATION

4.1 Monthly Retainer. In consideration of the services provided under this Agreement, the
Client shall pay the Service Provider a fixed monthly retainer fee in the amount of
eighteen thousand seven hundred fifty dollars ($18,750.00) per month (the "Monthly Service
Fee"). The Monthly Service Fee shall be payable in arrears on or before the thirtieth (30th)
calendar day following each month of service. For the avoidance of doubt, payments are due
within thirty (30) calendar days of invoice receipt.

4.2 Per-Unit Shipping Fee. In addition to the Monthly Service Fee, the Client shall pay
the Service Provider a per-unit shipping fee of two dollars and fifty cents ($2.50) for
each Client Product unit shipped from the Facility (the "Per-Unit Fee"). The Per-Unit Fee
covers all picking, packing, and shipping coordination costs associated with each unit.

4.3 Monthly Volume Cap and Surcharge. The Per-Unit Fee structure set forth in Section 4.2
shall apply to the first ten thousand (10,000) Client Product units shipped during each
calendar month (the "Monthly Volume Cap"). For any Shipping Volume exceeding the Monthly
Volume Cap in a given calendar month, the Client shall pay a surcharge of one dollar and
twenty-five cents ($1.25) per additional unit in excess of the Monthly Volume Cap. The
Service Provider shall notify the Client in writing when Shipping Volume is projected to
exceed eighty percent (80%) of the Monthly Volume Cap to allow the Client an opportunity
to adjust order release schedules.

4.4 Invoicing. The Service Provider shall submit monthly invoices to the Client no later
than the fifth (5th) Working Day following the end of each calendar month. Each invoice
shall itemize the Monthly Service Fee, the number of units shipped, the Per-Unit Fee
amount, any applicable Surcharges, and any additional charges for supplementary services
rendered during the invoiced period.

4.5 Taxes. All fees payable under this Agreement are exclusive of applicable taxes. The
Client shall be responsible for any sales tax, use tax, or similar taxes imposed on the
services provided under this Agreement, except for taxes based on the Service Provider's
net income.

5. DELIVERABLES AND MILESTONES

5.1 Warehouse Setup. The Service Provider shall complete the physical setup and configuration
of the Facility for Client Product storage and fulfillment operations within thirty (30)
calendar days of the Effective Date. Warehouse setup shall include installation of storage
racking systems, designation of receiving, staging, and shipping areas, configuration of
safety equipment, and completion of initial staff training on Client-specific handling
requirements.

5.2 WMS Integration. The Service Provider shall complete the integration of the WMS platform
with the Client's order management and inventory systems within sixty (60) calendar days of
the Effective Date. WMS integration shall include electronic data interchange (EDI)
connectivity, real-time inventory synchronization, automated order import functionality,
and shipping label and tracking data transmission.

5.3 First Audit Report. The Service Provider shall deliver the first comprehensive operational
audit report to the Client within ninety (90) calendar days of the Effective Date. The audit
report shall cover inventory accuracy, damage rates, fulfillment accuracy, order processing
times, and compliance with operational procedures established under this Agreement.

5.4 Quarterly Performance Reviews. Commencing at the end of the first full quarter of
operations, the Service Provider shall conduct quarterly performance reviews in accordance
with Section 9.3. These reviews shall be ongoing throughout the term of this Agreement and
shall include a written performance summary delivered to the Client within fifteen (15)
Working Days following the end of each calendar quarter.

6. FACILITY REQUIREMENTS

6.1 General Standards. The Facility utilized by the Service Provider for the performance of
services under this Agreement shall meet or exceed the following minimum requirements:

    (a) Minimum clear height of twenty-four (24) feet to accommodate high-bay storage
    racking systems suitable for furniture storage.

    (b) Sprinkler system installed and maintained in accordance with NFPA 13 standards
    for storage occupancies, including ESFR (Early Suppression Fast Response) sprinkler
    heads where required by local fire code.

    (c) Climate control systems capable of maintaining interior temperature between
    fifty-five (55) and eighty-five (85) degrees Fahrenheit and relative humidity
    between thirty percent (30%) and sixty percent (60%) to protect furniture finishes
    and materials from environmental damage.

    (d) Sealed concrete flooring with load-bearing capacity sufficient for fully loaded
    storage racks and material handling equipment operation.

    (e) Adequate dock doors equipped with dock levelers, dock seals, and restraint systems
    to accommodate simultaneous loading and unloading of multiple delivery vehicles.

    (f) Secure perimeter fencing, controlled access points, and a comprehensive video
    surveillance system covering all interior and exterior areas of the Facility.

6.2 Rack Specifications. All storage racking systems installed at the Facility shall be
designed and installed in compliance with the Rack Manufacturers Institute (RMI) standards
and shall be rated for the specific weight loads of Client Products to be stored. Rack
configurations shall be documented in the Facility Layout Plan attached hereto as Exhibit A.

6.3 Maintenance. The Service Provider shall maintain the Facility, including all building
systems, material handling equipment, racking, and safety equipment, in good working order
and in compliance with all applicable building codes, fire codes, and safety regulations.

7. HEALTH AND SAFETY

7.1 OSHA Compliance. The Service Provider shall comply with all applicable provisions of the
Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970 and the regulations promulgated thereunder,
including 29 C.F.R. Part 1910 (General Industry Standards). The Service Provider shall
maintain an effective workplace safety program that addresses hazard identification,
incident reporting, near-miss documentation, safety training, and continuous improvement
of safety performance.

7.2 Personal Protective Equipment. The Service Provider shall provide and require the use
of appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) by all personnel performing work at the
Facility, including but not limited to steel-toed footwear, high-visibility vests, gloves,
and eye protection where required by the nature of the work being performed.

7.3 Forklift and Material Handling Equipment. All forklift operators and material handling
equipment operators at the Facility shall be trained and certified in accordance with OSHA
requirements. The Service Provider shall maintain training records and certification
documentation for all equipment operators and shall make such records available for
inspection by the Client upon reasonable request.

7.4 Incident Reporting. The Service Provider shall report to the Client within twenty-four
(24) hours any workplace incident involving Client Products that results in personal injury,
significant property damage, or regulatory agency notification.

8. TECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION

8.1 Warehouse Management System (WMS). The Service Provider shall provide and maintain a
WMS platform for the management of Client Product inventory and fulfillment operations at
the Facility. The WMS shall provide real-time inventory visibility, automated task
direction for receiving, putaway, picking, and packing operations, and comprehensive
reporting and analytics capabilities. The technical specifications of the WMS platform
are set forth in Exhibit B attached hereto.

8.2 Barcode and Scanning Technology. The Service Provider shall utilize barcode scanning
technology throughout all warehouse operations to ensure accurate identification and
tracking of Client Products. All inbound shipments, inventory movements, and outbound
shipments shall be scanned and recorded in the WMS in real time.

8.3 RFID Capability. Where Client Products are equipped with RFID tags, the Service Provider
shall utilize RFID scanning technology to supplement barcode scanning for inventory
tracking purposes. The Service Provider shall maintain RFID readers at key operational
points including receiving docks, storage zone entry points, packing stations, and
shipping docks.

8.4 Data Security. The Service Provider shall implement and maintain reasonable technical
and organizational measures to protect the security and integrity of all data processed
through the WMS and related systems, including encryption of data in transit and at rest,
access controls, audit logging, and regular security assessments.

9. QUALITY CONTROL

9.1 Inspection Procedures. The Service Provider shall implement standardized inspection
procedures for all Client Products at each stage of warehouse operations, including
inbound receiving inspection, storage condition monitoring, pre-shipping inspection, and
return merchandise inspection.

9.2 Damage Reporting. The Service Provider shall maintain a comprehensive damage reporting
system that documents all instances of damage to Client Products, including photographic
evidence, damage classification, root cause analysis, and corrective action documentation.
Damage reports shall be submitted to the Client within forty-eight (48) hours of discovery.

9.3 Performance Metrics. The Service Provider shall be measured against the following key
performance indicators:

    (a) Inventory Accuracy: minimum ninety-eight percent (98%) at SKU level.
    (b) Fulfillment Accuracy: minimum ninety-nine percent (99%).
    (c) Order Processing Time: maximum twenty-four (24) hours from order receipt to
    carrier pickup.
    (d) Damage Rate: maximum zero point five percent (0.5%).
    (e) On-Time Shipment: minimum ninety-seven percent (97%).

10. TRANSPORTATION AND SHIPPING

10.1 Carrier Selection. The Service Provider shall maintain relationships with multiple
licensed and insured carriers for the transportation of Client Products. Carrier selection
shall be based on service quality, transit time reliability, claims performance, geographic
coverage, and cost competitiveness. The Carrier Rate Card applicable to Client Product
shipments is attached hereto as Exhibit C.

10.2 LTL and FTL Coordination. The Service Provider shall coordinate both less-than-truckload
and full-truckload shipments as appropriate based on order volume, delivery requirements,
and cost optimization considerations. For FTL shipments, the Service Provider shall manage
carrier scheduling, load planning, and appointment coordination.

10.3 International Shipping. For Client Products destined for international destinations, the
Service Provider shall coordinate with the Client's designated customs broker and freight
forwarder to ensure compliance with applicable export regulations, proper documentation
including commercial invoices and packing lists, and adherence to all applicable trade
compliance requirements.

10.4 Shipping Documentation. The Service Provider shall prepare and maintain all shipping
documentation required for the transportation of Client Products, including bills of lading,
packing lists, shipping labels, hazardous material declarations (if applicable), and
certificates of origin.

11. RETURNS AND REVERSE LOGISTICS

11.1 Returns Authorization. All returns of Client Products from end customers shall be
processed only upon receipt of a valid Returns Merchandise Authorization (RMA) issued by
the Client or, where authorized, by the Service Provider on behalf of the Client.

11.2 Returns Inspection. The Service Provider shall inspect all returned Client Products
within seventy-two (72) hours of receipt at the Facility. Inspection shall include
verification of product identity, assessment of product condition, identification of
missing components or accessories, and photographic documentation.

11.3 Returns Disposition. Following inspection, the Service Provider shall categorize each
returned item according to the Client's disposition guidelines, which may include restocking
to available inventory, refurbishment, liquidation, recycling, or destruction. Disposition
decisions shall be communicated to the Client for approval where required by the Client's
returns policy.

11.4 Returns Reporting. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with weekly returns
summary reports, including the number of returns received, return reason codes, disposition
outcomes, and inventory impact analysis.

12. SEASONAL VOLUME PLANNING

12.1 Volume Forecasts. The Client shall provide the Service Provider with rolling twelve-month
volume forecasts, updated quarterly, to facilitate capacity planning, staffing, and resource
allocation at the Facility.

12.2 Seasonal Surges. During periods of anticipated peak volume, including but not limited to
the spring home improvement season (March through May), back-to-school and fall furnishing
season (August through October), and the holiday season (November through December), the
Service Provider shall plan for and accommodate Shipping Volumes of up to one hundred fifty
percent (150%) of the Monthly Volume Cap, subject to advance notice from the Client in
accordance with Section 12.1.

12.3 Temporary Staffing. The Service Provider shall maintain relationships with qualified
temporary staffing providers and shall procure additional warehouse labor as needed to support
seasonal volume increases, ensuring that temporary staff receive adequate training on
Client-specific handling requirements before being assigned to Client Product operations.

13. TERM

13.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for
a period of eighteen (18) months, concluding on November 30, 2025 (the "Initial Term").

13.2 Renewal. This Agreement shall not automatically renew upon expiration of the Initial
Term. Any extension or renewal of this Agreement beyond the Initial Term shall require the
execution of a written amendment or renewal agreement signed by authorized representatives
of both parties.

13.3 Early Termination. Either party may terminate this Agreement prior to the expiration of
the Initial Term by providing sixty (60) calendar days' prior written notice to the other
party.

14. TERMINATION

14.1 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience
without cause upon sixty (60) calendar days' prior written notice to the other party. The
terminating party shall be responsible for any fees and expenses incurred through the
effective date of termination.

14.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause upon written
notice if the other party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement and
fails to cure such breach within twenty (20) calendar days following receipt of written
notice specifying the nature of the breach.

14.3 Termination for SLA Failure. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Client may terminate
this Agreement upon thirty (30) calendar days' written notice if the Service Provider fails
to meet the performance standards set forth in Section 9.3 for three (3) consecutive
calendar months, provided that the Service Provider has been given written notice of each
month's performance deficiency and a reasonable opportunity to implement corrective measures.

14.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Service
Provider shall cooperate with the Client in the orderly transition of warehouse operations,
including the transfer of Client Products to a designated alternative facility, the return
of all Client materials and documentation, and the provision of final inventory reconciliation
reports within thirty (30) calendar days of the termination effective date.

MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

Agreement Number: MSA-2024-0892
Effective Date: March 1, 2024

PREAMBLE AND RECITALS

WHEREAS, the healthcare industry in the United States operates within one of the most complex and heavily regulated environments of any economic sector, with overlapping federal, state, and local requirements governing the delivery, billing, privacy, and quality of patient care services;

WHEREAS, the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act"), imposes extensive obligations on covered entities and their business associates with respect to the use, disclosure, and safeguarding of protected health information, including requirements for administrative, physical, and technical safeguards as set forth in 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164;

WHEREAS, the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970 ("OSHA") and regulations promulgated thereunder by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration establish mandatory workplace safety standards applicable to healthcare facilities, including standards related to bloodborne pathogens, hazard communication, respiratory protection, and ergonomic hazards;

WHEREAS, state health departments and professional licensing boards impose additional regulatory requirements on healthcare providers, including but not limited to state-level patient privacy laws, mandatory reporting obligations, and facility licensing requirements that may exceed federal minimums;

WHEREAS, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services ("CMS") maintains extensive Conditions of Participation, Conditions for Coverage, and Interpretive Guidelines that govern the operations of healthcare facilities participating in federal healthcare programs;

WHEREAS, the Client operates healthcare facilities and related clinical operations across multiple jurisdictions and recognizes the need for specialized regulatory compliance expertise to navigate the foregoing regulatory landscape and to maintain compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local requirements;

WHEREAS, the Service Provider possesses specialized expertise in healthcare regulatory compliance, including but not limited to HIPAA privacy and security compliance, OSHA workplace safety compliance, healthcare staff training and competency assessment, and regulatory audit preparation and response;

WHEREAS, the Service Provider maintains a team of qualified professionals with backgrounds in healthcare administration, nursing, health information management, occupational health, and regulatory affairs, all of whom hold relevant certifications and have substantial experience in the healthcare compliance field;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to engage the Service Provider to provide healthcare regulatory compliance consulting services to the Client, and to define the terms and conditions under which such services shall be provided;

WHEREAS, both parties acknowledge that the services to be provided hereunder involve potential access to protected health information and other sensitive patient and business data, and that appropriate safeguards must be established to protect such information in accordance with applicable law and industry best practices;

WHEREAS, both parties wish to define their respective rights, obligations, and responsibilities in connection with the services described herein, and to establish clear communication protocols, reporting requirements, and performance standards;

WHEREAS, the healthcare industry has experienced significant regulatory enforcement activity in recent years, including increased scrutiny from the Office for Civil Rights with respect to HIPAA compliance, heightened penalties for willful neglect of privacy and security obligations, and a growing trend toward mandatory compliance program requirements imposed by both federal and state regulators;

WHEREAS, the emergence of new care delivery models, including telehealth, remote patient monitoring, and accountable care organizations, has introduced additional regulatory complexities that require specialized expertise to navigate effectively, particularly with respect to cross-jurisdictional licensing, reimbursement, and privacy requirements;

WHEREAS, recent trends in HIPAA enforcement have demonstrated that the Office for Civil Rights has significantly increased its audit and investigation activities, resulting in numerous multi-million-dollar settlements and resolution agreements with healthcare organizations of all sizes, underscoring the critical importance of proactive compliance program design and ongoing risk management;

WHEREAS, OSHA compliance challenges in the healthcare sector have become increasingly complex in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic, with new and evolving standards related to infectious disease prevention, respiratory protection, workplace violence mitigation, and ergonomics requiring healthcare facilities to continuously update and strengthen their safety programs;

WHEREAS, the proliferation of electronic health records, digital health technologies, and health information exchanges has created new vectors for potential privacy and security breaches, making robust data governance and cybersecurity programs essential components of any comprehensive healthcare compliance strategy;

WHEREAS, the Client acknowledges that regulatory compliance is not a one-time activity but rather an ongoing process that requires continuous monitoring, assessment, training, and improvement to address evolving regulatory requirements, emerging risks, and changes in the Client's operations and business environment;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby mutually acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATIONS

1.1 "Affiliate" means, with respect to any person or entity, any other person or entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with such person or entity. For purposes of this definition, "control" (including, with correlative meanings, "controlled by" and "under common control with") means the possession, directly or indirectly, of the power to direct or cause the direction of the management and policies of a person or entity, whether through ownership of voting securities, by contract, or otherwise.

1.2 "Applicable Laws" means all applicable federal, state, local, and foreign statutes, laws, regulations, ordinances, rules, codes, orders, judgments, decrees, directives, and requirements of any governmental authority, including but not limited to HIPAA, the HITECH Act, OSHA, the False Claims Act, the Anti-Kickback Statute, the Stark Law, state privacy and security laws, and any amendments, successor statutes, or implementing regulations thereto.

1.3 "Audit Finding" means any observation, deficiency, non-conformity, area of concern, or opportunity for improvement identified during the course of a compliance audit, regulatory survey, internal assessment, or other review process conducted by or on behalf of the Service Provider, the Client, or any Regulatory Body, regardless of the severity or classification of such observation.

1.4 "Business Day" means any day other than a Saturday, a Sunday, or a day on which banking institutions in the City of Chicago, Illinois are authorized or required by law or executive order to remain closed.

1.5 "Compliance Committee" means the internal governing body established by the Client to oversee, direct, and monitor the organization's compliance program, including but not limited to the review of compliance policies, investigation of reported concerns, evaluation of audit findings, and implementation of corrective actions, which shall include representatives from senior management, legal, clinical operations, human resources, and information technology.

1.6 "Confidential Information" means all non-public, confidential, or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of one party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other party (the "Receiving Party"), whether in oral, written, electronic, graphic, or other form, regardless of whether such information is marked or designated as confidential. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: trade secrets, proprietary methodologies, business plans, financial data, pricing information, customer lists, patient data, protected health information, employee information, and any other information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential or proprietary in nature.

1.7 "Corrective Action Plan" or "CAP" means a documented, structured plan developed in response to an Audit Finding, compliance deficiency, regulatory citation, or other identified area of non-compliance, which sets forth the specific actions to be taken, the responsible parties, the timeline for completion, the resources required, and the metrics to be used to measure the effectiveness of the corrective actions.

1.8 "Deliverable" or "Deliverables" means any and all work product, reports, analyses, manuals, training materials, policies, procedures, templates, assessments, and other tangible or intangible materials produced or developed by the Service Provider in the course of performing the Services under this Agreement, including any drafts, revisions, or updates thereto.

1.9 "EHR" or "Electronic Health Record" means a digital version of a patient's paper chart that is real-time, patient-centered, and makes information available instantly and securely to authorized users, containing medical and treatment histories of patients, and maintained in compliance with applicable federal and state regulations.

1.10 "EMR" or "Electronic Medical Record" means a digital version of a paper chart in a clinician's office, containing the medical and treatment history of the patients in one practice, and used by clinicians within that practice for diagnosis and treatment.

1.11 "Force Majeure" means any event or circumstance beyond the reasonable control of the affected party that prevents or materially impairs such party's ability to perform its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, public health emergencies, war, terrorism, civil unrest, government actions, orders or restrictions, labor disputes or strikes (other than those involving the affected party's own employees), power outages, telecommunications failures, and failures of third-party service providers or systems.

1.12 "Governing Board" means the board of directors, trustees, or other governing body of the Client that has ultimate authority and responsibility for the overall operation and management of the Client's healthcare facilities and programs, including the authority to establish policies, approve budgets, and ensure compliance with all Applicable Laws.

1.13 "HIPAA" means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended, and the regulations promulgated thereunder, including the Privacy Rule at 45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and E of Part 164, the Security Rule at 45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and C of Part 164, the Breach Notification Rule at 45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and D, and the Enforcement Rule at 45 CFR Part 160, Subparts C, D, and E.

1.14 "HIPAA Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of Protected Health Information in a manner not permitted under HIPAA that compromises the security or privacy of such information, as further defined in 45 CFR Section 164.402, except where an applicable exception applies as set forth in 45 CFR Section 164.402(a).

1.15 "Meaningful Use" means the set of standards defined by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services that governs the use of electronic health records and the eligible providers and hospitals that must adopt such systems and demonstrate their use in order to qualify for Medicare and Medicaid incentive payments and to avoid future payment adjustments.

1.16 "Notice" means any notice, request, demand, consent, approval, or other communication required or permitted under this Agreement, which shall be in writing and delivered in accordance with Section 14.6.

1.17 "OCR" or "Office for Civil Rights" means the office within the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services responsible for enforcing the HIPAA Privacy Rule, the HIPAA Security Rule, and the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule, as well as investigating complaints of discrimination in health and human services.

1.18 "Patient Safety" means the prevention of errors and adverse effects to patients associated with health care, encompassing the processes, structures, and outcomes of care delivery designed to protect patients from preventable harm, including medication errors, healthcare-associated infections, surgical complications, diagnostic errors, and other preventable adverse events.

1.19 "PHI" or "Protected Health Information" has the meaning given to such term in HIPAA and the regulations promulgated thereunder, and means individually identifiable health information that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media or any other form or medium.

1.20 "Quality Measure" means a standardized, evidence-based metric used to assess, compare, and track the quality of healthcare services provided by healthcare organizations, including but not limited to clinical process measures, patient experience measures, outcome measures, and efficiency measures, as defined and reported to CMS, The Joint Commission, state health departments, and other relevant entities.

1.21 "Regulatory Body" means any federal, state, local, or foreign governmental agency, department, commission, board, bureau, authority, or other regulatory body having jurisdiction over the Client or the Services, including but not limited to: the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, the Office for Civil Rights, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, the Occupational Safety and Health Administration, state health departments, state attorneys general, and state professional licensing boards.

1.22 "Risk Assessment" means a comprehensive, systematic process for identifying, evaluating, and prioritizing risks to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of protected health information, patient safety, regulatory compliance, and organizational operations, including the analysis of threats, vulnerabilities, potential impacts, and the development of risk mitigation strategies.

1.23 "Sentinel Event" means an unexpected occurrence involving death or serious physical or psychological injury, or the risk thereof, as defined by The Joint Commission, which signals the need for immediate investigation and response, including but not limited to events involving unanticipated death or major permanent loss of function not related to the natural course of the patient's illness or underlying condition.

1.24 "Services" means the healthcare regulatory compliance consulting services described in Article 4 of this Agreement, together with any modifications, additions, or amendments thereto as may be mutually agreed upon by the parties in writing from time to time.

1.25 "Survey" means an on-site or remote evaluation conducted by a Regulatory Body, accrediting organization, or other authorized entity to assess the Client's compliance with applicable regulatory standards, Conditions of Participation, accreditation requirements, or other applicable requirements, including but not limited to surveys conducted by state health departments, The Joint Commission, CMS, and OSHA.

1.26 "Variance" means a written authorization granted by a Regulatory Body, accrediting organization, or the Client's Governing Board that permits a temporary or permanent departure from a specific regulatory requirement, policy, procedure, or standard, subject to such conditions, limitations, and reporting requirements as may be imposed by the authorizing entity.

1.27 "Work Product" means all materials, documents, analyses, reports, tools, templates, methodologies, processes, procedures, recommendations, and other tangible and intangible results produced, developed, or created by the Service Provider, whether alone or jointly with the Client, in the course of performing the Services.

1.28 "Termination Date" means the date on which this Agreement is terminated or expires in accordance with the provisions of Article 12.

1.29 "Cure Period" means the period of thirty (30) days following written notice of a breach, during which the breaching party may cure such breach.

1.30 "Expenses" means reasonable, documented, out-of-pocket expenses incurred by the Service Provider in connection with the performance of the Services, including but not limited to travel expenses, lodging, meals, photocopying, and specialized software or database access fees, subject to the limitations set forth in Article 7.

ARTICLE 4: SCOPE OF SERVICES

4.1 General Description of Services. The Service Provider shall provide healthcare regulatory compliance consulting services to the Client, including HIPAA audit preparation, OSHA compliance review, staff training programs, and quarterly compliance reporting. The Services shall be performed in accordance with the standards, methodologies, and procedures described in this Agreement and the applicable Exhibits.

4.2 Detailed Scope. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Services shall include the following:

    (a) HIPAA Audit Preparation: Conducting a comprehensive assessment of the Client's current HIPAA privacy and security compliance posture, including review of policies and procedures, administrative safeguards, physical safeguards, technical safeguards, workforce training, business associate agreements, breach notification processes, and documentation practices. This assessment shall include a HIPAA Gap Analysis identifying areas of non-compliance and recommended remediation measures, to be delivered within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date.

    (b) OSHA Compliance Review: Performing a thorough review of the Client's workplace safety practices, programs, and documentation to assess compliance with applicable OSHA standards, including but not limited to bloodborne pathogens, hazard communication, respiratory protection, personal protective equipment, emergency action plans, fire safety, and workplace violence prevention. The Service Provider shall prepare a comprehensive OSHA Compliance Manual documenting current compliance status and required improvements, to be delivered within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Effective Date.

    (c) Staff Training Programs: Developing and delivering customized training programs for the Client's workforce on topics including HIPAA privacy and security awareness, OSHA workplace safety, infection control, emergency preparedness, and other compliance-related subjects as mutually agreed. The Service Provider shall prepare comprehensive Training Materials, including presentations, handouts, quizzes, and attendance documentation templates, to be delivered within one hundred fifty (150) days of the Effective Date.

    (d) Quarterly Compliance Reporting: Preparing and delivering written Quarterly Reports summarizing compliance activities, findings, recommendations, and status updates on remediation efforts. The first Quarterly Report shall be delivered within one hundred eighty (180) days of the Effective Date, with subsequent reports delivered at the end of each calendar quarter thereafter.

4.3 Excluded Services. The following are expressly excluded from the Services and shall be subject to separate engagement and fees if requested by the Client: (a) legal advice or legal representation; (b) direct engagement with Regulatory Bodies on behalf of the Client; (c) implementation or installation of technology systems or software; (d) clinical or medical advice; (e) billing or coding audits; (f) credentialing services; (g) accreditation survey preparation beyond the scope described herein.

4.4 Service Standards. The Service Provider shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry practices and standards applicable to healthcare regulatory compliance consulting. The Service Provider shall assign qualified personnel with appropriate expertise and experience to each engagement.

ARTICLE 5: COMPLIANCE PROGRAM DESIGN

5.1 Program Structure. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in the design, development, and implementation of a comprehensive healthcare compliance program that meets the requirements set forth in the Federal Sentencing Guidelines for Organizations, the OIG Compliance Program Guidance for Healthcare Organizations, and all other applicable regulatory standards. The compliance program shall include, at a minimum, the following seven core elements: (a) written policies and procedures; (b) designation of a compliance officer and compliance committee; (c) training and education; (d) internal monitoring and auditing; (e) reporting mechanisms; (f) enforcement through disciplinary guidelines; and (g) prompt response to detected offenses and development of corrective action plans.

5.2 Policies and Procedures. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in drafting, reviewing, and updating compliance policies and procedures that address all applicable regulatory requirements, including but not limited to HIPAA privacy and security, OSHA workplace safety, anti-kickback and Stark law compliance, billing and coding integrity, conflicts of interest, patient rights, and clinical documentation requirements. All policies and procedures shall be reviewed at least annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in Applicable Laws, regulatory guidance, and the Client's operations.

5.3 Compliance Officer and Committee. The Service Provider shall provide guidance to the Client regarding the establishment and effective operation of a Compliance Committee, including recommendations regarding the committee's composition, charter, meeting frequency, reporting structure, and responsibilities. The Service Provider shall assist the Compliance Committee in developing its annual work plan, setting priorities, and establishing metrics for evaluating the effectiveness of the compliance program.

5.4 Training Requirements. The Service Provider shall develop a training curriculum that addresses the specific compliance risks and regulatory requirements applicable to the Client's operations. Training shall be provided to all members of the workforce, including employees, volunteers, trainees, contractors, and any other persons whose conduct is under the direct control of the Client, regardless of whether they have access to PHI. Training shall be provided upon initial hire and at least annually thereafter, with additional training provided as necessary in response to changes in Applicable Laws, regulatory requirements, or the identification of compliance deficiencies.

5.5 Internal Monitoring and Auditing. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in establishing an internal monitoring and auditing program that includes regular assessments of compliance with key regulatory requirements, analysis of billing patterns and coding accuracy, review of access controls and audit logs for electronic systems, evaluation of physical safeguards, and assessment of workforce compliance with policies and procedures. The results of all monitoring and auditing activities shall be documented and reported to the Compliance Committee on a regular basis.

ARTICLE 6: REGULATORY REPORTING

6.1 CMS Reporting Requirements. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in understanding and meeting all applicable CMS reporting requirements, including but not limited to the submission of quality data through the Hospital Inpatient Quality Reporting Program, the Outpatient Prospective Payment System Quality Reporting Program, the Physician Quality Reporting System, the Value-Based Purchasing Program, the Hospital-Acquired Condition Reduction Program, and any other mandatory or voluntary reporting programs administered by CMS.

6.2 State Health Department Reporting. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in identifying and complying with all applicable state health department reporting requirements in each jurisdiction in which the Client operates, including but not limited to mandatory reporting of healthcare-associated infections, reportable diseases, adverse events, sentinel events, and patient safety incidents. The Service Provider shall prepare a jurisdiction-specific reporting matrix identifying each applicable reporting obligation, the responsible party, the reporting timeline, and the required content of each report.

6.3 Joint Commission Reporting. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in meeting all applicable reporting requirements of The Joint Commission, including the reporting of sentinel events in accordance with The Joint Commission's Sentinel Event Policy, the submission of performance measure data, and the preparation of evidence of standards compliance for survey readiness assessments. The Service Provider shall provide guidance on root cause analysis methodology, corrective action plan development, and the documentation requirements associated with regulatory surveys.

6.4 Adverse Event Reporting. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in establishing processes and procedures for the timely identification, investigation, internal reporting, and external reporting of adverse events, near misses, and other patient safety incidents in accordance with all Applicable Laws and the policies of the Client's Governing Board. The Service Provider shall provide training to the Client's staff on the recognition and reporting of adverse events and the documentation requirements associated with such events.

ARTICLE 7: TRAINING PROGRAM

7.1 Curriculum Development. The Service Provider shall develop a comprehensive training curriculum that covers all regulatory compliance topics relevant to the Client's operations, including but not limited to HIPAA privacy and security awareness, OSHA workplace safety, infection prevention and control, emergency preparedness, cultural competency, patient rights and responsibilities, corporate compliance and ethics, fraud, waste, and abuse prevention, and documentation requirements. The curriculum shall be tiered to address the specific roles, responsibilities, and risk profiles of different workforce categories.

7.2 Delivery Methods. Training shall be delivered through a combination of modalities designed to maximize engagement, knowledge retention, and practical application, including but not limited to: live instructor-led sessions, interactive workshops and case study discussions, web-based learning modules accessible through the Client's learning management system, printed reference materials and quick-reference guides, simulation exercises and tabletop drills for emergency preparedness training, and on-the-job training and competency verification for clinical and technical staff.

7.3 Competency Assessments. Each training module shall include a competency assessment component designed to verify that participants have achieved the learning objectives established for the module. Assessments may include written examinations, practical demonstrations, return demonstrations, case study analyses, and observed performance evaluations. Participants who do not achieve a passing score on any competency assessment shall be provided with additional training and an opportunity to retest within thirty (30) days. All training activities and competency assessment results shall be documented and maintained in accordance with the Client's records retention policies.

7.4 Annual Training Updates. The Service Provider shall review and update the training curriculum at least annually to reflect changes in Applicable Laws, regulatory guidance, industry best practices, and the Client's operational environment. Updated training materials shall be provided to the Client at least sixty (60) days prior to the beginning of each annual training cycle to allow adequate time for review, approval, and scheduling.

ARTICLE 8: QUALITY IMPROVEMENT

8.1 QI Methodology. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in establishing a quality improvement program based on recognized quality improvement methodologies, including but not limited to Plan-Do-Study-Act ("PDSA") cycles, Lean methodology, Six Sigma principles, failure mode and effects analysis ("FMEA"), root cause analysis, and other evidence-based approaches to performance improvement in healthcare settings.

8.2 PDSA Cycles. The Service Provider shall provide guidance and facilitation support for the Client's quality improvement initiatives using the PDSA cycle framework. This shall include assistance with: (a) identifying and prioritizing improvement opportunities based on data analysis, regulatory requirements, and organizational priorities; (b) formulating testable hypotheses and designing small-scale tests of change; (c) implementing tests of change and collecting data to evaluate the effectiveness of the changes; (d) analyzing results and determining whether to adopt, adapt, or abandon the tested changes; and (e) standardizing successful changes and disseminating lessons learned across the organization.

8.3 Outcome Measurement. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in establishing a comprehensive set of quality measures and key performance indicators to track the effectiveness of its compliance program and quality improvement initiatives. Such measures may include, without limitation: compliance training completion rates, incident reporting rates and trends, time to resolution of identified deficiencies, regulatory survey outcomes, audit finding closure rates, patient safety event rates, and workforce satisfaction scores related to compliance culture.

8.4 Continuous Improvement. The Service Provider shall provide recommendations for continuous improvement of the Client's compliance program based on the results of monitoring and auditing activities, regulatory developments, industry best practices, and feedback from the Client's workforce and leadership. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in conducting an annual comprehensive review of the compliance program to assess its overall effectiveness and identify opportunities for enhancement.

ARTICLE 9: EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS

9.1 Pandemic Response Planning. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in developing and maintaining a comprehensive pandemic and infectious disease emergency response plan that addresses, at a minimum: (a) the establishment of an emergency management command structure with clearly defined roles and responsibilities; (b) protocols for surveillance, detection, and early warning of potential pandemic threats; (c) strategies for maintaining continuity of essential healthcare services during a pandemic, including staffing plans, supply chain management, and facility operations; (d) infection prevention and control measures specific to pandemic response, including personal protective equipment requirements, patient cohorting strategies, and environmental cleaning protocols; and (e) communication protocols for internal and external stakeholders, including patients, families, staff, Regulatory Bodies, and the public.

9.2 Disaster Recovery. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in developing a disaster recovery plan that addresses the restoration of critical business functions, information systems, and physical infrastructure following a natural disaster, cyberattack, prolonged power outage, or other catastrophic event. The disaster recovery plan shall include: (a) a business impact analysis identifying critical functions, systems, and processes and their recovery time objectives; (b) data backup and recovery procedures, including offsite storage and cloud-based backup solutions; (c) hardware and software inventory and replacement procedures; (d) alternate site operations plans; and (e) testing and maintenance schedules for the disaster recovery plan.

9.3 Business Continuity. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in developing a business continuity plan that ensures the continued delivery of essential healthcare services during and after an emergency or disaster. The business continuity plan shall address: (a) succession planning for key leadership positions; (b) emergency staffing plans, including mutual aid agreements with other healthcare organizations; (c) supply chain resilience strategies, including identification of critical supplies, alternative vendors, and strategic reserve inventories; (d) communication systems and protocols for emergency operations; and (e) financial contingency planning for emergency response activities.

9.4 Testing and Exercises. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in developing and conducting regular emergency preparedness exercises, including tabletop exercises, functional exercises, and full-scale drills, to test the effectiveness of the Client's emergency response, disaster recovery, and business continuity plans. Exercises shall be conducted at least annually, and the results of each exercise shall be documented, evaluated, and used to identify areas for improvement and update the applicable plans.

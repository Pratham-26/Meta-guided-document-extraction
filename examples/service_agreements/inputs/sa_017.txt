REAL ESTATE PROPERTY MANAGEMENT SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Real Estate Property Management Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number PMG-2024-1208, is entered into as of October 1, 2024
(the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1
below.

WHEREAS, the Phoenix metropolitan area has emerged as one of the fastest-growing
commercial real estate markets in the United States over the past two decades,
driven by sustained population growth, favorable tax structures, business-friendly
regulatory environments, and the relocation of major corporate headquarters from
higher-cost markets along the West Coast and Northeast corridor;

WHEREAS, the commercial office sector in the Phoenix market has undergone
significant transformation in the post-pandemic era, with evolving tenant
demands for flexible workspaces, enhanced building amenities, upgraded HVAC and
air quality systems, and technology-enabled building management platforms that
support hybrid work environments and tenant experience optimization;

WHEREAS, effective property management has become increasingly critical to
maintaining and enhancing the value of commercial office portfolios, requiring
specialized expertise in tenant relations, lease administration, financial
management, capital planning, regulatory compliance, and vendor coordination to
navigate the complexities of multi-tenant commercial properties in a competitive
market environment;

WHEREAS, Desert Crown Realty Trust, a Maryland-chartered real estate investment
trust with a significant portfolio of commercial office properties in the
Phoenix metropolitan area, has determined that professional property management
services are essential to optimizing the operational performance and financial
returns of its portfolio;

WHEREAS, Cornerstone Property Management LLC is a full-service property
management firm headquartered in Scottsdale, Arizona, with extensive experience
managing commercial office properties throughout the Phoenix metropolitan area
and a demonstrated track record of maximizing occupancy rates, minimizing
operating costs, and enhancing tenant satisfaction for institutional real estate
owners;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration,
the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree
as follows:

1. PARTIES

1.1 Service Provider. Cornerstone Property Management LLC, a limited liability
company duly organized and existing under the laws of the State of Arizona, with
its principal offices located at 6200 North Scottsdale Road, Suite 200,
Scottsdale, AZ 85253 (hereinafter referred to as "Service Provider,"
"Cornerstone," or "Manager").

1.2 Client. Desert Crown Realty Trust, a real estate investment trust organized
under the laws of the State of Maryland, with its principal offices located at
3000 Camelback Road, Suite 350, Phoenix, AZ 85016 (hereinafter referred to as
"Client," "Owner," or "Trust").

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents and warrants that
they have the full legal authority to bind their respective organization to the
terms and conditions of this Agreement, and that the execution of this
Agreement has been duly authorized by all necessary corporate and trust approvals.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Base Rent" means the fixed periodic rent payable by a Tenant under a
Lease, prior to the addition of any operating expenses, taxes, insurance
premiums, or other charges.

2.2 "CAM Charges" or "Common Area Maintenance Charges" means the Tenant's
proportionate share of the costs incurred by the Owner for the maintenance,
repair, and operation of the Common Areas, as more fully described in each
Lease.

2.3 "Capital Improvement" means any repair, replacement, alteration, or
addition to the Managed Properties which (a) has a useful life of more than
one (1) year, (b) constitutes an addition to or improvement of the Managed
Properties as distinguished from ordinary maintenance and repair, or (c) is
required by applicable law or regulation, and which is capitalized in accordance
with generally accepted accounting principles.

2.4 "Capital Reserve" means the reserve fund established and maintained by the
Manager in accordance with the approved Annual Operating Budget for the purpose
of funding anticipated Capital Improvements and extraordinary maintenance
expenditures.

2.5 "Common Area" means all areas of the Managed Properties that are not
leased or otherwise occupied by Tenants and that are available for the common
use and enjoyment of all Tenants and their invitees, including but not limited
to lobbies, corridors, elevators, stairwells, restrooms, parking facilities,
landscaped areas, and building mechanical systems.

2.6 "Gross Collected Rents" means the aggregate amount of all Base Rent,
CAM Charges, operating expense reimbursements, parking revenues, and other
income actually collected by the Manager from Tenants during a given period,
net of any allowances, concessions, or abatements granted to Tenants, but
excluding any security deposits held on behalf of Tenants.

2.7 "Gross Lease" means a Lease under which the Tenant pays a fixed Base Rent
and the Owner is responsible for paying all or substantially all operating
expenses, taxes, and insurance costs associated with the leased premises.

2.8 "Lease" means a commercial office lease, sublease, license, or other
occupancy agreement between the Owner (or its predecessor in interest) and a
Tenant with respect to space within the Managed Properties.

2.9 "Lease Abstract" means a comprehensive summary document prepared by the
Manager for each Lease, containing all material business terms and conditions
including rent amounts, escalation schedules, expense reimbursement obligations,
option provisions, tenant improvement allowances, and critical date milestones.

2.10 "Managed Properties" or "Portfolio" means the six (6) commercial office
buildings identified on Exhibit A (Property List) attached hereto, comprising
a total of approximately four hundred twenty thousand (420,000) rentable square
feet in the Phoenix metropolitan area, and such additional properties as may be
added to this Agreement by mutual written agreement of the parties.

2.11 "NNN Lease" or "Triple Net Lease" means a Lease under which the Tenant
pays Base Rent plus the Tenant's proportionate share of real property taxes,
building insurance premiums, and common area maintenance charges.

2.12 "Operating Expenses" means all costs and expenses incurred in connection
with the ownership, management, operation, and maintenance of the Managed
Properties, as more fully defined in each Lease, but excluding capital
expenditures, debt service, depreciation, leasing commissions, and income taxes
of the Owner.

2.13 "Pro Forma" means a projected statement of income and expenses for a
Managed Property, prepared by the Manager in connection with the Annual
Operating Budget process or for the evaluation of proposed transactions.

2.14 "Rent Roll" means a detailed schedule of all Leases for the Managed
Properties, including Tenant names, leased premises, lease commencement and
expiration dates, current Base Rent, rent escalation schedules, security deposit
amounts, and occupancy status.

2.15 "RUBS" or "Ratio Utility Billing System" means a method of allocating
utility costs among Tenants based on a reasonable and equitable formula, such
as square footage, occupancy, or number of occupants.

2.16 "Tenant" means any person, firm, corporation, or other entity that is
a party to a Lease with respect to space within any of the Managed Properties.

2.17 "Tenant Improvement" or "TI" means any construction, renovation, or
alteration work performed within the leased premises of a Tenant, whether at
the expense of the Owner or the Tenant, as more fully described in the
applicable Lease.

2.18 "Turnover" means the process of transitioning a leased premises from one
Tenant to another, including unit inspection, make-ready work, vendor
coordination, lease execution, and Tenant move-in.

2.19 "Vacancy Rate" means the percentage of total rentable square feet in the
Managed Properties that is not currently leased or occupied by Tenants.

2.20 "Work Order" means a formal request for maintenance, repair, or other
services submitted by a Tenant or generated by the Manager in connection with
the management of the Managed Properties.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide full-service property
management services for the Managed Properties, which consist of a portfolio of
six (6) commercial office buildings totaling approximately four hundred twenty
thousand (420,000) rentable square feet located in the Phoenix metropolitan area.
The Service Provider shall serve as the on-site and administrative property
manager for all Managed Properties and shall be responsible for the day-to-day
operation, maintenance, and administration of the Portfolio in accordance with
the terms of this Agreement, all applicable Leases, and all applicable laws,
codes, and regulations.

3.2 Tenant Relations and Lease Administration. The Service Provider shall be
responsible for all aspects of tenant relations and lease administration for
the Managed Properties, including but not limited to the following:

    (a) Serving as the primary point of contact for all Tenant communications,
        inquiries, complaints, and service requests, and responding to all
        routine Tenant inquiries within one (1) business day and all emergency
        requests within four (4) hours;

    (b) Preparing and maintaining comprehensive Lease Abstracts for all active
        Leases, ensuring that all critical dates, rent escalations, expense
        reimbursement obligations, option provisions, and other material terms
        are accurately tracked and monitored;

    (c) Administering all Lease terms and conditions, including rent
        escalations, operating expense pass-throughs and reconciliations,
        tenant improvement allowance disbursements, renewal option notices, and
        expansion and contraction rights;

    (d) Tracking all Lease expiration dates and initiating renewal negotiations
        with Tenants at least twelve (12) months prior to Lease expiration, and
        coordinating with the Owner on renewal terms, rental rate adjustments,
        and tenant improvement commitments;

    (e) Coordinating Tenant move-in and move-out processes, including lease
        commencement orientations, space inspections, utility transfers, access
        credential issuance, and make-ready scheduling;

    (f) Managing all Tenant complaints, disputes, and requests in a professional
        and timely manner, escalating material issues to the Owner as appropriate.

3.3 Rent Collection and Financial Reporting. The Service Provider shall be
responsible for all aspects of rent collection, financial management, and
reporting for the Managed Properties, including but not limited to the following:

    (a) Collecting all rents, additional rents, CAM charges, operating expense
        reimbursements, parking revenues, and other income due under the Leases,
        and depositing all collected funds into the Owner's designated operating
        accounts within one (1) business day of receipt;

    (b) Administering a comprehensive rent collection program, including
        preparation and distribution of monthly rent statements, follow-up on
        delinquent accounts, issuance of late payment notices in accordance with
        Lease terms, and coordination of legal proceedings for non-payment or
        other Tenant defaults when authorized by the Owner;

    (c) Maintaining accurate and complete financial records for each Managed
        Property, including accounts receivable, accounts payable, general
        ledger, security deposit tracking, and bank account reconciliations;

    (d) Preparing and delivering Monthly Financial Statements for each Managed
        Property, including a statement of income and expenses, rent roll
        summary, accounts receivable aging report, variance analysis comparing
        actual performance to budget, and a narrative management report
        highlighting significant events, pending matters, and recommended
        actions;

    (e) Preparing Annual Operating Budgets for each Managed Property and the
        Portfolio as a whole, for review and approval by the Owner, and
        managing all expenditures in accordance with the approved budgets;

    (f) Processing and paying all operating expenses, vendor invoices, utility
        bills, insurance premiums, property taxes, and other obligations of the
        Owner in connection with the Managed Properties, subject to the
        expenditure approval thresholds set forth herein.

3.4 Maintenance and Repair Coordination. The Service Provider shall be
responsible for all aspects of maintenance and repair coordination for the
Managed Properties, including but not limited to the following:

    (a) Administering a comprehensive work order system for receiving,
        prioritizing, assigning, tracking, and documenting completion of all
        maintenance and repair requests, whether generated by Tenants, building
        staff, or the Manager's own inspections;

    (b) Establishing and maintaining emergency response procedures for each
        Managed Property, including twenty-four (24) hour emergency contact
        protocols, on-call maintenance personnel, and pre-arranged emergency
        vendor relationships for plumbing, electrical, HVAC, fire protection,
        and other critical building systems;

    (c) Developing and implementing a preventive maintenance program for all
        building systems, including HVAC, plumbing, electrical, elevators, fire
        protection, life safety, parking structures, and building envelope
        components, based on manufacturer recommendations, industry standards,
        and the specific conditions of each Managed Property;

    (d) Conducting regular property inspections, including weekly building
        walkthroughs, monthly exterior inspections, and quarterly comprehensive
        property condition assessments, and preparing written inspection reports
        for the Owner's review.

3.5 Capital Improvement Project Oversight. The Service Provider shall be
responsible for the oversight and coordination of capital improvement projects
for the Managed Properties, including but not limited to the following:

    (a) Identifying, evaluating, and recommending capital improvement projects
        based on property condition assessments, Tenant needs, market
        conditions, and the Owner's investment objectives;

    (b) Preparing project scopes, specifications, and budget estimates for
        proposed capital improvements for the Owner's review and approval;

    (c) Managing the competitive bidding process for capital improvement
        projects, including solicitation of bids from qualified contractors,
        evaluation of bids, and recommendation of awardees to the Owner;

    (d) Overseeing the execution of approved capital improvement projects,
        including contractor management, schedule coordination, quality
        assurance inspections, change order processing, and progress reporting;

    (e) Tracking and administering all warranties associated with capital
        improvement work, including punch list completion, warranty claim
        processing, and warranty expiration monitoring.

3.6 Vendor Management. The Service Provider shall be responsible for the
selection, engagement, and management of all vendors, contractors, and service
providers engaged in connection with the management and operation of the Managed
Properties, including but not limited to the following:

    (a) Establishing vendor selection criteria and maintaining an approved
        vendor list for all categories of services required for the Managed
        Properties;

    (b) Verifying that all vendors maintain adequate insurance coverage as
        specified in this Agreement and the Vendor Insurance Requirements set
        forth in Exhibit C;

    (c) Negotiating favorable pricing and service terms with vendors, obtaining
        competitive bids for significant services, and monitoring vendor
        performance on an ongoing basis;

    (d) Ensuring that all vendor agreements and service contracts are properly
        executed, maintained, and renewed in a timely manner.

3.7 Common Area Maintenance. The Service Provider shall be responsible for
the maintenance, repair, and upkeep of all Common Areas within the Managed
Properties, including but not limited to common area cleaning, landscaping and
irrigation, parking lot maintenance, exterior lighting, interior corridor and
lobby maintenance, elevator maintenance, and restroom servicing.

3.8 Security Coordination. The Service Provider shall coordinate all security
services for the Managed Properties, including but not limited to the
administration of building access control systems, security guard services,
after-hours patrol services, CCTV monitoring, and coordination with local law
enforcement and emergency services as appropriate.

3.9 Regulatory Compliance. The Service Provider shall be responsible for
ensuring that the Managed Properties comply with all applicable federal, state,
and local laws, codes, regulations, and ordinances, including but not limited
to the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), fire and life safety codes,
elevator inspection requirements, environmental regulations, and all permitting
and licensing requirements applicable to the operation of commercial office
properties in the Phoenix metropolitan area.

4. PROPERTY MANAGEMENT PLAN

4.1 Within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date, the Service Provider shall
prepare and deliver to the Owner a comprehensive Property Management Plan for
each Managed Property. Each Property Management Plan shall address, at a minimum,
the current condition of the property, tenant profile and lease analysis,
operating budget projections, capital improvement recommendations, staffing and
service requirements, vendor management approach, and strategic positioning
recommendations.

4.2 The Property Management Plans shall be updated annually or upon the
occurrence of any material change in the condition, occupancy, or operations of
any Managed Property.

5. ANNUAL OPERATING BUDGETS

5.1 Within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date and no later than November 1
of each subsequent year, the Service Provider shall prepare and submit to the
Owner proposed Annual Operating Budgets for each Managed Property and for the
Portfolio as a whole for the upcoming calendar year.

5.2 Each Annual Operating Budget shall include detailed line-item projections
of all anticipated income and expenses, a narrative explanation of significant
variances from the prior year, and recommended capital improvement projects with
estimated costs and timing.

5.3 The Service Provider shall manage all expenditures in accordance with the
approved Annual Operating Budgets. Expenditures that exceed the approved budget
by more than five percent (5%) for any line item, or that involve a single
expenditure in excess of ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00), shall require the
prior written approval of the Owner.

6. TERM

6.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and
shall continue for a period of thirty-six (36) months, concluding on September
30, 2027 (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with
Section 15.

6.2 Auto-Renewal. Upon expiration of the Initial Term and any subsequent
renewal term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twenty-four
(24) month periods (each a "Renewal Term"), unless either party provides the
other party with at least one hundred twenty (120) calendar days' prior written
notice of its intent not to renew.

6.3 Individual Property Removal. The Owner may remove any individual Managed
Property from this Agreement upon ninety (90) calendar days' prior written
notice to the Service Provider, in which event the management fee shall be
prorated to reflect the reduced portfolio. The Service Provider shall cooperate
fully in transitioning management responsibilities for any removed property to
the Owner's designated successor property manager.

6.4 Survival. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall not affect
any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination,
including without limitation Sections 11 (Intellectual Property), 12
(Confidentiality), 15 (Termination), 16 (Liability), and 18 (Governing Law).

7. COMPENSATION

7.1 Base Management Fee. As compensation for the services provided under this
Agreement, the Owner shall pay the Service Provider a base management fee equal
to three and one-half percent (3.5%) of the Gross Collected Rents for each
calendar month. Based on current lease terms and occupancy levels, the annual
base management fee is estimated to be approximately two hundred ninety-four
thousand dollars ($294,000.00) per year; provided, however, that the actual
fee may be higher or lower depending on the amount of Gross Collected Rents in
any given period.

7.2 Leasing Commission — New Leases. The Owner shall pay the Service Provider
a leasing commission equal to four percent (4%) of the total Base Rent to be
paid during the first year of any new Lease executed for space at any of the
Managed Properties. Leasing commissions for new Leases shall be payable in equal
monthly installments over the first twelve (12) months of the Lease term.

7.3 Leasing Commission — Renewals. The Owner shall pay the Service Provider
a leasing commission equal to two percent (2%) of the total Base Rent to be
paid during the first year of any Lease renewal, extension, or expansion
executed with an existing Tenant. Leasing commissions for renewals shall be
payable in equal monthly installments over the first twelve (12) months of the
renewal term.

7.4 Construction Management Fee. The Owner shall pay the Service Provider a
construction management fee equal to five percent (5%) of the total cost of all
Capital Improvement projects managed by the Service Provider. This fee covers
project management, contractor oversight, quality assurance, change order
processing, and project closeout services. The construction management fee shall
be payable monthly in arrears based on actual project costs incurred during the
preceding month.

7.5 Payment Terms. All fees payable under this Agreement shall be paid by the
Owner to the Service Provider monthly in arrears, within thirty (30) days of
receipt of the Service Provider's monthly invoice. Invoices shall be submitted
no later than the tenth (10th) day of each month for services rendered and fees
earned during the preceding calendar month.

7.6 Late Payments. Invoices not paid within thirty (30) days of receipt shall
be subject to interest at a rate of one percent (1%) per month, calculated from
the due date until the date of actual payment.

8. DELIVERABLES AND REPORTING

8.1 Property Management Plans. The Service Provider shall deliver Property
Management Plans for each Managed Property within thirty (30) days of the
Effective Date, as described in Section 4.

8.2 Annual Operating Budgets. The Service Provider shall deliver proposed
Annual Operating Budgets within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date and no
later than November 1 of each subsequent year, as described in Section 5.

8.3 Monthly Financial Statements. The Service Provider shall deliver Monthly
Financial Statements for each Managed Property no later than the twentieth (20th)
day of the month following the reporting period. Each Monthly Financial Statement
shall include a statement of income and expenses, rent roll summary, accounts
receivable aging report, variance analysis, and narrative management report.

8.4 Quarterly Property Condition Assessments. The Service Provider shall
conduct and deliver written Quarterly Property Condition Assessments for each
Managed Property no later than thirty (30) days following the end of each fiscal
quarter. Each assessment shall include a comprehensive evaluation of building
systems, common areas, structural components, and grounds, together with
recommended maintenance and capital improvement priorities.

8.5 Annual Lease Abstraction Updates. The Service Provider shall review and
update the Lease Abstracts for all active Leases at least annually, and shall
deliver the updated Lease Abstracts to the Owner no later than January 31 of
each year. The updated Lease Abstracts shall reflect all amendments,
modifications, and changes to the Leases during the preceding year.

9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

9.1 Client Ownership. The Owner shall own all financial data, tenant records,
rent rolls, lease files, property-specific documentation, and all other data,
reports, and materials prepared by the Service Provider in the course of
performing services under this Agreement that are specific to the Managed
Properties or the Owner's business (the "Client Materials").

9.2 Service Provider Property. The Service Provider shall retain all right,
title, and interest in its proprietary property management systems, software
platforms, operating templates, form documents, vendor network and
relationships, and proprietary management methodologies, tools, and processes
(the "Manager Property"). The Service Provider grants the Owner a non-exclusive,
non-transferable license to use the Manager Property solely in connection with
the services provided under this Agreement during the Term and any applicable
survival period.

10. CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all
Confidential Information of the other party disclosed in connection with this
Agreement. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, the Owner's
financial data, tenant financial records, lease terms, rental rates, operating
costs, capital improvement plans, investment strategies, and any other
non-public information concerning the Managed Properties or the Owner's business,
as well as the Service Provider's management methodologies, vendor pricing,
proprietary systems, and internal business operations.

10.2 Tenant Financial Records. Notwithstanding the general confidentiality
provisions set forth above, the parties specifically acknowledge that Tenant
financial records, including but not limited to Tenant financial statements,
credit reports, rent payment histories, and security deposit records, constitute
highly sensitive Confidential Information that shall be maintained in strict
confidence for a period of seven (7) years following the expiration or
termination of this Agreement.

10.3 General Duration. Except as otherwise provided in Section 10.2 with
respect to Tenant financial records, the obligations of confidentiality set
forth in this Section 10 shall survive the expiration or termination of this
Agreement for a period of five (5) years.

11. TERMINATION

11.1 Termination Without Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement
without cause upon one hundred twenty (120) calendar days' prior written notice
to the other party.

11.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for
cause if the other party commits a material breach of any provision of this
Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after
receiving written notice specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable
detail.

11.3 Individual Property Removal. The Owner may remove any individual Managed
Property from this Agreement upon ninety (90) calendar days' prior written
notice to the Service Provider, subject to the provisions of Section 6.3.

11.4 Payment Upon Termination. Upon termination of this Agreement for any
reason, the Owner shall pay all undisputed invoices for services rendered and
fees earned through the effective date of termination within thirty (30) days
of such effective date.

11.5 Transition Obligations. Upon receipt of notice of termination, the
Service Provider shall cooperate fully in transitioning management
responsibilities to the Owner's designated successor property manager,
including the transfer of all records, files, keys, access credentials, vendor
contracts, and other materials relating to the Managed Properties.

12. LIABILITY

12.1 Limitation of Liability. TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE
LAW, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT,
INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING
BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF RENTAL INCOME, LOSS OF PROPERTY VALUE, BUSINESS
INTERRUPTION, OR LOSS OF TENANT RELATIONSHIPS, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO
THIS AGREEMENT OR THE PERFORMANCE OF SERVICES HEREUNDER, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER
SUCH DAMAGES ARE BASED ON CONTRACT, TORT, STRICT LIABILITY, ANY OTHER LEGAL
THEORY, AND EVEN IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH
DAMAGES.

12.2 Liability Cap. The Service Provider's total aggregate liability for all
claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement, whether in contract, tort,
or otherwise, shall not exceed one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) per
occurrence and three million dollars ($3,000,000.00) in the aggregate for all
claims arising during any twelve (12) month period.

13. INSURANCE

13.1 General Liability Insurance. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the
Service Provider shall maintain commercial general liability insurance in an
amount not less than two million dollars ($2,000,000.00) per occurrence, covering
bodily injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury arising from
the Service Provider's operations in connection with the Managed Properties.

13.2 Professional Liability Insurance. Throughout the term of this Agreement,
the Service Provider shall maintain professional liability (errors and omissions)
insurance in an amount not less than one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) per
claim, covering claims arising from negligent acts, errors, or omissions in the
provision of property management services under this Agreement.

13.3 Fidelity Bond. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the Service Provider
shall maintain a fidelity bond in an amount not less than one million dollars
($1,000,000.00), covering loss of funds or property resulting from fraudulent
or dishonest acts by the Service Provider's employees, agents, or
representatives.

13.4 Evidence of Coverage. The Service Provider shall provide certificates of
insurance evidencing the required coverage to the Owner upon execution of this
Agreement and upon each renewal of such policies. All insurance policies shall
name the Owner as an additional insured with respect to the general liability
coverage, and shall provide the Owner with at least thirty (30) calendar days'
prior written notice of cancellation or material modification.

14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

14.1 Mediation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to
this Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve such dispute through
mediation administered by a mutually agreed-upon mediator in Phoenix, Arizona.
The parties shall share equally the costs of mediation.

14.2 Arbitration. If the parties are unable to resolve a dispute through
mediation within sixty (60) days of initiating the mediation process, such
dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration in Phoenix, Arizona, in
accordance with the commercial arbitration rules of the American Arbitration
Association (AAA) then in effect. The decision of the arbitrator(s) shall be
final and binding, and judgment upon the award may be entered in any court of
competent jurisdiction.

15. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of
its obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such failure or delay
is caused by circumstances beyond the reasonable control of the affected party,
including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, government
actions, labor disputes, civil unrest, war, terrorism, power or
telecommunications failures, and public health emergencies. The affected party
shall notify the other party promptly in writing of the force majeure event
and its expected duration, and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to
mitigate its impact on the performance of its obligations under this Agreement.

18. GENERAL PROVISIONS

18.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, including all exhibits attached hereto,
constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the
subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements,
understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written, between
the parties relating to such subject matter.

18.2 Amendments. This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented
except by a written instrument signed by both parties.

18.3 Assignment. Neither party may assign or transfer any of its rights or
obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other
party, except that the Owner may assign this Agreement to a successor entity
in connection with a merger, acquisition, sale of the Managed Properties, or
transfer of substantially all of the Owner's assets.

18.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid,
illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision
shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and
enforceable, and the remaining provisions of this Agreement shall continue in
full force and effect.

18.5 Waiver. The failure of either party to enforce any provision of this
Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such party's right to enforce such
provision or any other provision in the future.

18.6 Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be
in writing and shall be deemed delivered when (a) delivered personally, (b)
sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, (c) sent by nationally
recognized overnight courier, or (d) sent by email with confirmation of
receipt, to the addresses set forth in Section 1 or such other address as
either party may designate in writing.

18.7 Independent Contractor. The Service Provider is an independent contractor
and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Owner. Nothing
in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship,
partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties.

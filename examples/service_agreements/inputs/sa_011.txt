ENVIRONMENTAL CONSULTING SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Environmental Consulting Services Agreement (the "Agreement"), bearing
reference number ECS-2024-4456, is entered into as of August 1, 2024 (the
"Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the renewable energy sector has experienced unprecedented growth in the
United States over the past decade, with utility-scale solar installations
expanding at a compound annual growth rate exceeding twenty-five percent and
contributing to a transformative shift in the nation's electricity generation
portfolio. This rapid expansion has been driven by declining photovoltaic module
costs, favorable federal and state policy frameworks including the Investment
Tax Credit and Production Tax Credit, corporate procurement commitments, and
increasing utility demand for clean energy resources to meet renewable portfolio
standards and decarbonization targets adopted by state legislatures across the
country;

WHEREAS, the development of utility-scale solar energy facilities on greenfield
sites in ecologically sensitive regions such as West Texas presents a complex
array of environmental regulatory requirements that must be carefully navigated
to ensure compliance with federal, state, and local environmental laws and
regulations. These requirements encompass, but are not limited to, the National
Environmental Policy Act (NEPA), the Endangered Species Act (ESA), the Clean
Water Act (CWA), the Clean Air Act (CAA), the National Historic Preservation Act
(NHPA), and their respective implementing regulations, as well as state-level
environmental permitting requirements administered by the Texas Commission on
Environmental Quality (TCEQ) and other applicable state and local agencies;

WHEREAS, the identification and assessment of potential environmental impacts
associated with large-scale solar development requires the application of
specialized scientific expertise across multiple environmental disciplines,
including ecology, biology, hydrology, geology, air quality science, noise
engineering, and cultural resource management. The complexity of these
assessments is further amplified by the need to coordinate with numerous federal
and state regulatory agencies, each with distinct jurisdictional authorities,
review procedures, and consultation requirements that must be satisfied before
development activities may lawfully commence;

WHEREAS, TerraVerde Environmental Consultants LLC brings over twenty years of
specialized experience in providing comprehensive environmental consulting
services to the renewable energy industry, with a demonstrated track record of
successfully guiding utility-scale solar, wind, and energy storage projects
through the full spectrum of environmental permitting and compliance processes
across the southwestern and south-central United States;

WHEREAS, Meridian Energy Partners LP has identified a 500-acre site in West
Texas for the development of a utility-scale solar farm installation and has
determined that comprehensive environmental consulting services are required to
characterize the environmental conditions of the site, assess potential impacts
associated with the proposed development, and obtain all necessary environmental
permits and approvals required for construction and operation;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the
receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as
follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "ASTM E1527-21" means the Standard Practice for Environmental Site
Assessments: Phase I Environmental Site Assessment Process, published by ASTM
International, as revised or superseded.

2.2 "ASTM E1903-19" means the Standard Practice for Environmental Site
Assessments: Phase II Environmental Site Assessment Process, published by ASTM
International, as revised or superseded.

2.3 "Brownfield" means a property, the expansion, redevelopment, or reuse of
which may be complicated by the presence or potential presence of a hazardous
substance, pollutant, or contaminant.

2.4 "Cultural Resource" means any prehistoric or historic district, site,
building, structure, or object included in, or eligible for inclusion in, the
National Register of Historic Places, as defined by 36 CFR Part 60, including
properties of traditional religious and cultural importance to federally
recognized Indian tribes.

2.5 "Endangered Species Act" or "ESA" means the Endangered Species Act of 1973,
16 U.S.C. Sections 1531-1544, as amended, and the regulations promulgated
thereunder.

2.6 "EPA" means the United States Environmental Protection Agency.

2.7 "NEPA" means the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969, 42 U.S.C.
Sections 4321-4370m-12, as amended, and the regulations implementing NEPA
promulgated by the Council on Environmental Quality (CEQ) at 40 CFR Parts
1500-1508.

2.8 "Phase I ESA" means a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment conducted in
substantial accordance with the procedures and requirements set forth in ASTM
E1527-21, including a records review, site reconnaissance, interviews, and
preparation of a written report.

2.9 "Phase II ESA" means a Phase II Environmental Site Assessment conducted in
substantial accordance with the procedures and requirements set forth in ASTM
E1903-19, including subsurface investigation, sampling, and laboratory analysis
to confirm or deny the presence of recognized environmental conditions identified
during the Phase I ESA.

2.10 "Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan" or "SWPPP" means a comprehensive
plan identifying potential sources of stormwater pollution at the Project Site
and describing the Best Management Practices (BMPs) to be implemented to
minimize or eliminate the discharge of pollutants in stormwater runoff.

2.11 "Threatened Species" means any species which is likely to become an
endangered species within the foreseeable future throughout all or a significant
portion of its range, as designated by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service or
the National Marine Fisheries Service under the Endangered Species Act.

2.12 "TCEQ" means the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality, the state
agency responsible for environmental regulation in the State of Texas.

2.13 "USFWS" means the United States Fish and Wildlife Service, the federal
agency within the Department of the Interior responsible for the conservation,
protection, and enhancement of fish, wildlife, plants, and their habitats.

2.14 "USACE" means the United States Army Corps of Engineers, the federal agency
responsible for administering the Section 404 permit program under the Clean
Water Act and for establishing and applying the methodology for delineation of
jurisdictional wetlands.

2.15 "Wetlands" means those areas that are inundated or saturated by surface
water or groundwater at a frequency and duration sufficient to support, and that
under normal circumstances do support, a prevalence of vegetation typically
adapted for life in saturated soil conditions, as defined by the USACE at 33 CFR
Part 328 and further clarified by the Federal Manual for Identifying and
Delineating Jurisdictional Wetlands.

2.16 "Wind Rose" means a meteorological tool used to display the distribution
of wind speed and direction at a particular location over a specified period of
time, used in the assessment of air quality dispersion modeling and noise impact
analysis for the Project Site.

2.17 "BMP" means Best Management Practice, a technique, measure, or structural
control that is used for a given set of conditions to manage the quantity and
improve the quality of stormwater runoff in the most cost-effective manner.

2.18 "Project Site" means the approximately 500-acre parcel of real property
located in West Texas, the legal description of which is set forth in Exhibit A,
upon which the Client proposes to develop a utility-scale solar farm installation.

2.19 "Confidential Information" means all non-public environmental data, site
assessment results, biological survey findings, cultural resource information,
permit applications, regulatory correspondence, and other sensitive information
disclosed by either party in connection with this Agreement.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive
environmental consulting services for the Client's proposed solar farm
installation on the Project Site, including but not limited to the following
service categories:

    (a) Phase I Environmental Site Assessment. Conduct a Phase I Environmental
    Site Assessment of the Project Site in accordance with ASTM E1527-21,
    including historical use research, regulatory database review, site
    reconnaissance, interviews with knowledgeable parties, and preparation of a
    comprehensive Phase I ESA report identifying any recognized environmental
    conditions, controlled recognized environmental conditions, or historical
    recognized environmental conditions associated with the Project Site.

    (b) Phase II Environmental Site Assessment. If the Phase I ESA identifies
    recognized environmental conditions that warrant further investigation, the
    Service Provider shall conduct a Phase II Environmental Site Assessment in
    accordance with ASTM E1903-19, including the design and implementation of
    a subsurface investigation and sampling program, laboratory analysis of
    soil, groundwater, and/or soil vapor samples, and preparation of a Phase II
    ESA report with findings and recommendations. The Phase II ESA is
    expressly conditioned upon and contingent on the findings of the Phase I
    ESA and shall not be initiated without the Client's written authorization
    following review and acceptance of the Phase I ESA report.

    (c) Wetlands Delineation. Conduct a comprehensive wetlands delineation of
    the Project Site in accordance with the USACE Wetlands Delineation Manual
    (1987 Manual) and the applicable Regional Supplement, including field
    identification and delineation of jurisdictional wetlands and other waters
    of the United States, preparation of a wetlands delineation report with
    data sheets and maps, and coordination with the USACE for a preliminary
    jurisdictional determination (JD).

    (d) Threatened and Endangered Species Surveys. Conduct biological surveys
    of the Project Site to identify the presence or absence of federal- and
    state-listed threatened and endangered species and their designated critical
    habitat, including protocol-level surveys for any target species identified
    during the desktop review, habitat suitability assessments, and preparation
    of a biological assessment report for coordination with the USFWS under
    Section 7 of the Endangered Species Act.

    (e) Cultural Resource Surveys. Conduct an intensive cultural resource survey
    of the Project Site in accordance with the Secretary of the Interior's
    Standards and Guidelines for Archaeology and Historic Preservation,
    including pedestrian survey, shovel testing, and preparation of a cultural
    resource survey report for review by the Texas Historical Commission (THC)
    and for compliance with Section 106 of the National Historic Preservation
    Act, including initiation of government-to-government consultation with
    federally recognized Indian tribes as appropriate.

    (f) NEPA Compliance Documentation. Prepare NEPA compliance documentation for
    the proposed solar farm installation, which may include a Categorical
    Exclusion determination, an Environmental Assessment (EA), or, if
    warranted based on the results of the environmental studies, an
    Environmental Impact Statement (EIS), including all supporting technical
    appendices, public notice and comment coordination, and agency review
    facilitation.

    (g) Air Quality Impact Assessment. Conduct an air quality impact assessment
    for the Project Site, including atmospheric dispersion modeling, ambient air
    quality monitoring, evaluation of potential criteria pollutant and
    hazardous air pollutant emissions associated with construction and operation
    of the proposed solar facility, and assessment of conformity with
    applicable National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS) and state
    implementation plan requirements.

    (h) Noise Impact Study. Conduct a noise impact study for the Project Site,
    including baseline ambient noise level measurements, prediction of
    construction and operational noise levels at receptor locations, assessment
    of compliance with applicable federal, state, and local noise standards
    and ordinances, and identification and evaluation of feasible noise
    mitigation measures.

    (i) Stormwater Management Plan. Prepare a comprehensive Stormwater
    Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) for the Project Site, including
    identification of potential pollutant sources, design of Best Management
    Practices (BMPs) for erosion and sediment control during construction and
    stormwater management during operation, preparation of stormwater
    management facility design criteria, and coordination with TCEQ regarding
    Construction General Permit (TXR150000) and Industrial Stormwater Permit
    requirements.

4. ENVIRONMENTAL SITE ASSESSMENT METHODOLOGY

4.1 Phase I ESA Procedures. The Phase I ESA shall be conducted in accordance
with ASTM E1527-21. The Service Provider shall perform a thorough review of
historical records and environmental database reports for the Project Site and
all adjoining properties within the applicable search distances specified by
ASTM E1527-21, including but not limited to EDR, TRW, and state environmental
database reviews. The site reconnaissance shall include a systematic visual
inspection of the Project Site and all adjoining properties, with photographic
documentation of observed conditions.

4.2 Phase II ESA Procedures. If authorized by the Client, the Phase II ESA
shall be conducted in accordance with ASTM E1903-19. The scope of the Phase II
ESA shall be determined based on the findings of the Phase I ESA and shall be
documented in a Phase II ESA Work Plan submitted to the Client for review and
approval prior to commencement of field activities. The Work Plan shall identify
the specific media to be investigated (soil, groundwater, soil vapor, surface
water), the number and location of sample points, the analytical methods and
detection limits, and the data quality objectives for the investigation.

5. WETLANDS DELINEATION PROCEDURES

5.1 Delineation Methodology. Wetlands delineation shall be conducted in
accordance with the USACE Wetlands Delineation Manual (1987) and the applicable
Regional Supplement for the Arid West region, as well as the USACE guidance
documents regarding the delineation of waters of the United States following
the Sackett v. Environmental Protection Agency decision. The delineation shall
include the collection of vegetation, soils, and hydrology data at
representative sample points across the Project Site to determine the presence
or absence of jurisdictional wetlands and other waters of the United States.

5.2 Delineation Report. The Service Provider shall prepare a comprehensive
wetlands delineation report that includes a project description, methodology
description, data sheets for each sample point, a wetlands delineation map
showing the boundaries of all identified jurisdictional areas, and a
determination of the total acreage of jurisdictional wetlands and other waters
on the Project Site. The report shall be prepared in a format suitable for
submission to the USACE for a preliminary jurisdictional determination.

6. BIOLOGICAL SURVEYS

6.1 Protocol for T&E Species. The Service Provider shall conduct biological
surveys in accordance with published USFWS survey protocols for any target
species identified during the desktop review, including focused surveys for
federally listed species known to occur in the Project Site vicinity. Survey
methods, timing, and effort shall comply with the applicable USFWS survey
guidelines and state wildlife agency protocols.

6.2 Habitat Assessment. The Service Provider shall conduct a habitat
suitability assessment for the Project Site, evaluating the presence and
quality of habitat features required by potentially occurring threatened and
endangered species, including foraging habitat, nesting habitat, migration
corridors, and potential roost sites.

6.3 Seasonal Survey Windows. Where applicable, biological surveys shall be
conducted during the appropriate seasonal survey windows established by the
USFWS and applicable state wildlife agencies to ensure maximum detectability of
target species. If seasonal windows are missed due to factors beyond the
Service Provider's control, the Client shall be responsible for authorizing
additional survey efforts in subsequent survey seasons.

7. CULTURAL RESOURCE ASSESSMENT

7.1 Section 106 Compliance. The Service Provider shall conduct an intensive
(census-level) cultural resource survey of the Project Site in accordance with
the Secretary of the Interior's Standards and Guidelines for Identification
and Evaluation, 36 CFR Part 61, and the Council on Environmental Quality's
regulations implementing Section 106 of the National Historic Preservation Act,
36 CFR Part 800.

7.2 Tribal Coordination. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in
initiating government-to-government consultation with federally recognized
Indian tribes that may have cultural or religious ties to the Project Site or
the surrounding area, in accordance with the requirements of Section 106, the
Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act (NAGPRA), and applicable
tribal consultation policies and executive orders.

8. NEPA DOCUMENTATION

8.1 Categorical Exclusions. The Service Provider shall initially assess whether
the proposed solar farm installation qualifies for a Categorical Exclusion
under the applicable federal agency's NEPA procedures. If a Categorical
Exclusion is appropriate, the Service Provider shall prepare the necessary
documentation and coordinate with the lead federal agency to obtain the
applicable determination.

8.2 Environmental Assessment. If the proposed action does not qualify for a
Categorical Exclusion, the Service Provider shall prepare an Environmental
Assessment (EA) in accordance with 40 CFR Part 1508.6, including a description
of the proposed action, identification and evaluation of the affected
environment, analysis of the environmental consequences of the proposed action
and alternatives, and preparation of a Finding of No Significant Impact (FONSI)
if warranted by the analysis.

8.3 EIS Thresholds. If the EA analysis indicates that the proposed action may
result in significant environmental impacts, the Service Provider shall notify
the Client and the lead federal agency of the need to prepare an Environmental
Impact Statement (EIS). Preparation of an EIS would constitute a change in
scope under this Agreement and would be subject to separate negotiation and
pricing.

9. AIR QUALITY ASSESSMENT

9.1 Dispersion Modeling. The Service Provider shall conduct air quality
dispersion modeling using EPA-approved models (e.g., AERMOD, CALPUFF) to
estimate ground-level pollutant concentrations resulting from construction
activities and facility operations, including fugitive dust emissions from
site preparation and grading activities.

9.2 Ambient Monitoring. The Service Provider shall deploy ambient air quality
monitoring equipment at the Project Site and at downwind receptor locations to
establish baseline ambient air quality conditions and to support the
dispersion modeling analysis.

9.3 Permit Requirements. The Service Provider shall assess the need for
applicable federal, state, and local air quality permits, including but not
limited to TCEQ New Source Review permits, federal Title V operating permits,
and any applicable permit-by-rule authorizations.

10. NOISE IMPACT STUDY

10.1 Measurement Methodology. The Service Provider shall conduct baseline
ambient noise level measurements at representative receptor locations in the
vicinity of the Project Site in accordance with ANSI/ASA S12.9 and applicable
TCEQ guidance. Measurements shall be conducted over a minimum continuous
24-hour period at each receptor location to characterize diurnal noise patterns.

10.2 Receptor Analysis. The Service Provider shall identify and categorize
noise-sensitive receptors within the study area, including residential
dwellings, schools, churches, hospitals, and other occupied structures, and
shall predict the construction-period and operational-period noise levels at
each identified receptor location.

10.3 Mitigation Measures. Where predicted noise levels are projected to exceed
applicable standards or to result in a substantial increase over existing
ambient conditions, the Service Provider shall identify and evaluate feasible
noise mitigation measures, including but not limited to equipment noise
specifications, setbacks, barriers, and operational restrictions.

11. STORMWATER MANAGEMENT

11.1 SWPPP Requirements. The Service Provider shall prepare a comprehensive
Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) for the Project Site in accordance
with the requirements of the TCEQ Construction General Permit (TXR150000) and
the EPA Multi-Sector General Permit (MSGP), as applicable.

11.2 BMP Design. The Service Provider shall design Best Management Practices
(BMPs) for the Project Site, including structural controls (silt fences,
sediment basins, check dams, rock outlet protection) and non-structural
controls (phased construction sequencing, preservation of existing vegetation,
minimization of disturbed area) to control erosion and sedimentation during
construction and to manage stormwater quality and quantity during operation.

12. QUALITY ASSURANCE

12.1 Data Validation. The Service Provider shall implement a comprehensive
data validation program for all analytical data generated under this Agreement,
including field duplicate samples, equipment blanks, trip blanks, matrix spike
and matrix spike duplicate samples, and laboratory control samples, in
accordance with applicable EPA guidance documents and project-specific data
quality objectives.

12.2 Chain of Custody. The Service Provider shall maintain a rigorous chain
of custody for all environmental samples collected under this Agreement,
including field documentation, sample shipping records, laboratory receipt
documentation, and electronic data deliverable tracking.

12.3 Laboratory Accreditation. All laboratory analyses performed in support
of the environmental investigations under this Agreement shall be conducted by
laboratories accredited under the National Environmental Laboratory
Accreditation Program (NELAP) or an equivalent accreditation program, and
shall employ analytical methods approved by EPA or applicable state regulatory
agencies.

13. REGULATORY AGENCY COORDINATION

13.1 Agency Correspondence. The Service Provider shall prepare and submit all
correspondence, applications, and documentation required for coordination with
federal and state regulatory agencies, including but not limited to the USACE,
USFWS, TCEQ, Texas Historical Commission, and the lead federal agency
responsible for NEPA compliance.

13.2 Permit Applications. The Service Provider shall prepare and submit all
permit applications required for the development of the Project Site, including
but not limited to USACE Section 404 individual permit applications, TCEQ
water quality certifications, and applicable stormwater permit registrations.

13.3 Meeting Coordination. The Service Provider shall coordinate and
facilitate meetings with regulatory agency staff as necessary to advance the
environmental review and permitting process, and shall prepare meeting agendas,
attend meetings, and provide meeting minutes and follow-up documentation.

Assessment described in Section 3.1(a), inclusive of all labor, travel,
database review, report preparation, and other costs associated with the
completion of the Phase I ESA report.

15.2 Phase II ESA Fee. If the Client authorizes the Phase II ESA described in
Section 3.1(b), the Client shall pay the Service Provider a fixed fee of
sixty-five thousand dollars ($65,000.00) for the Phase II Environmental Site
Assessment, inclusive of all labor, field investigation, sampling, laboratory
analysis, report preparation, and other costs associated with the completion of
the Phase II ESA report. The Phase II ESA fee is expressly contingent upon the
findings of the Phase I ESA and the Client's written authorization to proceed
with Phase II investigation activities.

15.3 Monthly Retainer. In addition to the fixed fees described in Sections
15.1 and 15.2, the Client shall pay the Service Provider a monthly retainer of
twelve thousand dollars ($12,000.00) for ongoing environmental monitoring,
reporting, regulatory agency coordination, and other services performed during
the term of this Agreement that are not covered by the fixed-fee service
categories described above. The monthly retainer shall be payable commencing
on the Effective Date and continuing on the first day of each calendar month
throughout the term of this Agreement.

15.4 Field Work Day Rates. For field work days requiring the on-site presence
of Service Provider scientists at the Project Site, the Client shall pay a
rate of two thousand eight hundred dollars ($2,800.00) per scientist per day.
Field work day rates apply exclusively to on-site field work activities,
including but not limited to site reconnaissance, soil and groundwater
sampling, biological survey fieldwork, wetlands delineation fieldwork, and
noise monitoring field measurements. Field work day rates do not apply to
office-based activities, report preparation, data analysis, or regulatory
coordination, which are covered by the fixed fees and monthly retainer described
above.

15.5 Payment Terms. Invoices shall be submitted monthly and shall be payable
by the Client within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt. Invoices shall
include a detailed breakdown of all services rendered, field work days
incurred, and applicable expenses during the billing period.

RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT CONSORTIUM SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Research and Development Consortium Services Agreement (the
"Collaboration Agreement") is entered into by and between the parties
identified below (each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties") in
connection with a multi-institutional collaborative preclinical research
program for the development of a novel antibody-nanoparticle drug delivery
platform for oncology applications.

Reference: Exhibit 1 — Agreement Reference Number: RDC-2024-[REDACTED]

WHEREAS, the discovery and development of effective oncology therapeutics
remains one of the most resource-intensive and technically challenging
endeavors in modern pharmaceutical science, with the average cost of
bringing a single new cancer therapy from bench to bedside exceeding
$2.6 billion and timelines routinely spanning twelve to fifteen years from
initial target identification through regulatory approval;

WHEREAS, collaborative research models involving partnerships between
academic and nonprofit research institutions, biotechnology companies, and
pharmaceutical corporations have emerged as a highly effective approach to
accelerating drug development timelines, sharing the financial risk
inherent in early-stage research, and leveraging complementary expertise
and capabilities across organizational boundaries;

WHEREAS, the National Institutes of Health (NIH) has issued guidelines
encouraging industry-academia partnerships through programs such as the
Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology
Transfer (STTR) programs, the NIH Cooperative Research and Development
Agreement (CRADA) framework, and the Clinical and Translational Science
Awards (CTSA) program, all of which promote the translation of basic
scientific discoveries into clinical applications;

WHEREAS, the Bayh-Dole Act (35 U.S.C. §§ 200-212) provides a statutory
framework for the allocation of intellectual property rights in inventions
made under federally funded research, and establishes guidelines for the
licensing of such inventions to promote commercialization and ensure that
the benefits of federally funded research are made available to the public;

WHEREAS, BioGenesis Research Institute, a nonprofit research corporation
organized under the laws of the State of Maryland, has developed proprietary
monoclonal antibody technology targeting tumor-associated antigens in solid
tumors, and maintains state-of-the-art laboratory facilities and scientific
staff with deep expertise in antibody engineering, protein expression, and
preclinical oncology models;

WHEREAS, NanoMed Devices LLC, a limited liability company organized under
the laws of the State of Colorado, has developed proprietary nanoparticle
drug delivery platforms capable of encapsulating therapeutic antibodies and
targeting them to specific tumor microenvironments, and maintains
specialized equipment for nanoparticle formulation, characterization, and
scale-up;

WHEREAS, PharmaDyne Therapeutics Corp., a corporation organized under the
laws of the State of Delaware, maintains an established oncology drug
development pipeline with expertise in preclinical validation, regulatory
strategy, and clinical translation, and desires to leverage the combined
capabilities of BioGenesis and NanoMed to develop a novel antibody-
nanoparticle drug delivery platform for its oncology therapeutic programs;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to establish a collaborative research consortium
(the "Consortium") to conduct preclinical research combining BioGenesis's
antibody technology with NanoMed's nanoparticle delivery system, jointly
developed for application to PharmaDyne's oncology pipeline, under the
terms and conditions set forth in this Collaboration Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration,
the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties
agree as follows:

4. RESEARCH PROGRAM AND SCOPE

4.1 Research Objective. The Research Program shall be directed toward the
collaborative development of a novel drug delivery platform that combines
BioGenesis's proprietary monoclonal antibody technology targeting tumor-
associated antigens with NanoMed's proprietary nanoparticle delivery system,
for the purpose of delivering therapeutic antibodies with enhanced tumor
targeting, improved pharmacokinetics, and reduced systemic toxicity. The
platform shall be developed for application to PharmaDyne's oncology
pipeline, with initial focus on solid tumor indications.

4.2 Research Phases. The Research Program shall be conducted in three
sequential phases:

    (a) Feasibility Phase (Months 1-6): Screening and characterization of
    antibody-nanoparticle conjugates, assessment of in vitro binding affinity
    and specificity, preliminary formulation optimization, and initial
    cytotoxicity screening in relevant cancer cell lines. Success criteria:
    identification of at least one lead conjugate candidate demonstrating
    binding affinity (Kd) of less than 10 nM and in vitro cytotoxicity IC50
    of less than 500 nM in at least two cancer cell lines.

    (b) Optimization Phase (Months 7-18): Systematic optimization of lead
    conjugate formulation including nanoparticle size, surface charge, drug
    loading efficiency, and stability characterization; in vivo
    pharmacokinetic and biodistribution studies in murine xenograft models;
dose-range finding studies; and preliminary efficacy evaluation. Success
    criteria: achievement of tumor-to-plasma concentration ratio of greater
    than 5:1 at 24 hours post-dose, and demonstration of at least 50% tumor
    growth inhibition in at least one xenograft model.

    (c) Preclinical Validation Phase (Months 19-30): Comprehensive
    preclinical efficacy studies in multiple xenograft and syngeneic tumor
    models; GLP-compliant toxicology studies (single-dose and repeat-dose);
    IND-enabling pharmacology and toxicology studies; preparation of
    preclinical validation dossier suitable for regulatory submission. Success
    criteria: demonstration of statistically significant tumor growth
    inhibition (p < 0.05) in at least two tumor models, and completion of
    GLP toxicology package meeting FDA guidance for IND-enabling studies.

4.3 Research Governance. The Steering Committee shall have overall
governance authority over the Research Program, including approval of the
Research Plan, oversight of Phase transitions, resolution of scientific
disputes, and approval of any material changes to the scope, timeline, or
budget of the Research Program.

5. STEERING COMMITTEE

5.1 Composition. The Steering Committee shall be composed of three (3)
representatives from BioGenesis (including the Program Director), two (2)
representatives from PharmaDyne (including the R&D Project Lead), and two
(2) representatives from NanoMed (including the VP of Technology Development),
for a total of seven (7) members. BioGenesis shall designate the Chair of
the Steering Committee.

5.2 Voting. Decisions of the Steering Committee shall be made by majority
vote, provided that no decision to advance from one Phase to the next shall
be made without the affirmative vote of at least one (1) representative from
each Party. In the event of a deadlock on any matter other than a Phase
transition, the Chair of the Steering Committee shall have the deciding vote.

5.3 Meetings. The Steering Committee shall meet in person at least quarterly
and shall convene by teleconference at least monthly during the active
phases of the Research Program. Additional meetings may be called by the
Chair or upon the request of any two (2) members.

5.4 Phase Gate Reviews. At the conclusion of each Phase of the Research
Program, the Steering Committee shall conduct a comprehensive Phase Gate
Review to evaluate the results of the completed Phase against the go/no-go
criteria set forth in the Research Plan. The Steering Committee shall issue
a written determination as to whether the Research Program shall advance to
the next Phase, be modified and repeated, or be terminated.

6. PUBLICATION RIGHTS

6.1 General Right to Publish. The Parties acknowledge the importance of
academic freedom and the free exchange of scientific information. Subject
to the provisions of this Section 6, each Party shall have the right to
publish, present, or otherwise disclose the results of the Research Program
in scientific journals, at academic conferences, or through other public
forums.

6.2 Patent Filing Delay. Prior to submitting any manuscript, abstract, or
presentation for publication or public disclosure that discloses any
invention or discovery that may be patentable, the publishing Party shall
provide written notice to the other Parties at least sixty (60) days in
advance of the intended submission date. Upon receipt of such notice, any
Party may request, with written justification, a delay of up to ninety (90)
additional days to permit the filing of one or more patent applications
covering the disclosed invention.

6.3 Manuscript Review. At least thirty (30) days prior to submission for
publication, the publishing Party shall provide draft manuscripts to the
other Parties for review and comment. The other Parties shall have fifteen
(15) days to provide comments, which the publishing Party shall consider in
good faith. Publication shall not be unreasonably withheld; provided,
however, that no Party shall be required to consent to publication that
discloses its trade secrets or confidential business information.

7. FUNDING AND CONTRIBUTIONS

7.1 Total Program Value. The estimated total value of contributions to the
Research Program from all Parties is eight million one hundred thousand
dollars ($8,100,000.00), comprising cash contributions from PharmaDyne and
in-kind contributions from NanoMed and BioGenesis as described below.

7.2 PharmaDyne Cash Contributions. PharmaDyne shall contribute a total of
four million two hundred thousand dollars ($4,200,000.00) in cash funding
to the Research Program, disbursed in three (3) equal tranches of one
million four hundred thousand dollars ($1,400,000.00) each, as follows:
(a) First Tranche: $1,400,000.00 payable upon execution of this
Collaboration Agreement by all Parties;
(b) Second Tranche: $1,400,000.00 payable upon successful completion of
the Feasibility Phase and Steering Committee approval to advance to the
Optimization Phase;
(c) Third Tranche: $1,400,000.00 payable upon successful completion of
the Optimization Phase and Steering Committee approval to advance to the
Preclinical Validation Phase.

7.3 NanoMed In-Kind Contributions. NanoMed shall contribute in-kind access
to its nanoparticle formulation and characterization equipment (including
dynamic light scattering instruments, electron microscopy facilities, and
high-performance liquid chromatography systems), and dedicated scientific
personnel time at a level equivalent to two (2) full-time research
scientists throughout the Research Program. The estimated value of NanoMed's
in-kind contributions is one million eight hundred thousand dollars
($1,800,000.00).

7.4 BioGenesis In-Kind Contributions. BioGenesis shall contribute dedicated
laboratory facilities and research space (approximately 2,000 square feet of
BSL-2 laboratory space), core facility access (flow cytometry, cell culture,
and animal facility), and scientific staff time at a level equivalent to
three (3) full-time research scientists and one (1) postdoctoral fellow
throughout the Research Program. The estimated value of BioGenesis's in-kind
contributions is two million one hundred thousand dollars ($2,100,000.00).

7.5 Payment Terms. PharmaDyne shall remit each cash contribution tranche
by wire transfer to an account designated by BioGenesis within fifteen (15)
business days of the triggering event specified in Section 7.2. No other
payment terms or invoicing procedures are specified in this Collaboration
Agreement.

8. DELIVERABLES AND MILESTONES

8.1 Feasibility Report. Within six (6) months of the Effective Date,
BioGenesis shall deliver to PharmaDyne and NanoMed a comprehensive
Feasibility Report summarizing the screening and characterization results,
including in vitro binding affinity data, cytotoxicity profiles, and a
recommendation regarding lead conjugate candidate selection.

8.2 Optimization Data Package. Within eighteen (18) months of the Effective
Date, BioGenesis shall deliver to PharmaDyne and NanoMed a complete
Optimization Data Package containing all formulation optimization data,
in vivo pharmacokinetic and biodistribution results, dose-range finding
study data, and preliminary efficacy data from murine xenograft models.

8.3 Preclinical Validation Dossier. Within thirty (30) months of the
Effective Date, BioGenesis shall deliver to PharmaDyne a comprehensive
Preclinical Validation Dossier suitable for inclusion in an Investigational
New Drug (IND) application to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA),
including all GLP-compliant toxicology study reports, pharmacology data,
and manufacturing process descriptions.

8.4 Joint Invention Disclosures. As Joint Inventions arise during the
Research Program, the Party whose personnel first conceived the invention
shall prepare and submit a written invention disclosure to the other Parties
within thirty (30) days of conception. Each Party shall cooperate in the
preparation and prosecution of patent applications covering Joint
Inventions.

8.5 Annual Progress Reports. Within thirty (30) days of the anniversary of
the Effective Date, BioGenesis shall deliver to PharmaDyne and NanoMed an
Annual Progress Report summarizing the research activities, results,
milestone achievements, and expenditures for the preceding twelve-month
period.

9. DATA SHARING

9.1 Raw Data. All raw experimental data generated under the Research Program
shall be shared among the Parties on a quarterly basis through a secure,
shared data repository maintained by BioGenesis. Each Party shall have
unrestricted access to all raw data generated by all Parties.

9.2 Analyzed Results. Analyzed and interpreted results, including statistical
analyses, shall be shared with the Steering Committee at each quarterly
meeting and shall be included in the deliverables described in Section 8.

9.3 Database Access. BioGenesis shall provide PharmaDyne and NanoMed with
access credentials to the shared data repository within fifteen (15) days
of the Effective Date. Access shall be maintained for the duration of the
Research Program and for five (5) years following its completion or
termination.

10. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

10.1 Background IP. Each Party shall retain sole ownership of all Background
IP that it brings to the Research Program. No license to Background IP is
granted under this Collaboration Agreement except as may be separately agreed
in writing by the Party owning such Background IP.

10.2 Ownership of Joint Inventions. All Joint Inventions conceived or
reduced to practice under the Research Program shall be jointly owned by the
Parties, with ownership interests allocated as follows: BioGenesis shall own
a fifty percent (50%) undivided interest; PharmaDyne shall own a thirty-
three percent (33%) undivided interest; and NanoMed shall own a seventeen
percent (17%) undivided interest (the "Ownership Split"). The Ownership
Split reflects the relative contributions of expertise, facilities, and
personnel of each Party to the Research Program.

10.3 Patent Prosecution. BioGenesis, as Lead Research Organization, shall be
responsible for the preparation, filing, and prosecution of patent
applications covering Joint Inventions, after consultation with and approval
by the Steering Committee. Patent prosecution costs shall be shared among
the Parties in proportion to their ownership interests (50% BioGenesis,
33% PharmaDyne, 17% NanoMed).

10.4 License Grants — Internal.
    (a) Non-Exclusive Research License. Each Party shall receive a non-
    exclusive, royalty-free license to use Joint IP for internal, non-
    commercial research purposes, including the conduct of further research
    and development activities.
    (b) PharmaDyne Exclusive Commercial License. PharmaDyne shall receive an
    exclusive license to develop, manufacture, use, sell, offer for sale, and
    import products incorporating or utilizing Joint Inventions within the
    Field of Use (oncology therapeutics). This exclusive license shall be
    subject to PharmaDyne's compliance with the terms of this Collaboration
    Agreement, including timely payment of its funding obligations.
    (c) NanoMed License. NanoMed shall receive a non-exclusive license to
    develop, manufacture, and sell nanoparticle delivery systems incorporating
    Joint Inventions for use outside the Field of Use (i.e., non-oncology
    therapeutic applications).
    (d) BioGenesis License. BioGenesis shall receive a non-exclusive license
    to use Joint Inventions for academic research, educational purposes, and
    further collaborative research with third parties outside the Field of
    Use.

10.5 Sublicensing. PharmaDyne shall have the right to grant sublicenses of
its exclusive commercial license within the Field of Use to its affiliates
and third-party collaborators, subject to the condition that each sublicensee
shall be bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those
set forth in Section 11. PharmaDyne shall notify BioGenesis and NanoMed of
any sublicense granted under this Section 10.5 within thirty (30) days of
execution.

10.6 Technology Transfer. Upon successful completion of the Preclinical
Validation Phase, BioGenesis and NanoMed shall cooperate with PharmaDyne in
transferring all necessary technology, data, know-how, and materials to
PharmaDyne to enable PharmaDyne to advance the drug delivery platform into
clinical development.

11. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

11.1 Confidential Information. Each Party agrees to hold in strict confidence
all Confidential Information of the other Parties disclosed in connection
with the Research Program. Confidential Information includes, without
limitation, proprietary research data, experimental results, formulations,
manufacturing processes, business plans, financial information, regulatory
strategies, and any other non-public information concerning the Research
Program or any Party's business operations.

11.2 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section
11 shall survive the expiration or termination of this Collaboration
Agreement for a period of ten (10) years. The Parties acknowledge that this
extended confidentiality period is appropriate given the sensitive nature of
biotechnology research data, the long development timelines characteristic
of oncology therapeutics, and the potential competitive value of preclinical
research results.

11.3 Biotechnology Data. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, all
preclinical research data, including raw experimental data, analyzed results,
formulation data, and biological characterization data, shall be treated as
Confidential Information of the Party or Parties that generated such data,
subject to the sharing obligations set forth in Section 9.

11.4 Patient Information. In the event that the Research Program generates
any data derived from human subjects (which is not anticipated under the
current Research Plan), such data shall be handled in accordance with all
applicable federal and state privacy laws, including the Health Insurance
Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and any applicable institutional
review board (IRB) protocols.

12. REGULATORY INTERACTIONS

12.1 FDA Communications. PharmaDyne shall be responsible for all
communications with the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) regarding
the regulatory development pathway for the drug delivery platform, including
pre-IND meetings, IND submissions, and any other regulatory interactions.
PharmaDyne shall keep the Steering Committee informed of all material FDA
communications.

12.2 IND-Enabling Studies. The Preclinical Validation Phase of the Research
Program shall include all IND-enabling studies necessary to support an IND
application to the FDA, including GLP-compliant toxicology studies,
pharmacology studies, and manufacturing process development, as described in
the Research Plan.

14. TERMINATION

14.1 Termination Without Cause. Any Party may terminate this Collaboration
Agreement without cause upon one hundred twenty (120) calendar days' prior
written notice to the other Parties.

14.2 Termination for Bankruptcy. This Collaboration Agreement shall terminate
immediately upon written notice by any Party if any other Party files a
petition in bankruptcy, is adjudicated as bankrupt, makes an assignment for
the benefit of creditors, or becomes the subject of any proceeding under
any bankruptcy or insolvency law, whether domestic or foreign.

14.3 Termination for Scientific Infeasibility. Any Party may terminate this
Collaboration Agreement if the Research Program proves to be scientifically
infeasible, as determined by the Steering Committee following a Phase Gate
Review at which the go/no-go criteria for the then-current Phase have not
been met. Such termination shall require sixty (60) calendar days' prior
written notice and a written justification setting forth the scientific basis
for the determination of infeasibility.

14.4 Termination for Cause. Any Party may terminate this Collaboration
Agreement for cause if another Party commits a material breach of any
provision of this Collaboration Agreement and fails to cure such breach
within sixty (60) calendar days after receiving written notice specifying
the nature of the breach.

14.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this
Collaboration Agreement, the provisions of Sections 10 (Intellectual
Property), 11 (Confidential Information), and 17 (Governing Law) shall
survive. Each Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential
Information of the other Parties, except that each Party may retain one (1)
copy for archival and legal compliance purposes.

15. LIABILITY

15.1 Limitation of Liability. Each Party shall be liable only for its own
negligent acts or omissions, and shall not be liable for the negligent acts
or omissions of any other Party or the personnel, agents, or contractors
of any other Party. Nothing in this Collaboration Agreement shall be
construed to create any liability of one Party for the acts or omissions of
another Party.

15.2 No Aggregate Cap. The Parties have not agreed to an aggregate cap on
liability under this Collaboration Agreement. Each Party's liability shall
be determined in accordance with applicable law based on the specific facts
and circumstances of any claim.

16. FORCE MAJEURE

16.1 Definition. "Force Majeure" means any event or circumstance beyond the
reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts
of God, natural disasters, pandemics, epidemics, government actions, war,
terrorism, civil unrest, laboratory accidents, equipment failures, supply
chain disruptions, and inability to obtain necessary materials or reagents.

16.2 Effect. If a Party is unable to perform its obligations under this
Collaboration Agreement due to a Force Majeure event, such Party shall
promptly notify the other Parties in writing and shall be excused from
performance for the duration of the Force Majeure event, provided that the
affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects
of the Force Majeure event and to resume performance as soon as reasonably
practicable.

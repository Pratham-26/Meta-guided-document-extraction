EVENT MANAGEMENT AND PRODUCTION SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Event Management and Production Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number EVT-2025-0612, is entered into as of June 1, 2025 (the
"Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the pharmaceutical and life sciences industry in the United States
operates within one of the most heavily regulated commercial environments in the
global economy, with promotional activities, medical education programs, and
professional engagement events subject to extensive oversight by the Food and Drug
Administration (FDA), the Office of Inspector General (OIG), and various state
and federal regulatory bodies that impose strict requirements on the content,
format, and delivery of scientific and promotional communications directed at
healthcare professionals and patients;

WHEREAS, the pharmaceutical conference and medical education event industry has
undergone a profound transformation over the past decade, evolving from
traditional in-person congresses and symposia to sophisticated multi-format
engagement platforms encompassing live general sessions, parallel breakout tracks,
simulated-live broadcasts, fully virtual attendance experiences, and hybrid models
that seamlessly integrate in-person and remote participation while maintaining
regulatory compliance across all modalities and jurisdictions;

WHEREAS, the COVID-19 pandemic and its aftermath fundamentally reshaped the
medical event landscape, accelerating the adoption of virtual and hybrid event
technologies, establishing new expectations for digital engagement and
interactivity, and creating permanent shifts in how pharmaceutical companies,
medical societies, and healthcare organizations plan, produce, and measure the
impact of their educational and promotional events;

WHEREAS, regulatory frameworks governing pharmaceutical promotional events
continue to evolve, with the FDA's Office of Prescription Drug Promotion (OPDP),
the PhRMA Code on Interactions with Healthcare Professionals, the AdvaMed Code
of Ethics for Interactions with Healthcare Professionals, and the Sunshine Act
transparency reporting requirements collectively establishing a complex compliance
environment that demands specialized expertise in event design, content
development, and attendee management to ensure that all event activities conform
to applicable promotional guidelines, fair balance requirements, and adverse event
reporting obligations;

WHEREAS, the annual medical conference has become a cornerstone of pharmaceutical
marketing and medical affairs strategy, serving simultaneously as a platform for
continuing medical education (CME) delivery, scientific data presentation, key
opinion leader (KOL) engagement, product awareness building, peer-to-peer
networking, and competitive intelligence gathering, with the complexity and scale
of such events requiring professional event management expertise encompassing
venue negotiation, audiovisual production, registration technology, speaker
coordination, sponsor activation, and comprehensive post-event analytics;

WHEREAS, Catalyst Event Productions Inc. is a leading full-service event
management and production company with extensive experience in the pharmaceutical,
biotechnology, and medical device sectors, specializing in the planning and
execution of regulatory-compliant medical conferences, advisory board meetings,
product launch events, and scientific symposia, with a demonstrated track record
of producing events that achieve measurable engagement metrics while maintaining
full compliance with applicable industry codes and regulatory requirements;

WHEREAS, Pinnacle Pharmaceuticals Corp. is a specialty pharmaceutical company
focused on the development and commercialization of therapies for rare diseases
and specialty conditions, and has determined that professional event management
and production services are required for the planning and execution of its annual
medical conference, which is expected to attract approximately two thousand five
hundred (2,500) in-person attendees and five thousand (5,000) virtual attendees;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements
set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and
sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1.1 Service Provider. Catalyst Event Productions Inc., a corporation duly organized

1.2 Client. Pinnacle Pharmaceuticals Corp., a corporation duly organized and
existing under the laws of the State of New Jersey, with its principal offices

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents and warrants that they
have the full legal authority to bind their respective organization to the terms

duly authorized by all necessary corporate approvals.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "AVL" or "Audiovisual List" means a comprehensive inventory of all
audiovisual, lighting, staging, and technical equipment required for the
production of the Event, including specifications, quantities, placement
diagrams, and interconnection schematics.

2.2 "Breakout Session" means a smaller-format educational or scientific
presentation conducted in a secondary meeting room separate from the General
Session, typically accommodating between twenty-five (25) and two hundred (200)
attendees and focused on a specific therapeutic area, clinical data set, or
professional development topic.

2.3 "CME" or "Continuing Medical Education" means educational activities that
have been developed and delivered in accordance with the accreditation standards
and requirements of the Accreditation Council for Continuing Medical Education
(ACCME) or an equivalent accrediting body recognized by applicable state and
federal regulatory authorities.

2.4 "General Session" or "Plenary" means the primary large-format presentation
or ceremony held in the main hall or keynote theater of the Event venue,
typically attended by all in-person attendees and simultaneously broadcast to
virtual attendees, and featuring keynote addresses, company presentations,
or major scientific data disclosures.

2.5 "Green Room" means a designated backstage or pre-function area provided
for speakers, presenters, and VIP guests to prepare for their presentations,
conduct final rehearsals, review technical configurations, and receive
production cue instructions prior to their scheduled stage time.

2.6 "Hybrid Event" means an Event format that combines in-person attendance at
a physical venue with simultaneous virtual participation through a digital
streaming and engagement platform, enabling remote attendees to view live
presentations, participate in interactive sessions, engage with exhibitors, and
network with other attendees in a virtual environment.

2.7 "Keynote" means a featured presentation by a principal speaker, typically
scheduled as the opening or closing address of the Event, designed to establish
the thematic framework, communicate strategic priorities, or present significant
scientific or clinical data to the full assembly of attendees.

2.8 "Simulive" or "Simulated Live" means a pre-recorded presentation that is
broadcast at a scheduled time as if it were occurring live, often including a
live question-and-answer component moderated by a facilitator, designed to
provide a polished presentation experience while accommodating speaker scheduling
constraints or time zone differences.

2.9 "Virtual Platform" means the web-based or application-based digital
environment through which virtual attendees access Event content, including live
streaming video, on-demand session recordings, interactive chat and polling
features, virtual exhibition spaces, and networking functionality.

2.10 "Rider" or "Technical Rider" means a detailed document specifying the
technical, staging, audiovisual, and production requirements for each session,
speaker, or performance component of the Event, including stage layout, lighting
cues, sound configurations, video inputs, and teleprompter specifications.

2.11 "Strike" means the process of dismantling, removing, and loading out all
production equipment, scenic elements, signage, furnishings, and materials from
the Event venue following the conclusion of the Event, including the restoration
of the venue to its pre-Event condition as required by the venue contract.

2.12 "Load-In" means the process of delivering, staging, and installing all
production equipment, scenic elements, signage, furnishings, and materials at
the Event venue prior to the commencement of the Event, including the
construction and configuration of stages, lighting rigs, audio systems, video
walls, and registration areas.

2.13 "Exhibitor Kit" means a package of informational materials, logistical
instructions, and compliance guidelines provided to each sponsor or exhibitor
participating in the Event, including booth specifications, shipping and freight
instructions, setup and teardown schedules, lead retrieval system access, and
applicable regulatory compliance reminders.

2.14 "Sponsorship Tier" means a classification level of Event sponsorship that
corresponds to a defined set of benefits, entitlements, visibility placements,
and activation opportunities, with each tier carrying a specific sponsorship fee
and a specified scope of brand exposure and engagement access.

2.15 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by
either party to the other in connection with this Agreement, including but not
limited to event strategies, attendee data, speaker information, sponsorship
terms, financial arrangements, proprietary production methodologies, and
unpublished clinical or scientific data.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive event
management and production services for the Client's annual medical conference
(the "Event"), scheduled to take place in March 2026. The services shall include
but not be limited to the following service categories:

    (a) Venue Selection and Negotiation. The Service Provider shall identify,
    evaluate, and recommend suitable venue options for the Event based on the
    Client's requirements for meeting space capacity, breakout room
    availability, exhibition hall dimensions, audiovisual infrastructure,
    accessibility compliance, and geographic convenience for the anticipated
    attendee population. Upon Client approval of a venue, the Service Provider
    shall negotiate and execute the venue contract on behalf of the Client,
    including room block management, food and beverage minimums, and all
    ancillary venue agreements.

    (b) Stage and Audiovisual Production. The Service Provider shall design,
    fabricate, and install all staging, scenic elements, lighting, sound
    reinforcement, video projection, LED wall displays, camera systems, and
    associated technical infrastructure required for the Event, including the
    main general session stage, all breakout session rooms, the exhibition hall,
    and all common areas requiring audiovisual support.

    (c) Attendee Registration Platform. The Service Provider shall implement
    and manage a comprehensive attendee registration platform supporting both
    in-person and virtual registration, including online registration forms,
    payment processing, confirmation communications, badge printing for
    in-person attendees, and mobile application access for virtual attendees.

    (d) Virtual and Hybrid Broadcast Capabilities. The Service Provider shall
    design and implement a virtual and hybrid broadcast infrastructure enabling
    simultaneous live streaming of all general sessions and selected breakout
    sessions to virtual attendees, including multi-camera video production,
    professional audio mixing, real-time captioning, interactive engagement
    tools (polling, Q&A, chat), and on-demand content access following the
    Event.

    (e) Speaker Management. The Service Provider shall coordinate all aspects
    of speaker logistics, including invitation management, travel and
    accommodation arrangements, audiovisual requirement collection, rehearsal
    scheduling, slide deck collection and formatting, teleprompter preparation,
    green room management, and on-site speaker support throughout the Event.

    (f) Catering Coordination. The Service Provider shall coordinate all
    catering and hospitality services for the Event, including meal service
    planning, dietary accommodation management, break refreshment scheduling,
    reception and networking event food and beverage, and compliance with venue
    food service policies and applicable health regulations.

    (g) Sponsor Activation. The Service Provider shall manage the design,
    production, and installation of all sponsor recognition and activation
    elements, including booth and exhibit space allocation, signage and banner
    placement, digital sponsor listings, lead retrieval system configuration,
    and on-site sponsor liaison services.

    (h) Exhibition Hall Management. The Service Provider shall manage all
    aspects of the exhibition hall, including floor plan design, booth
    assignment, utility distribution (power, internet, compressed air), aisle
    signage, traffic flow management, security coordination, and exhibitor
    support services throughout the Event.

    (i) On-Site Event Staffing. The Service Provider shall provide and manage
    all on-site event staffing requirements, including registration desk
    personnel, room monitors, technical operators, stage managers, production
    assistants, and general event support staff.

    (j) Post-Event Analytics. The Service Provider shall compile and deliver
    a comprehensive post-event analytics report including registration and
    attendance metrics, session attendance data, virtual engagement statistics,
    exhibitor lead retrieval summaries, attendee satisfaction survey results,
    and financial reconciliation of Event expenditures.

4. VENUE SELECTION

4.1 Venue Requirements. The Service Provider shall identify venue options that
meet the following minimum requirements: (i) a main hall or ballroom capable
of seating at least three thousand (3,000) attendees theater-style; (ii) a
minimum of eight (8) breakout rooms accommodating between fifty (50) and three
hundred (300) attendees each; (iii) an exhibition hall of at least twenty
thousand (20,000) square feet; (iv) ADA-compliant accessibility throughout all
Event spaces; (v) high-speed internet connectivity with minimum 1 Gbps
dedicated bandwidth; and (vi) sufficient loading dock and freight elevator
access for production load-in and strike operations.

4.2 Negotiation Authority. The Service Provider is authorized to negotiate
venue contract terms on behalf of the Client, provided that any commitment
exceeding the approved production budget requires prior written Client approval.

4.3 ADA Compliance. The Service Provider shall ensure that all venue spaces,
staging configurations, seating arrangements, and attendee services comply with
the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and all applicable accessibility
regulations, including but not limited to wheelchair-accessible seating, sign
language interpretation upon request, assistive listening devices, and
accessible registration and exhibition areas.

5. AUDIOVISUAL PRODUCTION

5.1 Main Stage. The Service Provider shall design and install a main stage
production package including but not limited to a scenic stage set, LED video
wall (minimum 16:9 aspect ratio), front projection systems for speaker
support content, professional lighting rig with wash, spot, and effects
fixtures, line array audio system with stage monitors, confidence monitors,
teleprompter systems, and a multi-camera video production suite with
directional switching, graphics insertion, and recording capability.

5.2 Breakout Rooms. Each breakout room shall be equipped with a projection or
flat-panel display system, audio reinforcement with wireless lavalier and
handheld microphones, a confidence monitor, and lighting appropriate for
presentation environments.

5.3 Webcast Infrastructure. The Service Provider shall deploy a webcast
infrastructure supporting simultaneous live streaming of the general session
and up to four (4) breakout sessions, with redundant internet connectivity,
professional broadcast encoding, and integration with the virtual platform.

6. REGISTRATION AND ATTENDEE MANAGEMENT

6.1 Registration Platform. The Service Provider shall implement an online
registration platform supporting multi-track registration (in-person, virtual,
or hybrid), early bird and standard pricing tiers, group registration
discounts, and integration with the Event mobile application.

6.2 Badge Production. The Service Provider shall design, print, and distribute
attendee badges differentiated by attendee category (attendee, speaker,
exhibitor, media, staff), with appropriate access control indicators and sponsor
recognition elements.

6.3 Mobile Application. The Service Provider shall configure and deploy a
mobile application for the Event providing attendees with access to the event
schedule, session descriptions, speaker biographies, venue maps, interactive
floor plans, networking features, and real-time event notifications.

6.4 Accessibility. The registration platform and mobile application shall
comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards, including screen reader
compatibility, keyboard navigation, and appropriate color contrast ratios.

7. VIRTUAL AND HYBRID CAPABILITIES

7.1 Streaming Platform. The Service Provider shall implement a virtual event
platform providing live streaming of all general sessions and designated
breakout sessions, with high-definition video quality, professional audio,
real-time closed captioning, and simulive playback capability.

7.2 Engagement Tools. The virtual platform shall include interactive engagement
tools including live polling, moderated Q&A, session chat, virtual networking
lounges, and gamification elements designed to maximize virtual attendee
participation and satisfaction.

7.3 On-Demand Content. Following the Event, all recorded sessions shall be
made available on-demand through the virtual platform for a period of ninety
(90) days, with searchable session catalogs and viewer analytics.

8. SPEAKER MANAGEMENT

8.1 Speaker Coordination. The Service Provider shall serve as the primary
point of contact for all speakers, managing invitation logistics, travel
arrangements, hotel accommodations, ground transportation, and expense
reimbursement processing.

8.2 AV Requirements. The Service Provider shall collect, review, and implement
each speaker's audiovisual requirements, including slide deck formatting,
video playback specifications, audio requirements, and any specialized
technical needs.

8.3 Rehearsal Schedule. The Service Provider shall develop and manage a
comprehensive speaker rehearsal schedule, including technical walkthroughs,
audio checks, lighting cues, and teleprompter rehearsals, to be conducted
prior to the commencement of the Event.

9. CATERING AND HOSPITALITY

9.1 Dietary Requirements. The Service Provider shall coordinate with the venue
catering department to ensure that all meals and refreshments accommodate
common dietary requirements including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, kosher,
halal, and common food allergies, with clearly labeled food stations and
allergen information cards.

9.2 Service Levels. All catering services shall be provided at a level
consistent with a premium pharmaceutical industry event, including full-service
staffed buffet stations, barista stations for coffee service, and themed
reception service for networking events.

9.3 Bar Service. Where alcoholic beverages are served at Event functions, the
Service Provider shall ensure compliance with all applicable state and local
liquor licensing requirements and shall implement responsible service practices
including drink limit controls and non-alcoholic alternative availability.

10. SPONSOR AND EXHIBITOR MANAGEMENT

10.1 Booth Design. The Service Provider shall provide standardized booth design
templates for each Sponsorship Tier and shall coordinate with individual
sponsors on custom booth designs, ensuring compliance with venue regulations,
fire safety codes, and applicable pharmaceutical industry promotional guidelines.

10.2 Lead Retrieval. The Service Provider shall implement a digital lead
retrieval system enabling exhibitors to capture attendee contact information
through badge scanning, with real-time data synchronization to exhibitor dashboards.

10.3 Activation Rules. All sponsor and exhibitor activities within the
exhibition hall and common areas shall comply with the Client's promotional
guidelines, including restrictions on off-label promotion, adverse event
reporting procedures, and compliance with the PhRMA Code and applicable FDA
regulations.

11. HEALTH AND SAFETY

11.1 Medical Response. The Service Provider shall ensure the availability of
on-site medical response personnel and equipment throughout the duration of the
Event, including automated external defibrillators (AEDs) and first aid
stations staffed by certified medical professionals.

11.2 Emergency Procedures. The Service Provider shall develop and communicate
comprehensive emergency response procedures, including evacuation plans,
assembly points, emergency contact protocols, and coordination with local
emergency services.

11.3 COVID Protocols. The Service Provider shall implement health and safety
protocols in accordance with current CDC guidance and applicable state and
local health department requirements, including enhanced sanitation, hand
hygiene stations, and ventilation optimization, and shall adjust protocols as
conditions warrant.

12. FDA COMPLIANCE

12.1 Promotional Guidelines. The Service Provider acknowledges that the Event
is subject to FDA regulations governing pharmaceutical promotional activities
and shall ensure that all Event content, signage, materials, and activities
conform to the Client's internal promotional review procedures and applicable
regulatory requirements.

12.2 Off-Label Restrictions. The Service Provider shall implement controls to
prevent the presentation or dissemination of off-label promotional content
within Event sessions, exhibition spaces, and associated materials, and shall
immediately escalate any identified compliance concerns to the Client's
regulatory affairs department.

12.3 Adverse Event Reporting. The Service Provider shall cooperate with the
Client in implementing procedures for the identification, documentation, and
reporting of adverse events or product complaints that may be disclosed during
the Event, in accordance with FDA 21 CFR Part 312 and 21 CFR Part 314
reporting requirements.

13. TERM

13.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and
shall continue for a period of twelve (12) months, concluding on May 31, 2026
(the "Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 17. The
Term encompasses the full planning, production, execution, and post-event
phases for a single conference event scheduled for March 2026.

13.2 No Auto-Renewal. This Agreement shall not automatically renew upon
expiration. Any extension or new engagement following the expiration of the
Term shall require the execution of a new agreement or written amendment signed
by both parties.

13.3 Effect of Termination. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall
not affect any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive
termination, including without limitation Sections 15 (Intellectual Property),
16 (Confidentiality), 18 (Liability), 19 (Insurance), 20 (Dispute Resolution),
and 21 (Governing Law).

14. COMPENSATION

14.1 Event Management Fee. The Client shall pay the Service Provider a fixed
event management fee of ninety-five thousand dollars ($95,000.00) for all
planning, coordination, and on-site management services described in this
Agreement.

14.2 Production Budget. In addition to the Event Management Fee, the Client
shall approve a production budget of up to four hundred fifty thousand dollars
($450,000.00) for all production-related expenditures including venue deposits,
audiovisual equipment rental and labor, scenic fabrication, staging, lighting,
sound, video, webcast infrastructure, and related production costs. All
production expenditures shall be subject to Client approval on a per-line-item
basis, and no individual line item expenditure shall exceed ten thousand dollars
($10,000.00) without prior written Client approval. The Service Provider shall
provide detailed production budget estimates for Client review and approval
prior to any expenditure commitment.

14.3 Virtual Platform Licensing. The Client shall pay the Service Provider
twenty-eight thousand dollars ($28,000.00) for virtual platform licensing,
configuration, and technical support, encompassing the live Event period and
the ninety-day on-demand content access period.

14.4 Travel and Accommodation. The Client shall reimburse the Service Provider
for reasonable travel and accommodation expenses incurred by Service Provider
personnel in connection with the Event, at actual cost, subject to a maximum
cap of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) for all travel and
accommodation expenses incurred during the Term. All travel expenses shall be
supported by itemized receipts and shall be consistent with the Service
Provider's standard travel and expense policy.

14.5 Payment Schedule. Payments under this Agreement shall be made according
to the following schedule: (i) forty percent (40%) of the Event Management Fee
($38,000.00) shall be payable within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date;
(ii) thirty percent (30%) of the Event Management Fee ($28,500.00) shall be
payable at ninety (90) days from the Effective Date; and (iii) the remaining
thirty percent (30%) of the Event Management Fee ($28,500.00) shall be payable
within thirty (30) days following the conclusion of the Event. Production
budget expenditures, virtual platform licensing fees, and travel reimbursements
shall be invoiced separately and shall be payable within thirty (30) days of
receipt of invoice.

15. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

15.1 Client-Owned IP. The Client shall own all right, title, and interest in
all event branding materials, logos, trademarks, content recordings (including
video, audio, and photographic recordings of presentations and sessions),
attendee registration data, attendee contact information, survey responses,
and all other data collected in connection with the Event (collectively,
"Client Materials").

15.2 Service Provider-Owned IP. The Service Provider shall retain all right,
title, and interest in all production designs, show files, technical
configurations, scenic designs, lighting plots, audio system designs, stage
management documentation, event production templates, and proprietary
production methodologies developed or utilized in connection with the Event
(collectively, "Provider Materials").

15.3 License. Each party grants the other a limited, non-exclusive, non-
transferable license to use its respective intellectual property materials
solely for purposes related to the planning, execution, and post-event
activities under this Agreement.

16. CONFIDENTIALITY

16.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all
Confidential Information of the other party disclosed in connection with this
Agreement. Neither party shall disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal
Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent
of the disclosing party, except as required by law, regulation, or legal
process.

16.2 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section 16
shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of
three (3) years.

17. TERMINATION

17.1 General Termination. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon sixty
(60) calendar days' prior written notice to the other party.

17.2 Client Termination. The Client may terminate this Agreement at any time
upon sixty (60) calendar days' prior written notice; provided, however, that
upon such termination the Client shall be obligated to pay the Service Provider
for all non-cancellable commitments entered into on behalf of the Client prior
to the effective date of termination, including but not limited to venue
deposits, non-refundable equipment rentals, and confirmed vendor contracts.

17.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause
if the other party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement
and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving
written notice specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

LAW, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT,

BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF BUSINESS
OPPORTUNITY, BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, OR LOSS OF GOODWILL, ARISING OUT OF OR
RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE PERFORMANCE OF SERVICES HEREUNDER, REGARDLESS
OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES ARE BASED ON CONTRACT, TORT, STRICT LIABILITY, ANY
OTHER LEGAL THEORY, AND EVEN IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY
OF SUCH DAMAGES.

18.2 Liability Cap. The Service Provider's total aggregate liability for all
claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed the total
event management fee of ninety-five thousand dollars ($95,000), regardless of
the form of action or legal theory.

19. INSURANCE

19.1 General Liability. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the Service
Provider shall maintain commercial general liability insurance in an amount not
less than two million dollars ($2,000,000) per occurrence, covering bodily
injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury arising from the
Service Provider's operations in connection with the Event.

19.2 Professional Liability. The Service Provider shall maintain professional
liability (errors and omissions) insurance in an amount not less than one
million dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence, covering claims arising from
negligent acts, errors, or omissions in the provision of event management and
production services under this Agreement.

19.3 Evidence of Coverage. The Service Provider shall provide certificates of
insurance evidencing the required coverage to the Client upon execution of this
Agreement and upon each renewal of such policies.

20. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

20.1 Negotiation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to
this Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve such dispute through
good faith negotiations between senior executives of each party for a period of
not less than thirty (30) calendar days.

20.2 Arbitration. If the parties are unable to resolve a dispute through
negotiation, such dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered
by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in accordance with its Commercial
Arbitration Rules then in effect. The arbitration shall be conducted in Las
Vegas, Nevada. The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding.

23.6 Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be

23.8 Force Majeure. Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the
performance of its obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such

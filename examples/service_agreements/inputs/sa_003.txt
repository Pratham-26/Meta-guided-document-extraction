SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Software Development Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of April 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below. This Agreement governs the engagement of professional software development services for the design, engineering, testing, and deployment of a consumer-facing mobile banking application platform.

WHEREAS, the global financial technology sector has undergone transformative change in recent years, with mobile banking adoption rates increasing by over forty percent annually across developed and emerging markets alike, driven by ubiquitous smartphone penetration, the proliferation of high-speed mobile networks, and a generational shift in consumer preferences toward digital-first financial interactions, such that traditional branch-based banking models are rapidly becoming supplementary rather than primary channels for retail customer engagement;

WHEREAS, the emergence of neobank and challenger bank platforms across the United Kingdom, the European Economic Area, and other major financial markets has fundamentally altered competitive dynamics within the retail banking sector, compelling established financial institutions such as the Client to invest substantially in digital transformation initiatives in order to retain existing customers, attract digitally native demographic segments, and maintain market share against agile, technology-native competitors unencumbered by legacy infrastructure and legacy operational costs;

WHEREAS, the regulatory landscape governing digital financial services has evolved significantly in recent years, most notably through the implementation of the Revised Payment Services Directive (PSD2) within the European Economic Area, which mandates open banking interfaces and third-party access to payment account data through regulated application programming interfaces, thereby creating both opportunities and obligations for financial institutions seeking to leverage ecosystem partnerships while maintaining robust security and compliance postures;

WHEREAS, the Open Banking Implementation Entity and equivalent regulatory frameworks have established technical standards for application programming interface design, data sharing, and customer consent management that require financial institutions to implement secure, scalable, and interoperable technology platforms capable of supporting account information services, payment initiation services, and confirmation of funds services in compliance with applicable regulatory mandates and industry best practices;

WHEREAS, the Client, as a regulated financial institution operating within the jurisdiction of England and Wales, is subject to oversight by the Financial Conduct Authority and must ensure that any technology solutions deployed in connection with its retail banking operations comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards, including but not limited to the Payment Services Regulations 2017, the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679, the UK GDPR as retained in domestic law, the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard;

WHEREAS, the increasing sophistication and frequency of cyber security threats targeting financial services institutions, including distributed denial of service attacks, credential stuffing, social engineering, supply chain compromise, and advanced persistent threats, necessitates the implementation of comprehensive security architectures incorporating defense-in-depth strategies, real-time threat monitoring, automated fraud detection algorithms, and rigorous penetration testing protocols to safeguard customer assets, sensitive financial data, and institutional reputation;

WHEREAS, the Service Provider possesses specialized expertise in the architecture, development, and delivery of mobile application solutions tailored to the financial services industry, with demonstrated experience in building platforms that meet the rigorous security, reliability, and performance standards required of institutions operating under regulatory supervision, and with a proven track record of successful engagements involving the design and delivery of consumer-facing financial technology products for regulated entities in multiple jurisdictions;

WHEREAS, the parties have engaged in extensive preliminary discussions, due diligence processes, and commercial negotiations and have determined that their respective capabilities, strategic objectives, and operational requirements are sufficiently aligned to warrant the formal engagement contemplated by this Agreement, subject to the terms, conditions, representations, warranties, and limitations set forth herein; and

SECTION 3A — DEVELOPMENT METHODOLOGY

3A.1 Agile Framework. The Service Provider shall execute the development phases of the Project in accordance with agile software development principles, specifically adopting the Scrum framework as the primary project management methodology. The development effort shall be organized into a series of iterative sprint cycles, each of two (2) calendar weeks' duration, during which a defined increment of potentially shippable product functionality shall be designed, developed, tested, and demonstrated to the Client. The agile approach is intended to promote adaptive planning, early delivery, continuous improvement, and rapid response to change throughout the Project lifecycle.

3A.2 Sprint Planning. At the commencement of each sprint cycle, the Service Provider shall conduct a sprint planning ceremony attended by the development team, the Scrum Master, the product owner, and such Client representatives as the Client may designate. During sprint planning, the team shall review the prioritized product backlog, estimate the effort required for each User Story using the Service Provider's standard estimation methodology, select a subset of User Stories that the team commits to completing during the upcoming sprint, and decompose each selected User Story into discrete technical tasks with clear acceptance criteria and ownership assignments.

3A.3 Daily Coordination. The Service Provider shall conduct daily stand-up coordination sessions during each sprint, lasting no more than fifteen (15) minutes, during which each member of the development team shall briefly report on: (a) the tasks completed since the previous session; (b) the tasks planned for the current day; and (c) any impediments or blockers that are preventing progress. The Client shall be entitled to observe these daily sessions upon reasonable notice, and a summary of impediments shall be communicated to the Client's designated representative within one (1) business day of identification.

3A.4 Sprint Review and Retrospective. At the conclusion of each sprint cycle, the Service Provider shall conduct a sprint review demonstration during which the completed increment shall be presented to the Client for feedback and preliminary assessment. Following the sprint review, the Service Provider shall conduct a sprint retrospective, which is an internal team reflection exercise designed to identify process improvements, address team dynamics, and implement corrective actions for subsequent sprints. A summary of retrospective outcomes and resulting process adjustments shall be shared with the Client upon request.

3A.5 Velocity Tracking and Reporting. The Service Provider shall track team velocity, which is the aggregate measure of work completed per sprint, and shall use velocity data to refine release forecasts, inform capacity planning, and manage stakeholder expectations. Bi-weekly progress reports shall include velocity trend charts, burndown or burnup visualizations, cumulative flow diagrams, and a summary of completed, in-progress, and deferred User Stories, delivered to the Client's designated representative within three (3) business days following the conclusion of each sprint.

SECTION 3B — TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

3B.1 Microservices Architecture. The Application's back-end services shall be designed and implemented using a microservices architecture pattern, in which the overall system is decomposed into a set of loosely coupled, independently deployable Microservice components, each responsible for a bounded domain of business functionality. Inter-service communication shall be conducted over lightweight protocols, with synchronous request-response interactions mediated through the API Gateway using representational state transfer (REST) conventions and asynchronous event-driven interactions facilitated through a message broker infrastructure. This architectural approach is intended to promote service autonomy, fault isolation, independent scalability, and technology flexibility across the Application's back-end topology.

3B.2 API Design Standards. All application programming interfaces exposed by the Application's back-end services, whether internal service-to-service interfaces or external client-facing endpoints, shall be designed in accordance with RESTful design principles and the OpenAPI Specification (formerly Swagger) version 3.0 or higher. Each API endpoint shall be documented with comprehensive request and response schemas, authentication and authorization requirements, rate limiting policies, error response formats, and versioning strategies. The Service Provider shall maintain a centralized API catalog and developer documentation portal, which shall be made available to the Client and its authorized integration partners as described in the API Specification attached hereto as Exhibit J.

3B.3 Database Architecture. The Application shall employ a polyglot persistence strategy, utilizing multiple database technologies selected for their suitability to specific data storage and access patterns. Relational database management systems shall be employed for structured transactional data requiring strong consistency guarantees, document-oriented databases shall be utilized for semi-structured configuration and preference data requiring flexible schema evolution, and in-memory data stores shall be deployed for high-performance caching of frequently accessed reference data and session state. Database schema designs, migration strategies, backup procedures, and disaster recovery configurations shall be documented in the Architecture Document attached hereto as Exhibit K.

3B.4 Caching and Content Delivery. The Service Provider shall implement a multi-tier caching strategy comprising application-level caching for computed results and derived data, distributed caching for shared session state and frequently accessed reference data, and edge caching through a content delivery network for static assets including images, fonts, stylesheets, and JavaScript bundles. Cache invalidation policies, time-to-live configurations, and cache warming procedures shall be documented in the Architecture Document attached hereto as Exhibit K.

SECTION 3C — SECURITY ARCHITECTURE

3C.1 Encryption Standards. All data transmitted between the Application and the Back-End Services shall be encrypted in transit using Transport Layer Security (TLS) version 1.2 or higher, with strong cipher suites approved by the Service Provider's information security team. All sensitive data at rest, including but not limited to user credentials, personal identification information, and financial transaction data, shall be encrypted using AES-256 encryption or an equivalent encryption standard approved by the Client's information security team. The Service Provider shall implement key management practices in accordance with industry standards, including automated key rotation, secure key storage using hardware security modules where available, and documented key escrow and recovery procedures.

3C.2 Tokenization and Data Protection. The Application shall implement tokenization for sensitive payment card data, replacing primary account numbers with non-reversible tokens that maintain the format and length of the original data for processing purposes while rendering the sensitive cardholder data unusable in the event of unauthorized access. The tokenization service shall be implemented in compliance with PCI DSS requirements and shall ensure that clear-text cardholder data does not traverse or reside on the Application's servers at any point during the transaction lifecycle.

3C.3 Fraud Detection. The Application shall integrate a real-time fraud detection and prevention system capable of analyzing transaction patterns, device fingerprints, geolocation data, behavioral biometrics, and historical activity profiles to identify and flag potentially fraudulent transactions for additional verification or automated blockage. Fraud detection rules, risk scoring algorithms, and alerting thresholds shall be configurable by the Client's risk management team through an administrative interface, and all fraud detection events shall be logged for audit and compliance purposes.

3C.4 Session Management. The Application shall implement secure token-based session management using short-lived JSON Web Tokens with configurable expiration periods, secure cookie attributes including HttpOnly, Secure, and SameSite directives, and automatic session invalidation upon detection of anomalous activity including concurrent session usage from geographically distant locations, device fingerprint changes, and excessive failed authentication attempts. Session tokens shall be transmitted exclusively over encrypted channels and shall not be persisted in local storage on the client device.

SECTION 3D — TESTING STRATEGY

3D.1 Unit Testing. The Service Provider shall implement comprehensive unit testing across all components of the Application, targeting a minimum code coverage threshold of eighty percent (80%) for all back-end service components and seventy percent (70%) for all front-end application components. Unit tests shall be automated, repeatable, and integrated into the CI/CD Pipeline such that all unit tests execute automatically upon each code commit, with build failures triggered by any test regression. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with periodic code coverage reports and test execution summaries as part of the standard project reporting cadence.

3D.2 Integration Testing and System Integration Testing. The Service Provider shall conduct integration testing at the component level to verify that individual modules and services interact correctly through their defined interfaces, and shall conduct System Integration Testing (SIT) to validate that the complete Application functions correctly as an integrated system, including verification of data flows between the Application and all integrated third-party services, correct handling of error conditions and edge cases across service boundaries, and proper orchestration of multi-step business processes that span multiple microservices.

3D.3 End-to-End Testing. The Service Provider shall implement automated end-to-end testing scenarios that exercise critical user journeys through the Application from initial launch through task completion, including but not limited to the new user registration and onboarding flow, the login and multi-factor authentication flow, the domestic fund transfer flow, the bill payment flow, and the transaction history retrieval flow. End-to-end tests shall execute against a fully integrated staging environment that replicates the production Deployment Environment configuration.

3D.4 Performance Testing and Load Testing. The Service Provider shall conduct comprehensive performance testing to validate that the Application meets all specified performance requirements, including but not limited to response time thresholds for critical API endpoints under normal and peak load conditions, throughput capacity measured in transactions per second, concurrent user support, and resource utilization efficiency. Load Testing shall be conducted at progressively increasing volumes to establish the Application's performance envelope, identify scaling thresholds, and validate auto-scaling behaviors of the back-end infrastructure.

3D.5 Accessibility Testing. The Service Provider shall conduct accessibility testing of the Application in accordance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.1 at the AA conformance level, as further described in Section 3E of this Agreement. Accessibility testing shall include automated scanning using industry-standard accessibility testing tools, manual testing by personnel familiar with assistive technology usage patterns, and testing with actual screen reader software on both iOS and Android platforms.

SECTION 3E — ACCESSIBILITY COMPLIANCE

3E.1 WCAG 2.1 Conformance. The Application shall be designed, developed, and tested to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.1 at the AA conformance level, encompassing compliance with all Level A and Level AA success criteria across the four principles of accessibility: perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust. The Service Provider shall document the Application's accessibility compliance posture in the Accessibility Conformance Report, which shall be delivered as part of the Phase Three deliverables.

3E.2 Screen Reader Compatibility. The Application shall be fully compatible with the built-in screen reader technologies provided by the iOS and Android operating systems, namely VoiceOver on iOS and TalkBack on Android. All interactive elements, form fields, navigation components, status messages, and dynamic content updates shall be accessible via screen reader navigation, with appropriate accessibility labels, hints, and roles configured in accordance with the platform-specific accessibility programming interfaces. The Service Provider shall verify screen reader compatibility through manual testing conducted by personnel proficient in assistive technology usage.

3E.3 Visual Accessibility. The Application shall maintain minimum color contrast ratios of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text in accordance with WCAG 2.1 AA success criterion 1.4.3. The Application shall not rely solely on color to convey information, indicate status, or differentiate interactive elements. All images, icons, and graphical elements that convey meaningful information shall be accompanied by descriptive text alternatives. The Application shall support dynamic text sizing and respect the user's system-level text size preferences up to a minimum scaling factor of 200 percent without loss of content or functionality.

SECTION 3F — DEPLOYMENT PIPELINE

3F.1 CI/CD Infrastructure. The Service Provider shall establish and maintain a fully automated continuous integration and continuous deployment pipeline encompassing source code management, automated build orchestration, multi-stage testing execution, artifact management, and environment provisioning. The CI/CD Pipeline shall be configured to trigger automatically upon each code commit to the designated integration branch, executing the full suite of automated tests and applying quality gates that must be satisfied before a build is promoted to the next stage of the pipeline.

3F.2 Staging Environments. The Service Provider shall maintain dedicated staging environments that replicate the production Deployment Environment in terms of hardware specifications, software configurations, network topology, and data volume, for the purpose of conducting final validation testing, performance testing, and client acceptance testing prior to production deployment. Data used in the staging environment shall be synthetically generated or anonymized production data, and no live customer data shall be present in any non-production environment.

3F.3 Blue-Green Deployment. The Service Provider shall implement a blue-green deployment strategy for production releases, maintaining two identical production environments and routing user traffic between them in a controlled manner during deployment operations. New releases shall be deployed to the inactive environment, subjected to automated smoke testing and health check validation, and then promoted to active status through traffic rerouting, with the previous environment retained in standby for immediate rollback if post-deployment monitoring indicates anomalies or defects.

3F.4 Rollback Procedures. The Service Provider shall maintain documented rollback procedures for each production deployment, including automated rollback triggers based on predefined error rate thresholds, manual rollback procedures accessible to the on-call engineering team, and verification steps to confirm successful restoration of the previous stable version. Rollback procedures shall be tested and validated during the Phase Three testing activities and shall be documented in the Deployment Runbook attached hereto as Exhibit L.

SECTION 11 — SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

11.1 Encryption. All data transmitted between the Application and the Back-End Services shall be encrypted in transit using Transport Layer Security (TLS) version 1.2 or higher, with strong cipher suites approved by the Service Provider's information security team. All sensitive data at rest, including but not limited to user credentials, personal identification information, and financial transaction data, shall be encrypted using AES-256 encryption or an equivalent encryption standard approved by the Client's information security team.

11.2 Authentication. The Application shall implement user authentication mechanisms that include, at a minimum, password-based authentication with enforced complexity requirements, multi-factor authentication using one-time passwords generated by a time-based one-time password (TOTP) algorithm, and biometric authentication using device-native fingerprint or facial recognition capabilities where supported by the end user's device. Session management shall implement secure token-based authentication with configurable session timeout periods and automatic session termination upon detection of anomalous activity.

11.3 Audit Logging. The Application shall maintain comprehensive audit logs recording all security-relevant events, including but not limited to user authentication attempts (successful and unsuccessful), access to sensitive data, modification of account settings, initiation and execution of financial transactions, and administrative actions. Audit logs shall be tamper-resistant, retained for a minimum period of seven (7) years, and made available to the Client's compliance and audit teams upon request.

11.4 Vulnerability Management. The Service Provider shall conduct regular vulnerability scans and penetration tests of the Application and the Back-End Services throughout the development lifecycle and shall remediate all critical and high-severity vulnerabilities identified therein prior to deployment to the production environment.

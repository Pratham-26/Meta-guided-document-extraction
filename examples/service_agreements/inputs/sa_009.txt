LEGAL RESEARCH AND LITIGATION SUPPORT SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Legal Research and Litigation Support Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number LRS-2024-7883, is entered into as of November 1, 2024
(the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, modern commercial litigation has evolved into an increasingly complex and
resource-intensive endeavor, characterized by the exponential growth of electronic
discovery, the proliferation of multi-jurisdictional disputes, the expanding scope
of regulatory enforcement actions, and the heightened expectations of courts and
regulatory bodies regarding the thoroughness and precision of legal research and
preparation. The average cost of discovery in complex commercial litigation has
risen dramatically over the past two decades, with document review alone often
representing the single largest expenditure in large-scale matters, driven by the
vast volumes of electronically stored information (ESI) that must be collected,
processed, reviewed, and produced in accordance with applicable rules and court
orders;

WHEREAS, the legal research landscape has been transformed by advances in
technology, including the development of sophisticated legal research platforms
such as Westlaw, LexisNexis, and emerging artificial intelligence-powered research
tools that enable more efficient identification and analysis of relevant case law,
statutes, regulations, and secondary authorities. Despite these technological
advances, the interpretation and application of legal authorities to specific fact
patterns remains a fundamentally human endeavor requiring the exercise of
professional judgment, analytical rigor, and deep subject matter expertise that
cannot be fully replicated by automated systems. The integration of technology-
assisted review (TAR), predictive coding, and machine learning tools into the
document review process has improved efficiency while simultaneously raising new
questions about quality control, validation methodologies, and defensibility
standards that courts continue to refine;

WHEREAS, the preparation of litigation materials demands meticulous attention to
citation format, factual accuracy, procedural compliance, and strategic
positioning that reflects both the substantive legal merits of a party's position
and the practical considerations of advocacy before judges, magistrates, and
arbitrators who bring diverse judicial philosophies, procedural preferences, and
case management expectations to the adjudicative process. The quality of legal
research directly influences case outcomes, settlement dynamics, and the overall
cost-effectiveness of litigation, making it essential that research services meet
the highest professional standards of thoroughness, accuracy, and timeliness;

WHEREAS, privilege logging in contemporary litigation has become a complex and
often contentious process, requiring careful analysis of the attorney-client
privilege, the work product doctrine, and applicable jurisdictional variations in
privilege law. The proper identification and description of privileged materials
on privilege logs is critical to protecting sensitive communications and
confidential work product from compelled disclosure, while simultaneously
satisfying the informational requirements of courts and opposing parties under the
Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and applicable state discovery rules. Inadvertent
waiver of privilege through overbroad or imprecise logging remains a significant
risk in matters involving large document populations, necessitating rigorous review
protocols and quality assurance procedures;

WHEREAS, the coordination of expert witnesses in complex commercial litigation
requires specialized expertise in identifying, vetting, retaining, and preparing
testifying and consulting experts across diverse disciplines including economics,
accounting, engineering, environmental science, healthcare, and intellectual
property. The management of expert witness relationships involves navigating
complex ethical obligations, ensuring compliance with the requirements of Federal
Rule of Evidence 702 and the standards established by the Supreme Court in Daubert
v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals, Inc. (1993) and its progeny, including General
Electric Co. v. Joiner (1997) and Kumho Tire Co. v. Carmichael (1999), as well as
the corresponding state law equivalents adopted in jurisdictions that have not
formally embraced the Daubert framework;

WHEREAS, trial preparation represents the culmination of months or years of
litigation strategy and requires the synthesis of documentary evidence, witness
testimony, expert analysis, and legal argumentation into a coherent and persuasive
presentation tailored to the specific forum, judge, and jury involved. The
preparation of trial binders, demonstrative exhibits, witness outlines, jury
instructions, and proposed motions in limine demands meticulous organizational
skills, strategic judgment, and the ability to anticipate and counter opposing
arguments in real time during trial proceedings;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to engage in a collaborative relationship wherein the
Service Provider shall furnish comprehensive legal research and litigation support
services, enabling the Client to leverage specialized research capabilities and
litigation support resources while maintaining cost predictability and quality
consistency across its portfolio of commercial litigation matters;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements
set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and
sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Affirmative Defense" means a defense that, if established, will result in
a finding for the defendant even if the plaintiff proves all elements of the
 prima facie case, including but not limited to statute of limitations, laches,
waiver, estoppel, unclean hands, res judicata, collateral estoppel, failure to
mitigate damages, and comparative or contributory negligence.

2.2 "Brief" means any written legal argument submitted to a court, tribunal,
arbitrator, or regulatory body in connection with any matter covered by this
Agreement, including but not limited to opening briefs, answering briefs,
reply briefs, amicus curiae briefs, motion briefs, and post-trial submissions.

2.3 "Daubert Motion" means a motion to exclude or limit the testimony of an
expert witness pursuant to Federal Rule of Evidence 702 or the applicable state
law equivalent, challenging the qualifications, methodology, reliability, or
relevance of the expert's proposed testimony.

2.4 "Deposition" means the oral examination of a witness, whether party or
non-party, conducted under oath in accordance with the applicable rules of civil
procedure, including the preparation of deposition summaries, deposition
transcripts, and errata sheets.

2.5 "Discovery" means the pre-trial phase of litigation in which each party
may obtain evidence from the opposing party and third parties by means of
discovery devices including interrogatories, requests for production of
documents, requests for admissions, and depositions, as governed by the
Federal Rules of Civil Procedure or applicable state procedural rules.

2.6 "Exhibit" means any document, tangible object, demonstrative aid, chart,
diagram, photograph, video recording, audio recording, electronic file, or
other item identified, marked, and offered or admitted into evidence in any
proceeding, hearing, trial, arbitration, or regulatory investigation.

2.7 "Motion in Limine" means a pretrial motion seeking a ruling on the
admissibility of evidence, the scope of testimony, or other procedural or
evidentiary matters before such issues are presented to the trier of fact.

2.8 "Privilege Log" means a written record, prepared in accordance with the
Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and applicable state rules, identifying
documents, communications, or tangible things withheld from production on the
basis of the attorney-client privilege, the work product doctrine, or any other
applicable privilege or protection, including the date of the communication, the
author and all recipients, the general subject matter, and the basis for the
claim of privilege or protection.

2.9 "Pro Hac Vice" means the temporary admission of an attorney to practice
before a court or tribunal in a jurisdiction in which the attorney is not
regularly admitted, subject to the applicable rules and requirements of the
admitting jurisdiction.

2.10 "Request for Production" means a discovery request served pursuant to
applicable procedural rules demanding that the responding party produce for
inspection, copying, testing, or sampling designated documents, electronically
stored information, and tangible things in the responding party's possession,
custody, or control.

2.11 "Subpoena" means a legal order compelling the attendance of a witness for
testimony or the production of documents, electronically stored information,
or tangible things, issued pursuant to the applicable rules of civil procedure,
criminal procedure, or grand jury rules.

2.12 "Work Product Doctrine" means the qualified protection afforded to
materials prepared in anticipation of litigation by or for a party or its
representative, as established by Hickman v. Taylor, 329 U.S. 495 (1947), and
codified in Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 26(b)(3), protecting mental
impressions, conclusions, opinions, or legal theories of an attorney or other
representative of a party concerning the litigation.

2.13 "Matter" means any individual case, lawsuit, arbitration, regulatory
investigation, internal investigation, or other proceeding for which the Client
engages the Service Provider to perform services under this Agreement. Multiple
Matters may be active simultaneously under this Agreement.

2.14 "Research Work Product" means all memoranda, briefs, case analyses,
statutory analyses, regulatory summaries, deposition summaries, privilege logs,
trial preparation materials, and other written or electronic work product
created by the Service Provider specifically for the Client in connection with
any Matter covered by this Agreement.

2.15 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by
either party to the other in connection with this Agreement, including but not
limited to case strategy, settlement positions, privileged communications,
client confidences, business operations, financial data, and proprietary
methodologies, whether disclosed orally, in writing, by electronic
transmission, or by inspection.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 Legal Research Services. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive
legal research services for the Client's commercial litigation matters, including
but not limited to the following:

    (a) Case Law Research. Comprehensive research of relevant case law across
    federal and state jurisdictions, including identification of controlling
    authority, persuasive authority, and emerging trends in the applicable areas
    of law, utilizing Westlaw, LexisNexis, and other approved research platforms.

    (b) Statutory and Regulatory Research. Analysis of applicable federal and
    state statutes, regulations, administrative guidance, and regulatory
enforcement trends relevant to the Client's litigation matters, including
tracking of proposed and enacted legislative changes that may affect pending or
anticipated matters.

    (c) Brief Writing Support. Research, drafting, and revision support for
    dispositive motions, discovery motions, motions in limine, Daubert motions,
    summary judgment briefs, appellate briefs, and other substantive submissions,
    including the preparation of tables of authorities, statement of facts
sections, and legal argument frameworks.

    (d) Deposition Summary Preparation. Preparation of comprehensive deposition
    summaries for witness and expert depositions, including page-and-line
    references, key testimony highlights, impeachment material identification,
    and issue-tagged organization to facilitate efficient review by the Client's
    litigation team.

    (e) Document Review and Privilege Logging. First-pass and second-pass
    document review for relevance, responsiveness, and privilege, including the
    preparation of privilege logs in compliance with the requirements of the
applicable jurisdiction, coordination with the Client's privilege review team,
    and adherence to jurisdiction-specific privilege logging formats and
    standards.

    (f) Expert Witness Coordination. Assistance with the identification, vetting,
    retention, and preparation of testifying and consulting experts, including
    the preparation of expert disclosure materials, draft expert reports,
    rebuttal analyses, and Daubert challenge preparation and defense.

    (g) Trial Preparation Assistance. Comprehensive trial preparation support
    including the organization and preparation of trial binders, demonstrative
    exhibits, witness examination outlines, jury instruction proposals, proposed
    voir dire questions, exhibit lists, and witness lists.

4. RESEARCH STANDARDS AND METHODOLOGY

4.1 Research Platforms. All legal research shall be conducted using approved
research platforms, including Westlaw, LexisNexis, Bloomberg Law, and such other
platforms as may be designated by the Client from time to time. The Service
Provider shall ensure that all researchers have current subscriptions and
training on the approved platforms.

4.2 Citation Format. All research memoranda, briefs, and other written work
product shall comply with The Bluebook: A Uniform System of Citation (current
edition) or such other citation format as may be specified by the Client for a
particular matter or court.

4.3 Quality Review. All written work product shall undergo a two-tier quality
review process prior to submission to the Client. The first tier shall be
performed by a senior researcher or supervising attorney, and the second tier
shall be performed by a quality assurance reviewer designated by the Service
Provider. Quality review shall include verification of legal authority accuracy,
citation format compliance, logical coherence of analysis, and factual accuracy.

5. DOCUMENT REVIEW PROTOCOLS

5.1 Review Platforms. Document review shall be conducted on the Client's
designated document review platform or a platform mutually agreed upon by the
parties. The Service Provider shall ensure that all document reviewers are
trained and proficient in the designated platform prior to commencing review.

5.2 Coding Decisions. Document review coding decisions shall be made in
accordance with the review protocol established for each Matter, including
responsiveness determinations, privilege calls, confidentiality designations,
issue coding, and key document designations. The Service Provider shall maintain
detailed coding guidelines for each Matter and shall update such guidelines as
directed by the Client.

5.3 Privilege Calls. Privilege determinations shall be made in accordance with
the privilege review guidelines established for each Matter and shall comply
with the applicable jurisdiction's privilege logging requirements, including
the format, content, and level of detail required by the applicable rules of
civil procedure and any standing orders or case management orders specific to
the Matter.

6. DEPOSITION SUPPORT

6.1 Summary Format. Deposition summaries shall be prepared in the format
specified by the Client for each Matter, which may include chronological
summary format, topical summary format, or issue-based summary format. Each
summary shall include the deponent's name, the date of the deposition, the
page-and-line references for each summarized passage, and the key topics or
issues addressed.

6.2 Exhibit Preparation. The Service Provider shall assist in the preparation
and organization of deposition exhibits, including the designation of exhibits
for introduction, the preparation of exhibit witness binders, and the
maintenance of exhibit indexes.

6.3 Witness Files. The Service Provider shall prepare and maintain organized
witness files for each deponent and trial witness, including deposition
transcripts, prior testimony, relevant documents, impeachment materials, and
chronological summaries of the witness's involvement in the events at issue.

7. TRIAL PREPARATION

7.1 Demonstrative Exhibits. The Service Provider shall assist in the development
and preparation of demonstrative exhibits for use at trial, hearing, or
arbitration, including timelines, charts, diagrams, maps, and visual summaries
of complex data or testimony, in coordination with the Client's trial team and
any retained graphics or trial consulting firms.

7.2 Jury Instructions. The Service Provider shall assist in the research,
drafting, and organization of proposed jury instructions, including the
identification of applicable pattern jury instructions, the customization of
instructions for the specific facts and legal issues of the Matter, and the
preparation of joint and disputed instruction proposals.

7.3 Witness Outlines. The Service Provider shall assist in the preparation of
direct examination, cross-examination, and redirect examination outlines for
lay and expert witnesses, incorporating relevant deposition testimony,
documentary evidence, and legal authority.

8. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

8.1 Conflicts Check. Prior to commencing work on any Matter, the Service
Provider shall conduct a comprehensive conflicts check to determine whether any
actual or potential conflict of interest exists or may arise in connection with
the proposed engagement. The Service Provider shall maintain a centralized
conflicts database and shall update such database on a continuous basis as new
matters, clients, and adverse parties are identified.

8.2 Screening Procedures. In the event that a potential conflict of interest is
identified, the Service Provider shall implement ethical screening procedures in
accordance with the Massachusetts Rules of Professional Conduct and applicable
law, including the establishment of informational barriers, the prohibition of
communication between screened and unscreened personnel, and the maintenance of
screening records.

8.3 Reporting Requirements. The Service Provider shall promptly notify the
Client in writing of any actual or potential conflict of interest identified in
connection with any Matter covered by this Agreement, and shall not commence or
continue work on the affected Matter until the conflict has been resolved to
the satisfaction of the Client and in accordance with applicable ethical rules.

9. PRIVILEGE AND WORK PRODUCT

9.1 Attorney-Client Privilege. All Research Work Product prepared by the
Service Provider in connection with any Matter shall be prepared at the
direction and under the supervision of the Client's attorneys for the purpose
of providing legal advice or legal representation. The parties intend that all
such Research Work Product shall be protected by the attorney-client privilege
and the work product doctrine to the fullest extent permitted by applicable
law.

9.2 Joint Defense and Common Interest. In Matters involving joint defense
agreements or common interest arrangements, the Service Provider shall comply
with the applicable terms of such agreements and shall not disclose or use
Research Work Product in a manner that would waive or compromise the applicable
privilege or protection.

9.3 Waiver Issues. The Service Provider shall take all reasonable measures to
prevent the inadvertent disclosure or waiver of the attorney-client privilege
or work product protection, including the implementation of confidentiality
marking protocols, secure file handling procedures, and training programs for
all personnel with access to Research Work Product.

10. BILLING PRACTICES

10.1 Time Entry Standards. All time entries shall be recorded in increments of
one-tenth (0.1) of an hour and shall describe the specific work performed with
sufficient detail to enable the Client to understand the nature, purpose, and
value of the work. Vague or block-billed time entries that aggregate multiple
tasks or matters into a single time entry without adequate description are
prohibited.

10.2 Block Billing Prohibition. The Service Provider shall not aggregate time
for multiple tasks, matters, or activities into a single time entry. Each
distinct task or activity shall be separately identified and described in the
time entry.

10.3 Budget Guidelines. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with a
budget estimate for each Matter at the commencement of work and shall update
such estimate on a monthly basis or as otherwise agreed. In the event that
actual costs are projected to exceed the budget estimate by more than fifteen
percent (15%), the Service Provider shall notify the Client in writing prior to
incurring the excess costs and shall not proceed with the excess work without
the Client's prior written approval.

11. MATTER MANAGEMENT

11.1 Matter Intake. Upon engagement for a new Matter, the Service Provider shall
complete a matter intake form identifying the Matter name, court or forum, case
number, judge or arbitrator, key parties, key issues, anticipated services
required, budget parameters, and applicable deadlines.

11.2 Status Updates. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with written
status updates on each active Matter on a schedule to be determined by the
Client, but no less frequently than monthly. Status updates shall include a
summary of work performed, time expended, costs incurred, upcoming deadlines,
and any issues or concerns requiring the Client's attention.

11.3 Matter Closing. Upon conclusion of any Matter, the Service Provider shall
prepare and deliver a matter closing report summarizing the total services
rendered, time and costs incurred, outcomes achieved, and any outstanding
issues. All Research Work Product for the closed Matter shall be transmitted
to the Client in accordance with the intellectual property provisions of this
Agreement.

13. COMPENSATION

13.1 Hourly Rates. In consideration of the legal research and litigation support
services described in Section 3, the Client shall pay the Service Provider at
the following hourly rates for services rendered by personnel at the indicated
levels:

    (a) Senior Researchers: One hundred seventy-five dollars ($175.00) per hour.
    Senior Researchers are attorneys or research professionals with seven (7) or
    more years of relevant legal research or litigation support experience.

    (b) Junior Researchers: One hundred twenty dollars ($120.00) per hour.
    Junior Researchers are attorneys or research professionals with fewer than
    seven (7) years of relevant legal research or litigation support experience.

    (c) Document Review Personnel: Ninety-five dollars ($95.00) per hour.
    Document Review Personnel are trained reviewers responsible for first-pass
    and second-pass document review, coding, and privilege logging.

13.2 Blended Rate Cap. In any calendar month during which the total hours of
service rendered by the Service Provider across all Matters exceed two hundred
(200) hours, the effective blended hourly rate for all hours rendered during
that month (including hours exceeding the two hundred (200) hour threshold) shall
not exceed one hundred forty dollars ($140.00) per hour. The Service Provider
shall apply a credit or adjustment on the invoice for the applicable month to
ensure compliance with this blended rate cap.

13.3 Payment Terms. Monthly invoices shall be submitted by the Service Provider
on the last business day of each calendar month and shall be payable by the
Client within forty-five (45) calendar days of receipt.

13.4 Taxes. All fees payable under this Agreement are exclusive of applicable
taxes. The Client shall be responsible for any sales tax, use tax, or similar
taxes imposed on the services provided under this Agreement, except for taxes
based on the Service Provider's net income.

20. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

20.1 Good Faith Negotiation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or
relating to this Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve such
dispute through good faith negotiation between senior representatives of each
party. The parties shall meet and confer within fifteen (15) calendar days of
one party providing written notice of a dispute to the other party.

20.2 Mediation. If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation within
thirty (30) calendar days of the initial meeting, either party may submit the
dispute to non-binding mediation administered by a mutually agreed mediator in
Boston, Massachusetts. The costs of mediation shall be shared equally between
the parties.

20.3 Litigation. If mediation does not result in a resolution of the dispute
within sixty (60) calendar days of the commencement of mediation, either party
may pursue litigation, and any such litigation shall be brought exclusively in
the state or federal courts located in Suffolk County, Commonwealth of
Massachusetts.

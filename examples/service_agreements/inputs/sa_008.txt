HUMAN RESOURCES OUTSOURCING SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Human Resources Outsourcing Services Agreement (the "Agreement"), bearing reference
number HRO-2025-0022, is entered into as of January 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date"), by
and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the contemporary business landscape presents organizations of all sizes with an
increasingly complex array of human resources challenges, driven by rapid changes in
workforce demographics, evolving employee expectations, the proliferation of state and
federal employment regulations, and the accelerating adoption of technology-driven talent
management solutions. The management of human capital has emerged as a critical determinant
of organizational competitiveness, with companies that invest strategically in talent
acquisition, employee development, engagement, and retention consistently outperforming
their peers across key performance indicators including revenue growth, profitability,
innovation output, and customer satisfaction;

WHEREAS, the regulatory environment governing the employer-employee relationship in the
United States has grown substantially more complex over the past several decades, encompassing
a broad and interconnected framework of federal statutes including, without limitation, the
Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), the Americans
with Disabilities Act (ADA), Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, the Age
Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA), the Employee Retirement Income Security Act
(ERISA), the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA), the Health Insurance
Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), the Affordable Care Act (ACA, also referred
to as the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act or PPACA), the Occupational Safety
and Health Act (OSHA), and the National Labor Relations Act (NLRA), as well as a diverse
patchwork of state-specific employment laws, wage and hour regulations, leave entitlement
statutes, anti-discrimination provisions, and workplace safety requirements that vary
significantly across jurisdictions and impose substantial compliance obligations on
employers operating in multiple states;

WHEREAS, the administration of employee benefits programs has become a specialized
discipline requiring expertise in plan design, carrier negotiations, enrollment management,
claims processing, compliance with ERISA fiduciary standards, Section 125 Cafeteria Plan
administration, COBRA continuation coverage requirements, Affordable Care Act employer
shared responsibility provisions (including applicable large employer status determinations
and reporting under IRC Sections 6055 and 6056), Health Savings Account (HSA) and Flexible
Spending Account (FSA) administration, and the coordination of benefits across medical,
dental, vision, life insurance, short-term disability, long-term disability, and retirement
plan offerings including 401(k) plans subject to ERISA and Internal Revenue Code requirements;

WHEREAS, payroll processing represents a mission-critical function that demands precision,
timeliness, and compliance with complex federal, state, and local tax withholding
requirements, garnishment and levy processing obligations under federal and state law,
direct deposit and paycard administration, wage garnishment prioritization rules established
by the Consumer Credit Protection Act (CCPA), new hire reporting requirements under
federal and state law, and the accurate calculation and remittance of employment taxes
including FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) taxes, FUTA (Federal Unemployment
Tax Act) taxes, and SUTA (State Unemployment Tax Act) contributions;

WHEREAS, the effective management of the employee lifecycle — from recruitment and
onboarding through ongoing performance management, employee relations, professional
development, and eventual offboarding — requires dedicated expertise and systematic
processes that many organizations find inefficient or impractical to maintain in-house,
particularly mid-market companies experiencing rapid growth or organizational change that
place significant demands on limited internal human resources staff;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to engage in a collaborative relationship wherein the Service
Provider shall furnish comprehensive human resources outsourcing services, enabling the
Client to focus on its core business operations while ensuring professional management
of its human capital functions, regulatory compliance, and employee experience;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set
forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency
of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1.1 Service Provider. PeopleFirst HR Solutions Inc., a corporation duly organized and

"Service Provider," "PeopleFirst," or "Provider").

1.2 Client. RapidScale Tech Inc., a corporation duly organized and existing under the
laws of the State of Florida, with its principal place of business at 3200 Innovation
Circle, Orlando, FL 32801 (hereinafter referred to as "Client," "RapidScale," or
"Company").

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents and warrants that they have the
full legal authority to bind their respective organization to the terms and conditions of
this Agreement, and that the execution of this Agreement has been duly authorized by all
necessary corporate or organizational approvals.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "ACA" or "Affordable Care Act" means the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act
of 2010, as amended, and its implementing regulations, including the employer shared
responsibility provisions, the individual mandate (as applicable), and the related
reporting requirements under Internal Revenue Code Sections 6055 and 6056.

2.2 "ADA" means the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990, as amended, including the
ADA Amendments Act of 2008, and the regulations promulgated thereunder, prohibiting
discrimination against qualified individuals with disabilities in employment, public
accommodations, and government services.

2.3 "COBRA" means the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1985, as amended,
requiring group health plans to offer continuation coverage to qualified beneficiaries who
would otherwise lose coverage due to certain qualifying events.

2.4 "EEOC" means the United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, the federal
agency responsible for enforcing federal laws prohibiting employment discrimination based
on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, and
retaliation for opposing discriminatory practices or participating in discrimination
proceedings.

2.5 "Employee Handbook" means the comprehensive document maintained by or on behalf of the
Client that sets forth the policies, procedures, expectations, benefits summaries, and
rules governing the employment relationship between the Client and its employees, including
without limitation policies addressing attendance, conduct, anti-harassment, anti-
discrimination, leave of absence, electronic communications, and workplace safety.

2.6 "ERISA" means the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, as amended,
establishing minimum standards for the administration of employee benefit plans, including
fiduciary responsibilities, reporting and disclosure requirements, and participant
protections.

2.7 "FICA" means the Federal Insurance Contributions Act, requiring employers and employees
to pay Social Security and Medicare taxes through payroll withholding and employer matching
contributions.

2.8 "FLSA" means the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938, as amended, establishing minimum
wage, overtime compensation, recordkeeping, child labor standards, and defining exempt and
non-exempt employee classifications.

2.9 "FMLA" means the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, as amended, entitling eligible
employees of covered employers to take unpaid, job-protected leave for specified family and
medical reasons, with continuation of group health insurance coverage under the same terms
and conditions as if the employee had not taken leave.

2.10 "FSA" means a Flexible Spending Account established under Section 125 of the Internal
Revenue Code, allowing employees to set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for eligible out-of-
pocket health care or dependent care expenses.

2.11 "FUTA" means the Federal Unemployment Tax Act, imposing a federal payroll tax on
employers to fund unemployment compensation programs administered at the state level.

2.12 "Garnishment" means any legal process whereby a portion of an employee's wages are
withheld by the employer and paid directly to a creditor or other entity pursuant to a
court order, administrative order, or other legally enforceable instrument, including but
not limited to child support orders, tax levies, student loan garnishments, and creditor
judgments.

2.13 "HRIS" or "Human Resource Information System" means the integrated software platform
utilized by the Service Provider to manage employee data, payroll processing, benefits
administration, time and attendance tracking, compliance reporting, and other human
resources functions as contemplated by this Agreement.

2.14 "HSA" means a Health Savings Account established under Section 223 of the Internal
Revenue Code, allowing individuals covered by a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) to save
money on a pre-tax basis to pay for qualified medical expenses.

2.15 "I-9" means Employment Eligibility Verification Form (USCIS Form I-9), required by
the Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986 (IRCA) to verify the identity and employment
authorization of individuals hired for employment in the United States.

2.16 "Open Enrollment" means the annual period during which eligible employees may enroll
in, change, or cancel their elections under the Client's group health insurance plans and
other benefit programs offered through the Section 125 Cafeteria Plan, typically occurring
once per calendar year unless a qualifying life event triggers a mid-year election change.

2.17 "PPACA" means the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, Pub. L. No. 111-148,
as amended by the Health Care and Education Reconciliation Act of 2010, Pub. L. No.
111-152, collectively referred to as the Affordable Care Act or ACA.

2.18 "Section 125 Plan" means a Cafeteria Plan established pursuant to Section 125 of the
Internal Revenue Code, allowing employees to pay for qualified benefits on a pre-tax basis,
including health insurance premiums, FSA contributions, and HSA contributions through
payroll deduction.

2.19 "SUTA" means State Unemployment Tax Act contributions, which are state-level payroll
taxes imposed on employers to fund state unemployment insurance programs providing benefits
to eligible workers who become unemployed through no fault of their own.

2.20 "Workers' Compensation" means the system of state-mandated insurance providing benefits
to employees who suffer work-related injuries or illnesses, including medical expenses,
wage replacement (temporary or permanent disability benefits), vocational rehabilitation,
and death benefits, as required by the workers' compensation laws of the applicable state(s).

2.21 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by either party
to the other, whether orally, in writing, by electronic transmission, or by inspection,
including but not limited to employee personal information, compensation data, benefits
enrollment records, performance evaluations, disciplinary records, investigation files,
financial data, and proprietary methodologies.

2.22 "Covered Employees" means those employees of the Client for whom the Service Provider
is engaged to provide services under this Agreement, initially comprising approximately
four hundred fifty (450) employees as of the Effective Date, with such number subject to
increase or decrease as the Client's workforce changes over the term of this Agreement.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 Comprehensive HR Outsourcing Services. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive
human resources outsourcing services for the Client's workforce, encompassing the following
core service areas:

    (a) Payroll Processing. The Service Provider shall process the Client's payroll on
    a semi-monthly basis (or such other pay schedule as the Client may designate), including
    the calculation of gross pay, application of federal, state, and local tax withholdings,
    processing of pre-tax and post-tax deductions, administration of garnishments and
    levies in accordance with applicable priority rules, direct deposit processing for
    employees electing such payment method, generation and distribution of pay statements,
    and the timely remittance of all employment taxes and garnishment payments to the
    applicable governmental authorities and creditors.

    (b) Benefits Administration. The Service Provider shall administer the Client's
    employee benefits programs, including but not limited to medical, dental, and vision
    insurance plans, 401(k) retirement plan, Health Savings Account (HSA), Flexible
    Spending Account (FSA), group life insurance, short-term disability insurance, and
    long-term disability insurance. Benefits administration services shall include
    enrollment processing, eligibility verification, qualifying life event processing,
    COBRA administration and notification, carrier liaison and claims escalation support,
    annual Open Enrollment management, and benefits billing reconciliation.

    (c) Employee Onboarding. The Service Provider shall manage the new employee onboarding
    process, including but not limited to the preparation and distribution of offer letter
    templates and employment agreements (for Client approval), completion and processing of
    Form I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification, initiation of background checks through
    approved vendors, coordination of new hire orientation scheduling, enrollment in benefits
    programs within applicable timeframes, payroll setup and direct deposit enrollment,
    and provisioning of access to the HRIS employee self-service portal.

    (d) Employee Offboarding. The Service Provider shall manage the employee separation
    process, including but not limited to processing of termination or resignation
    documentation, calculation and processing of final pay in accordance with applicable
    state law requirements (including any statutory deadlines for final wage payment),
    coordination and scheduling of exit interviews, issuance of COBRA continuation coverage
    election notices within the timeframes required by law, benefits termination and
    conversion options notification, recovery of company property, and deprovisioning of
    HRIS and system access.

    (e) HRIS Management. The Service Provider shall manage and maintain the HRIS platform
    for the Client's workforce, including employee data entry and maintenance, organizational
    chart management, time and attendance tracking integration, absence management, data
    integrity assurance, user access controls and role-based permissions administration,
    system configuration and customization, report generation, audit trail maintenance, and
    coordination with IT support for system issues and upgrades.

    (f) Compliance Monitoring. The Service Provider shall monitor the Client's compliance
    with applicable federal, state, and local employment laws and regulations, including
    but not limited to FMLA leave tracking and administration (including designation notices,
    leave balance tracking, and return-to-work documentation), ADA reasonable accommodation
    tracking and interactive process documentation, EEO-1 Component 1 report preparation
    and filing assistance, FLSA exemption classification review and overtime tracking,
    required workplace postings updates, and new hire reporting to applicable state
    agencies.

    (g) Employee Relations Support. The Service Provider shall provide guidance and support
    to the Client on employee relations matters, including but not limited to consultation
    on disciplinary actions and progressive discipline procedures, investigation support
    for employee complaints (including harassment, discrimination, and retaliation
    allegations), preparation of written warnings, performance improvement plans, and
    other disciplinary documentation (for Client approval), mediation facilitation, and
    guidance on termination procedures to minimize legal risk.

    (h) Performance Management System Administration. The Service Provider shall administer
    the Client's performance management system within the HRIS platform, including
    configuration of performance review cycles, goal-setting templates and workflows,
    calibration session scheduling, 360-degree feedback survey administration, performance
    improvement plan tracking, and generation of performance management reports and
    analytics for Client review.

    (i) HR Analytics and Reporting. The Service Provider shall provide HR analytics and
    reporting services, including but not limited to a real-time HR dashboard accessible
    to authorized Client personnel, headcount and workforce demographic reporting, turnover
    and retention analysis, time-to-fill and recruitment metrics, compensation benchmarking
    reports, benefits utilization reports, compliance status summaries, and quarterly
    business reviews presenting key HR metrics, trends, and recommendations.

4. PAYROLL PROCESSING PROCEDURES

4.1 Pay Schedules. The Service Provider shall process the Client's payroll in accordance
with the designated pay schedule, which shall be semi-monthly unless otherwise agreed in
writing by the parties. The Client shall submit all payroll data, including hours worked,
new hires, terminations, pay rate changes, and other adjustments, to the Service Provider
by the designated payroll cutoff time, which shall be no later than three (3) business
days prior to the scheduled pay date.

4.2 Tax Withholdings. The Service Provider shall calculate and withhold all applicable
federal, state, and local income taxes from employee wages based on the employee's W-4
elections and applicable tax tables. The Service Provider shall also calculate and remit
employer FICA contributions (Social Security and Medicare), FUTA taxes, and SUTA
contributions in accordance with applicable rates and wage base limits.

4.3 Garnishment Processing. The Service Provider shall process all wage garnishments,
child support orders, tax levies, and student loan garnishments received by the Client
or Service Provider in accordance with applicable federal and state laws, including
compliance with the Consumer Credit Protection Act (CCPA) limitations on the amount of
disposable earnings subject to garnishment and the applicable priority hierarchy for
multiple garnishments.

4.4 Direct Deposit. The Service Provider shall process direct deposit payments for all
employees who have authorized electronic funds transfer. The Service Provider shall
ensure that direct deposit files are transmitted to the designated financial institutions
in a timely manner to ensure funds availability on the scheduled pay date.

5. BENEFITS ADMINISTRATION

5.1 Benefits Programs. The Service Provider shall administer the following benefits
programs on behalf of the Client:

    (a) Medical Insurance. Administration of the Client's group medical insurance
    plan(s), including enrollment, eligibility verification, qualifying life event
    processing, COBRA administration, carrier liaison, and claims escalation support.

    (b) Dental Insurance. Administration of the Client's group dental insurance plan(s),
    including enrollment, eligibility management, and carrier coordination.

    (c) Vision Insurance. Administration of the Client's group vision insurance plan(s),
    including enrollment, eligibility management, and carrier coordination.

    (d) 401(k) Retirement Plan. Administration of the Client's 401(k) plan, including
    enrollment processing, contribution amount management, loan administration support,
    hardship withdrawal processing coordination, and plan-level reporting assistance
    (plan administration and fiduciary responsibilities remain with the Client's plan
    administrator and recordkeeper).

    (e) Health Savings Account (HSA). Administration of HSA enrollment, contribution
    processing through payroll deduction, and employer contribution coordination with
    the HSA custodian.

    (f) Flexible Spending Account (FSA). Administration of FSA enrollment, contribution
    processing through payroll deduction, claims processing and reimbursement, grace
    period or carryover management, and use-it-or-lose-it deadline notifications.

    (g) Life Insurance and Disability. Administration of group life insurance, short-term
    disability, and long-term disability plans, including enrollment, beneficiary
    designation processing, premium billing reconciliation, and claims coordination.

5.2 Open Enrollment. The Service Provider shall manage the Client's annual Open Enrollment
period, including communication plan development, enrollment system configuration,
employee education materials, election processing, and confirmation statements.

6. EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING

6.1 Background Checks. The Service Provider shall coordinate background checks for all
new hires through the Client's designated background check vendor, in compliance with
applicable federal and state laws, including the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), and
any applicable ban-the-box or fair chance hiring ordinances.

6.2 I-9 Verification. The Service Provider shall complete and process Form I-9
Employment Eligibility Verification for all new hires within the timeframes required by
law (no later than the employee's first day of employment for Section 1, and no later
than the third business day after the employee's first day for Section 2), and shall
maintain I-9 records in compliance with USCIS retention requirements.

6.3 Orientation. The Service Provider shall coordinate new hire orientation scheduling
and provide orientation materials addressing benefits enrollment, HRIS self-service
portal access, company policies, and other relevant information, as directed by the
Client.

7. EMPLOYEE OFFBOARDING

7.1 Exit Interviews. The Service Provider shall coordinate and conduct exit interviews
with departing employees (either in person, by telephone, or through an online survey
platform), compile exit interview data and trend analysis, and provide periodic reports
to the Client.

7.2 COBRA Notifications. The Service Provider shall issue COBRA continuation coverage
election notices to qualified beneficiaries within the timeframes required by law
(generally within fourteen (14) days of the qualifying event, with an additional sixty
(60) days for the qualified beneficiary to elect coverage).

7.3 Final Pay. The Service Provider shall calculate and process final pay for separated
employees in accordance with applicable state law requirements, including any statutory
deadlines for final wage payment that may vary by state.

8. COMPLIANCE MONITORING

8.1 FMLA Tracking. The Service Provider shall track and administer FMLA leave for the
Client's eligible employees, including designation of qualifying leave, leave balance
tracking, intermittent leave management, fitness-for-duty certification management, and
return-to-work documentation processing.

8.2 ADA Accommodations. The Service Provider shall maintain records of ADA accommodation
requests, track the interactive process, and document accommodation determinations and
implementations, in coordination with the Client.

8.3 EEO Reporting. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in the preparation of
EEO-1 Component 1 data submissions as required by the EEOC, including workforce
demographic data compilation and report generation.

9. HRIS MANAGEMENT

9.1 Data Integrity. The Service Provider shall implement and maintain procedures to
ensure the accuracy and integrity of employee data within the HRIS platform, including
regular data audits, duplicate record identification and resolution, and data
validation protocols.

9.2 Access Controls. The Service Provider shall administer user access to the HRIS
platform based on role-based permissions, ensuring that employees, managers, and
administrators have appropriate access levels to employee data and system functions.

9.3 Audit Trails. The Service Provider shall maintain audit trails within the HRIS
platform for all data modifications, access events, and system configuration changes,
in accordance with the data retention requirements specified in this Agreement.

10. EMPLOYEE RELATIONS

10.1 Investigation Support. The Service Provider shall provide guidance and support to
the Client in conducting workplace investigations, including complaint intake, investigation
planning, witness interview protocols, documentation templates, and investigation report
preparation. The Service Provider shall not serve as the Client's legal counsel and all
investigation findings shall be subject to review and approval by the Client and/or its
legal counsel.

10.2 Disciplinary Actions. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in the preparation
of disciplinary documentation, including written warnings, suspension notices, and
termination letters, in accordance with the Client's progressive discipline policies
and applicable legal requirements.

10.3 Mediation. The Service Provider shall facilitate mediation between employees and
managers upon request by the Client, provided that such mediation is voluntary and does
not create an attorney-client relationship or otherwise constitute legal advice.

11. REPORTING AND ANALYTICS

11.1 Dashboard. The Service Provider shall provide the Client with access to a real-time
HR analytics dashboard, updated continuously with current workforce data, accessible
through a secure web-based portal.

11.2 Quarterly Business Reviews. The Service Provider shall conduct quarterly business
reviews with the Client's designated representatives, presenting key HR metrics, workforce
trends, compliance status updates, service performance summary, and recommendations for
process improvements.

12. DATA PRIVACY

12.1 Employee Data Handling. The Service Provider shall handle all employee personal
information in accordance with applicable federal, state, and local privacy laws, including
HIPAA requirements applicable to employee health information, state data breach notification
laws, and any applicable privacy regulations governing the processing of employee data.

12.2 International Employees. In the event that the Client employs individuals in the
European Union or other jurisdictions with comprehensive data protection legislation,
the Service Provider shall comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or
other applicable data protection laws with respect to such employees' personal data,
including the processing of international data transfers in compliance with Standard
Contractual Clauses or other approved transfer mechanisms.

13. TERM

13.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue
for a period of thirty-six (36) months, concluding on December 31, 2027 (the "Initial
Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions of Section 19.

13.2 Automatic Renewal. Upon expiration of the Initial Term or any subsequent Renewal
Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods
(each a "Renewal Term"), unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal to
the other party at least one hundred twenty (120) calendar days prior to the expiration
of the then-current term.

13.3 Effect of Termination. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall not affect
any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination, including without
limitation Sections 17 (Intellectual Property), 18 (Confidentiality), 20 (Liability), 21
(Dispute Resolution), 22 (Governing Law), and 23 (General Provisions).

14. COMPENSATION

14.1 Per-Employee Monthly Fee. In consideration of the human resources outsourcing services
described in Section 3, the Client shall pay the Service Provider a fee of eighty-five
dollars ($85.00) per Covered Employee per month for core services rendered under this
Agreement.

14.2 Minimum Monthly Fee. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Client shall pay a minimum
monthly fee of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) per calendar month, regardless
of the actual number of Covered Employees in any given month. If the per-employee fee
calculated pursuant to Section 14.1 for any month is less than twenty-five thousand
dollars ($25,000.00), the Client shall pay the difference as a minimum fee adjustment.

14.3 Implementation Fee. In addition to the ongoing monthly fees described above, the
Client shall pay the Service Provider a one-time, non-refundable implementation fee in
the amount of thirty-five thousand dollars ($35,000.00), payable in two equal installments:
the first installment of seventeen thousand five hundred dollars ($17,500.00) upon
execution of this Agreement, and the second installment of seventeen thousand five hundred
dollars ($17,500.00) upon the completion of HRIS configuration as described in Section 15.

14.4 Implementation Fee — Data Migration Limitation. The implementation fee set forth in
Section 14.3 covers one (1) employee data migration from the Client's legacy systems to
the HRIS platform. Any subsequent data migrations requested by the Client, whether arising
from system changes, acquisitions, reorganizations, or other causes, shall be subject to
an additional charge of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00) per migration event.

14.5 Payment Terms. Monthly invoices shall be submitted by the Service Provider on the
first business day of each calendar month and shall be payable by the Client within thirty
(30) calendar days of receipt.

14.6 Late Payment Interest. Any amounts not paid within the thirty (30) day payment period
shall bear interest at the rate of one and one-quarter percent (1.25%) per month (or the
maximum rate permitted by applicable law, if less), compounded monthly, from the due date
until paid in full.

14.7 Taxes. All fees payable under this Agreement are exclusive of applicable taxes. The
Client shall be responsible for any sales tax, use tax, or similar taxes imposed on the
services provided under this Agreement, except for taxes based on the Service Provider's
net income.

15. DELIVERABLES AND MILESTONES

15.1 HRIS Configuration and Data Migration. The Service Provider shall complete the
configuration of the HRIS platform and the initial migration of employee data from the
Client's legacy systems within sixty (60) calendar days of the Effective Date.

15.2 Benefits Enrollment Setup. The Service Provider shall complete the setup of benefits
enrollment processes and systems within ninety (90) calendar days of the Effective Date,
provided, however, that if the Client's Open Enrollment period commences prior to the
expiration of such ninety (90) day period, the Service Provider shall complete benefits
enrollment setup no later than fifteen (15) calendar days prior to the start of the
Client's Open Enrollment period, whichever is earlier.

15.3 First Payroll Cycle Processing. The Service Provider shall complete the processing
of the Client's first payroll cycle through the HRIS platform within forty-five (45)
calendar days of the Effective Date.

15.4 Quarterly Compliance Audit Reports. The Service Provider shall deliver comprehensive
compliance audit reports to the Client on a quarterly basis throughout the term of this
Agreement, summarizing the Client's compliance status with respect to FMLA, ADA, FLSA,
and other applicable employment laws, identifying any areas of concern, and providing
recommendations for corrective action where necessary.

17. CONFIDENTIALITY

17.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all Confidential

without the prior written consent of the disclosing party, except as required by law,

jurisdiction.

17.2 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section 17 shall
survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of seven (7) years.

17.3 Permitted Disclosures. Notwithstanding the foregoing, a receiving party may disclose
Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors who
have a need to know such information for purposes consistent with this Agreement and who
are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those set forth herein.

18. INSURANCE

18.1 Required Coverage. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the Service Provider shall
maintain, at its sole cost and expense, the following insurance coverages with insurers
rated A- VII or better by A.M. Best Company (or equivalent rating agency):

    (a) Professional Liability Insurance. Professional liability (errors and omissions)
    insurance in an amount not less than three million dollars ($3,000,000) per occurrence,
    covering claims arising from negligent acts, errors, or omissions in the provision of
    human resources outsourcing services under this Agreement.

    (b) Employment Practices Liability Insurance. Employment practices liability insurance
    in an amount not less than one million dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence, covering
    claims arising from employment-related practices including, but not limited to,
    discrimination, wrongful termination, harassment, and retaliation.

18.2 Evidence of Coverage. The Service Provider shall provide certificates of insurance
evidencing the required coverages to the Client upon execution of this Agreement and upon
each renewal of such policies. Such certificates shall provide that the Client shall receive
thirty (30) days' prior written notice of any cancellation, material modification, or
non-renewal of the required insurance policies.

19. TERMINATION

19.1 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience
upon one hundred twenty (120) calendar days' prior written notice to the other party.

19.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause if the other
party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such
breach within forty-five (45) calendar days after receiving written notice specifying the
nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

19.3 Termination upon Insolvency. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon
written notice if the other party becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, is
adjudicated as bankrupt, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver
appointed for a substantial portion of its assets.

19.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Service
Provider shall (a) deliver all completed and in-progress work product to the Client,
(b) return or securely destroy all Client Confidential Information and Client Data in its
possession, and (c) provide reasonable transition assistance to facilitate the Client's
engagement of a successor HR services provider, all at the Client's expense.

LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, LOSS OF GOODWILL,
OR LOSS OF DATA, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE PERFORMANCE OF
SERVICES HEREUNDER, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES ARE BASED ON CONTRACT, TORT,
STRICT LIABILITY, ANY OTHER LEGAL THEORY, AND EVEN IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE

TELECOMMUNICATIONS INFRASTRUCTURE SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Telecommunications Infrastructure Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number TIS-2025-0201, is entered into as of January 20, 2025
(the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the digital divide remains one of the most pressing infrastructure
challenges facing rural communities across the United States, with millions of
Americans in underserved and unserved areas lacking access to reliable, high-speed
broadband internet service that is essential for education, healthcare, economic
development, civic participation, and modern quality of life;

WHEREAS, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) has identified broadband
connectivity as a critical national priority, establishing the Rural Digital
Opportunity Fund (RDOF) and related programs to incentivize the deployment of
high-speed broadband infrastructure in areas where market conditions alone have
failed to attract private investment in telecommunications facilities;

WHEREAS, the Universal Service Fund (USF) and its various support mechanisms,
including the Connect America Fund and the Rural Digital Opportunity Fund, are
designed to promote the availability and adoption of broadband services in
high-cost areas through direct financial support to eligible telecommunications
carriers and infrastructure providers;

WHEREAS, the state of Georgia has a significant rural broadband gap, with
numerous counties in the southern portion of the state lacking access to broadband
speeds that meet or exceed the FCC's current benchmark of 25/3 Mbps download and
upload speeds, and many areas lacking access to any wired broadband service
whatsoever;

WHEREAS, Rural Broadband Initiative Inc., a nonprofit corporation organized
under the laws of the State of Georgia, has been established to address the
rural broadband access gap in southern Georgia through public-private partnerships,
grant-funded infrastructure programs, and community-based deployment initiatives;

WHEREAS, NetConnect Infrastructure Services LLC is a telecommunications
infrastructure company organized under the laws of the State of Georgia, with
extensive experience in the design, deployment, and operation of fiber-optic
broadband networks, including Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH) deployments utilizing
Gigabit Passive Optical Network (GPON) technology in rural and suburban
environments;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to enter into a comprehensive services agreement
under which the Service Provider will design, deploy, and maintain fiber-optic
broadband infrastructure serving underserved rural communities across twelve (12)
counties in southern Georgia, with an initial deployment target of approximately
fifteen thousand (15,000) premises passed;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration,
the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree
as follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Dark Fiber" means unlit optical fiber cable that has been installed but
is not yet connected to active transmission equipment, and which may be activated
at a later date by the installation of optical line terminal (OLT) equipment.

2.2 "Fiber-to-the-Home" or "FTTH" means a broadband network architecture in
which optical fiber cable is extended directly from the central office or hub
location to individual residential or business premises, providing end-to-end
fiber connectivity without the use of copper or coaxial cable in the last-mile
connection.

2.3 "GPON" means Gigabit Passive Optical Network, a passive optical network
architecture that delivers broadband connectivity over fiber using passive optical
splitters to serve multiple end users from a single optical line terminal, and
which supports downstream speeds of up to 2.5 Gbps and upstream speeds of up to
1.25 Gbps.

2.4 "Last Mile" means the final segment of the telecommunications network that
connects the distribution point or optical splitter to the individual end-user
premises, including the drop cable, optical network terminal (ONT), and any
associated customer premises equipment.

2.5 "Middle Mile" means the intermediate segment of the telecommunications
network that connects the central office or point of presence to the local
distribution network, typically consisting of fiber optic cable routes between
hub locations, switching centers, and aggregation points.

2.6 "NOC" means Network Operations Center, the centralized facility operated
by the Service Provider for the purpose of monitoring, managing, and maintaining
the telecommunications network deployed under this Agreement on a twenty-four
(24) hour, seven (7) day per week basis.

2.7 "OLT" means Optical Line Terminal, the active network equipment located
at the central office or hub that serves as the originating point for the GPON
network, converting electrical signals to optical signals for transmission over
the fiber distribution network.

2.8 "ONT" means Optical Network Terminal, the customer premises equipment
located at each served premise that converts optical signals from the fiber
network to electrical signals for delivery to customer devices via Ethernet or
Wi-Fi.

2.9 "Premises Passed" means a residential or business location that is within
two hundred fifty (250) feet of an active fiber drop point and is therefore
eligible for service activation without requiring additional fiber construction,
as verified by an independent third-party engineering firm selected by mutual
agreement of the parties.

2.10 "Right-of-Way" or "ROW" means the legal right to use a specific strip of
land for the purpose of installing, maintaining, and operating telecommunications
infrastructure, including utility poles, underground conduit, and associated
equipment, as granted by the applicable property owner or governmental authority.

2.11 "RDOF" means the Rural Digital Opportunity Fund, a Federal Communications
Commission program established to provide financial support for the deployment of
broadband infrastructure in unserved rural areas of the United States, as
administered by the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC).

2.12 "Split Ratio" means the number of end-user connections served by a single
GPON port through passive optical splitters, expressed as a ratio (e.g., 1:32 or
1:64), which affects the available bandwidth per subscriber and the maximum
distance from the OLT.

2.13 "Take Rate" means the percentage of Premises Passed that have subscribed
to and activated broadband service on the deployed network, used as a key metric
for evaluating network utilization and financial performance.

2.14 "USF" means the Universal Service Fund, the federal program administered
by the Universal Service Administrative Company under the direction of the FCC
that provides financial support for telecommunications services in high-cost,
rural, and underserved areas.

2.15 "WISP" means Wireless Internet Service Provider, a provider of broadband
internet access using fixed wireless or other wireless technologies, which may
serve as an interim or complementary technology in areas where fiber deployment
is not yet feasible.

2.16 "Service Level" or "SLA" means the performance standards and metrics
defined in this Agreement against which the quality, reliability, and
responsiveness of the Service Provider's network and maintenance services shall
be measured.

2.17 "Third-Party Verifier" means an independent engineering or consulting
firm mutually agreed upon by the parties, which shall perform independent
verification of Premises Passed counts and other deployment metrics as required
under this Agreement.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive
telecommunications infrastructure services for the design, deployment, and
maintenance of fiber-optic broadband infrastructure serving underserved rural
communities in southern Georgia. The initial deployment area encompasses twelve
(12) counties: Baker, Calhoun, Clay, Dougherty, Early, Grady, Miller, Mitchell,
Quitman, Randolph, Seminole, and Terrell. The deployment is designed to reach
approximately fifteen thousand (15,000) potential subscriber premises across the
twelve-county coverage area.

3.2 Network Design Services. The Service Provider shall perform comprehensive
network architecture design services, including but not limited to: selection
of GPON versus active Ethernet technology for each service area based on density,
cost, and scalability analysis; design of the core, distribution, and access
network layers; redundancy and failover planning; headend and hub site selection;
power redundancy design; and preparation of detailed network architecture
documentation.

3.3 Fiber Route Planning. The Service Provider shall perform detailed fiber
route planning, including: GIS-based route mapping and optimization; pole
attachment analysis and coordination with incumbent utility pole owners;
underground construction route design; directional boring plans for road and
waterway crossings; and preparation of detailed construction prints for each
deployment phase.

3.4 Permitting and Right-of-Way Coordination. The Service Provider shall be
responsible for obtaining all necessary permits and right-of-way agreements,
including: municipal construction permits in each jurisdiction; Georgia
Department of Transportation encroachment permits for state highway crossings;
railroad crossing permits; environmental clearances for sensitive areas; and
utility pole attachment agreements with incumbent pole owners.

3.5 Fiber Installation Services. The Service Provider shall perform all fiber
installation work, including both middle-mile and last-mile construction, aerial
and underground fiber placement, fiber splicing and testing, drop installation
to individual premises, ONT installation and provisioning, and end-to-end
circuit testing and acceptance.

3.6 Network Equipment Provisioning. The Service Provider shall procure,
install, and configure all network equipment, including OLTs, optical splitters,
ONTs, routers, switches, UPS systems, grounding equipment, and all other
hardware and software necessary for the operation of the broadband network.

3.7 NOC Monitoring Services. The Service Provider shall provide twenty-four
(24) hour, seven (7) day per week network monitoring and management services
through its Network Operations Center (NOC), including proactive fault detection
and alerting, incident response and escalation, performance monitoring and
reporting, and subscriber management support.

3.8 Maintenance Services. The Service Provider shall provide ongoing
maintenance services for the deployed fiber-optic infrastructure, including
preventive maintenance on a scheduled basis, emergency repair services,
fiber restoration following outages, equipment replacement and upgrades, and
vegetation management along aerial fiber routes.

4. NETWORK DESIGN STANDARDS

4.1 Technology Selection. The Service Provider shall deploy GPON technology as
the primary access network architecture, with a split ratio not exceeding 1:32
for residential deployments and 1:16 for business-class deployments. Active
Ethernet may be deployed in areas with high subscriber density or where
symmetrical gigabit service is required for institutional or commercial
subscribers.

4.2 Redundancy. The middle-mile network shall be designed with ring topology
providing at least N+1 redundancy between hub sites. Each hub site shall be
connected to at least two (2) diverse fiber routes. The Service Provider shall
deploy automated protection switching to ensure seamless failover in the event
of a fiber cut or equipment failure.

4.3 Scalability. All fiber routes shall be designed with sufficient spare fiber
capacity to support future expansion and demand growth. The Service Provider
shall deploy at least twelve (12) fiber strands in all middle-mile routes and
at least four (4) fiber strands in all distribution routes.

5. FIBER ROUTE PLANNING

5.1 GIS Mapping. The Service Provider shall utilize geographic information
system (GIS) tools to map all proposed fiber routes, identifying existing
utility infrastructure, property boundaries, environmental features, and other
relevant factors that may affect route selection and construction methods.

5.2 Pole Attachment. Where aerial construction is proposed, the Service
Provider shall negotiate pole attachment agreements with incumbent utility pole
owners in accordance with applicable FCC pole attachment rules and Georgia state
law. The Service Provider shall perform make-ready work as required by the
pole owner prior to fiber attachment.

5.3 Underground Construction. Where underground construction is required or
preferred, the Service Provider shall utilize directional boring methods to
minimize surface disruption, and shall coordinate with all utility locating
services prior to excavation in accordance with Georgia Utility Facility
Protection Act (OUCC Act) requirements.

6. PERMITTING

6.1 Municipal Permits. The Service Provider shall obtain all required
municipal construction permits, right-of-way permits, and encroachment permits
in each jurisdiction within the deployment area, and shall comply with all
applicable local ordinances governing telecommunications facility construction.

6.2 State DOT Permits. The Service Provider shall obtain all required permits
from the Georgia Department of Transportation for fiber crossings of state
highways and roads, including encroachment permits, utility adjustment permits,
and temporary traffic control plans.

6.3 Environmental Clearances. Where fiber routes traverse environmentally
sensitive areas, including wetlands, protected habitats, or cultural resource
sites, the Service Provider shall obtain all required environmental clearances
and permits from the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, the U.S. Army
Corps of Engineers, and other applicable agencies.

7. INSTALLATION STANDARDS

7.1 Splicing Specifications. All fiber splicing shall be performed by
certified fiber optic technicians in accordance with Telcordia GR-1095-CORE
and applicable industry standards. Fusion splice loss shall not exceed 0.10 dB
per splice, and all splices shall be documented in the network management
system with GPS coordinates and loss measurements.

7.2 Drop Installation. Fiber drops from the distribution network to individual
premises shall be installed in accordance with the Service Provider's standard
drop installation specifications, including proper sag, clearance, and
attachment requirements for aerial drops, and proper depth, conduit, and
handhole specifications for underground drops.

7.3 ONT Placement. ONTs shall be installed at each served premise in a
location approved by the subscriber that provides adequate power, ventilation,
and proximity to the fiber entry point. The Service Provider shall provide the
subscriber with a Wi-Fi router configured for optimal coverage.

7.4 Testing Protocols. All fiber circuits shall undergo end-to-end optical
testing prior to service activation, including optical loss testing, OTDR
testing, and bit error rate testing. Test results shall be documented and
retained in the network management system.

8. NETWORK EQUIPMENT

8.1 OLT Equipment. The Service Provider shall deploy carrier-grade OLT
equipment at each hub site, with sufficient port capacity to serve the planned
deployment area with at least twenty percent (20%) spare capacity for future
growth.

8.2 Optical Splitters. Passive optical splitters shall be deployed in
accordance with the network design specifications set forth in Section 4.1.
All splitter cabinets and closures shall be rated for outdoor use and shall
provide environmental protection consistent with the deployment environment.

8.3 Power and Grounding. All active network equipment shall be powered through
uninterruptible power supply (UPS) systems with a minimum of four (4) hours of
battery backup. All equipment shall be properly grounded in accordance with
NEC Article 770 and applicable telecommunications grounding standards.

9. NOC OPERATIONS

9.1 Monitoring. The Service Provider shall operate a Network Operations Center
(NOC) providing twenty-four (24) hour, seven (7) day per week monitoring of
all network elements deployed under this Agreement, including OLTs, ONTs,
routers, switches, power systems, and environmental monitoring systems at hub
sites.

9.2 Fault Detection and Response. The NOC shall implement automated fault
detection and alerting systems capable of identifying network outages,
degraded service conditions, and equipment failures within five (5) minutes of
occurrence. The NOC shall respond to critical alarms within fifteen (15)
minutes and shall initiate repair procedures in accordance with the escalation
procedures set forth in Section 9.3.

9.3 Escalation Procedures. The Service Provider shall maintain documented
escalation procedures for all alarm categories, including: P1 (critical) —
network-wide outage affecting more than 500 subscribers, response within
fifteen (15) minutes; P2 (major) — outage affecting a single community or
route segment, response within thirty (30) minutes; P3 (moderate) — degraded
service or partial outage, response within two (2) hours; P4 (minor) —
individual subscriber issues, response within four (4) hours.

10. MAINTENANCE

10.1 Preventive Maintenance. The Service Provider shall conduct preventive
maintenance on all network infrastructure on a scheduled basis, including:
quarterly inspection of all aerial fiber routes; semi-annual testing of all
fiber splices and connections; annual inspection of all hub sites and power
systems; and annual vegetation management along all aerial fiber routes.

10.2 Emergency Repairs. The Service Provider shall provide emergency repair
services on a twenty-four (24) hour, seven (7) day per week basis. The
Service Provider shall respond to fiber cut reports within two (2) hours and
shall restore service within twelve (12) hours for single-fiber cuts and
twenty-four (24) hours for multiple-fiber cuts or catastrophic damage.

10.3 Network Uptime SLA. The Service Provider shall maintain network uptime
of not less than ninety-nine point nine five percent (99.95%) on a monthly
basis, excluding planned maintenance windows and outages caused by events
beyond the Service Provider's reasonable control, including but not limited to
acts of God, severe weather, fiber cuts caused by third parties, and delays
caused by permitting authorities.

11. FCC COMPLIANCE

11.1 RDOF Administration. The Service Provider shall administer all aspects
of the FCC Rural Digital Opportunity Fund (RDOF) subsidy program on behalf of
the Client, including: preparation and submission of RDOF applications; ongoing
compliance reporting to the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC);
maintenance of required financial records; and coordination with FCC audit and
compliance reviews.

11.2 Universal Service Obligations. The Service Provider shall ensure that the
deployed network meets or exceeds all FCC broadband speed requirements
applicable to the RDOF program, including minimum download speeds of 100 Mbps
and minimum upload speeds of 20 Mbps to each served premise.

11.3 Consumer Protection. The Service Provider shall comply with all FCC
consumer protection requirements applicable to broadband service providers,
including the FCC's broadband transparency rule (47 CFR Part 8), truth-in-
billing requirements, and complaint handling procedures.

11.4 Reporting. The Service Provider shall prepare and submit all required
FCC and USAC reports in a timely manner, including: FCC Form 477 broadband
deployment reports; RDOF annual performance reports; universal service
contribution reports; and any other reports required by the FCC or USAC in
connection with the RDOF program.

12. SUBSCRIBER MANAGEMENT

12.1 Sign-Up Process. The Service Provider shall establish and operate a
subscriber sign-up process, including a toll-free number, website, and in-person
sign-up events in each community within the deployment area.

12.2 Installation Scheduling. The Service Provider shall schedule and complete
subscriber installations within ten (10) business days of subscription request,
subject to weather and permitting conditions.

12.3 Customer Support Handoff. Upon request by the Client, the Service Provider
shall transition subscriber management and customer support functions to the
Client or the Client's designated third-party service provider, subject to a
mutually agreed transition plan and timeline.

13. COMPENSATION

13.1 Design and Planning Fee. The Client shall pay the Service Provider a
fixed design and planning fee of four hundred fifty thousand dollars ($450,000.00)
for all network design, architecture, route planning, and permitting services
performed under Sections 3.2 through 3.4. This fee shall be payable in full
upon execution of this Agreement.

13.2 Per-Premises Passed Installation Fee. The Client shall pay the Service
Provider a fee of one thousand two hundred dollars ($1,200.00) per Premises
Passed for all fiber installation services performed under Sections 3.5 and
3.6. Premises Passed counts shall be verified on a monthly basis by an
independent Third-Party Verifier selected by mutual agreement of the parties,
whose fees shall be shared equally by the Client and the Service Provider.

13.3 Monthly NOC and Maintenance Fee. The Client shall pay the Service Provider
a monthly fee of twenty-eight thousand dollars ($28,000.00) for NOC monitoring
and maintenance services performed under Sections 3.7 and 3.8, payable monthly
in advance on the first day of each calendar month during the Term.

13.4 Estimated Total Value. Based on the estimated deployment of fifteen
thousand (15,000) premises at $1,200.00 per premises, the estimated total
installation value is eighteen million dollars ($18,000,000.00). The actual
total value will depend on the final verified Premises Passed count.

13.5 Federal Subsidy Pass-Through. The Service Provider shall administer FCC
RDOF subsidies directly, with all RDOF support payments received by the Service
Provider applied as a credit against the Client's obligations under this
Agreement, thereby reducing the Client's net cost of the deployment. The
Service Provider shall provide the Client with monthly accounting of all RDOF
subsidies received and applied.

13.6 Payment Terms. The design and planning fee shall be due upon execution of
this Agreement. Installation fees shall be due monthly based on the verified
Premises Passed count for the preceding month, with invoices submitted by the
fifteenth (15th) day of each month and payment due within thirty (30) days of
invoice receipt. The monthly NOC and maintenance fee shall be due on the first
day of each month.

14.4 First 5,000 Premises Connected. Within twenty-four (24) months of the
Effective Date, the Service Provider shall achieve Premises Passed verification

14.5 Full 15,000 Premises Connected. Within forty-two (42) months of the

14.6 Quarterly Network Performance Reports. Within fifteen (15) days following
the end of each calendar quarter, the Service Provider shall deliver a
comprehensive network performance report to the Client, including Premises
Passed counts, subscriber activation counts, network uptime statistics, outage
reports, NOC performance metrics, and RDOF compliance status.

17. TERMINATION

17.1 Termination Without Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement
without cause upon one hundred eighty (180) calendar days' prior written notice
to the other party.

17.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause
if the other party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement
and fails to cure such breach within sixty (60) calendar days after receiving
written notice specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

17.3 Termination for FCC Compliance Breach. Notwithstanding the cure period
set forth in Section 17.2, the Client may terminate this Agreement immediately
upon written notice if the Service Provider commits a material breach of any
FCC compliance obligation under Section 11, including but not limited to failure
to meet RDOF performance milestones, submission of false or misleading FCC or
USAC reports, or loss of RDOF eligibility, provided that such breach poses a
risk of FCC enforcement action or loss of federal subsidies.

17.4 Non-Renewal. Either party may decline to renew this Agreement at the end
of the Initial Term or any Renewal Term by providing the other party with at
least one hundred eighty (180) calendar days' prior written notice of its intent
not to renew.

17.5 Transition Obligations. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement,
the Service Provider shall cooperate in transitioning network operations to the
Client's designated successor provider, including the transfer of all network
documentation, subscriber data, FCC filings, and equipment as mutually agreed.

FOOD AND BEVERAGE CATERING AND CONCESSION SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Food and Beverage Catering and Concession Services Agreement (the
"Agreement"), bearing reference number FBC-2025-0067, is entered into as of
March 15, 2025 (the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified
in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the sports and entertainment venue food service industry has undergone
a dramatic transformation over the past two decades, evolving from traditional
hot dog and popcorn concession stands to sophisticated, chef-driven culinary
programs that reflect the diverse gastronomic culture of their host cities and
regions;

WHEREAS, modern sports venue attendees have come to expect a premium food and
beverage experience that rivals or exceeds the quality of standalone restaurants,
with particular emphasis on locally sourced ingredients, craft beverage programs,
diverse dietary accommodations, and innovative delivery methods including
in-seat service and mobile ordering platforms;

WHEREAS, the regulatory landscape governing food and beverage service at large
public assembly venues has become increasingly complex, encompassing federal,
state, and local health and safety codes, Alcoholic Beverage Control (ABC)
regulations, food allergen labeling requirements, occupational safety standards,
and environmental sustainability mandates that require specialized expertise and
dedicated compliance infrastructure;

WHEREAS, Oracle Park, home of the San Francisco Giants, is a premier Major
League Baseball venue with a seating capacity of 41,915 and hosts approximately
85 events per year, including all San Francisco Giants regular season home games
and a variety of private venue events, and requires comprehensive food and
beverage services capable of serving large volumes of patrons within compressed
time windows while maintaining the highest standards of food safety, quality, and
customer satisfaction;

WHEREAS, Bay Area Sports and Entertainment Authority, a joint powers authority
organized under the laws of the State of California, has determined that
exclusive food and beverage catering and concession services provided by an
experienced, full-service catering and concession operator are essential to
delivering an exceptional fan experience and maximizing revenue generation at
Oracle Park;

WHEREAS, Savory Events Catering Inc. is a full-service food and beverage
catering and concession company incorporated under the laws of the State of
California, with extensive experience operating large-scale food and beverage
programs at sports and entertainment venues throughout the San Francisco Bay
Area, and has demonstrated the operational capacity, culinary expertise, and
regulatory compliance infrastructure necessary to manage the comprehensive food
and beverage program at Oracle Park;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration,
the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree
as follows:

1. PARTIES

1.1 Service Provider. Savory Events Catering Inc., a corporation duly organized
and existing under the laws of the State of California, with its principal
offices located at 5800 Mission Street, San Francisco, CA 94112 (hereinafter
referred to as "Service Provider," "Savory," or "Caterer").

1.2 Client. Bay Area Sports and Entertainment Authority, a joint powers
authority organized under the laws of the State of California, with its
principal offices located at 24 Willie Mays Plaza, San Francisco, CA 94107
(hereinafter referred to as "Client," "Authority," or "Venue").

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents and warrants that
they have the full legal authority to bind their respective organization to the
terms and conditions of this Agreement, and that the execution of this
Agreement has been duly authorized by all necessary corporate and governmental
approvals.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "ABC License" means any license, permit, or authorization issued by the
California Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control that is required for the
sale, service, or distribution of alcoholic beverages at the Venue.

2.2 "Back of House" or "BOH" means all kitchen, preparation, storage, and
commissary areas within the Venue that are not accessible to the general public,
including but not limited to main kitchens, satellite kitchens, walk-in coolers
and freezers, dry storage rooms, dishwashing stations, and food preparation
areas.

2.3 "Commissary" means the central food production and distribution facility
operated by the Service Provider within or adjacent to the Venue, serving as the
primary location for bulk food preparation, inventory staging, and supply
distribution to all concession outlets and catering stations.

2.4 "Concession Stand" or "Concession Outlet" means any fixed or semi-permanent
food and beverage service location within the Venue, including but not limited
to permanent concession stands, portable concession carts, kiosks, and
counter-service locations operated by the Service Provider during Events.

2.5 "Front of House" or "FOH" means all service areas within the Venue that are
accessible to patrons, including but not limited to concourses, concession
stands, suite levels, club dining rooms, and in-seat service areas.

2.6 "Gross Sales" means the total amount of all sales of food, beverages, and
merchandise food items by the Service Provider at the Venue during an Event or
Event Period, before any deductions for taxes, discounts, refunds, or service
charges, and excluding only sales of alcoholic beverages (which are reported
separately as "Alcoholic Beverage Sales") and premium catering services (which
are reported separately as "Premium Catering Sales").

2.7 "Health Permit" means any permit, license, or authorization issued by the
San Francisco Department of Public Health or other applicable health authority
that is required for the preparation, storage, service, or sale of food or
beverages at the Venue.

2.8 "Luxury Suite" or "Premium Suite" means any of the luxury seating
enclosures located at the suite level of the Venue, including enclosed and
semi-enclosed suites, which receive dedicated catering and beverage services
from the Service Provider.

2.9 "Per Capita" means the average amount of food and beverage spending per
attendee at an Event, calculated by dividing total Gross Sales (including
Alcoholic Beverage Sales and Premium Catering Sales) by the total paid
attendance for such Event.

2.10 "Point of Sale" or "POS" means the electronic point of sale system
deployed by the Service Provider at all Concession Stands and service locations
within the Venue for the purpose of processing sales transactions, accepting
payments, tracking inventory, and generating sales and revenue reports.

2.11 "Premium Catering" or "Premium Catering Sales" means all food, beverage,
and service revenues generated by the Service Provider in connection with luxury
suite catering, club level dining, private dining rooms, field-level
hospitality areas, and other premium hospitality services provided at the Venue,
excluding general concession stand sales and portable cart sales.

2.12 "Retention Rate" means the percentage of total Gross Sales retained by the
Service Provider after payment of the Client's revenue share, calculated on a
category-by-category basis in accordance with the revenue sharing percentages
set forth in Section 7.

2.13 "Service Charge" means any gratuity, service fee, or administrative charge
added to the price of food, beverage, or catering services provided by the
Service Provider at the Venue, which shall be retained in full by the Service
Provider and shall not be included in the calculation of Gross Sales or revenue
sharing.

2.14 "Spend Per Head" means the total food and beverage spending per attendee
at an Event, calculated by dividing total Gross Sales by total attendance, and
used as a key performance indicator for evaluating the effectiveness and
profitability of the food and beverage program.

2.15 "Venue Agreement" means the existing venue use and management agreement
between the Client and the San Francisco Giants, and any amendments, extensions,
or renewals thereof, governing the use, operation, and management of Oracle Park.

2.16 "Event" means any San Francisco Giants home game, private venue event, or
other public or private assembly held at Oracle Park for which food and beverage
services are provided under this Agreement.

2.17 "Event Period" means the period beginning four (4) hours before the
scheduled start time of an Event and ending two (2) hours after the conclusion
of such Event, or such other period as may be mutually agreed upon by the
parties for specific Events.

2.18 "Alcoholic Beverage Sales" means the total amount of all sales of beer,
wine, spirits, cocktails, and other alcoholic beverages by the Service Provider
at the Venue during an Event or Event Period, before any deductions for taxes,
discounts, or refunds.

2.19 "Minimum Annual Guarantee" or "MAG" means the minimum annual payment
guaranteed by the Service Provider to the Client as set forth in Section 7.3,
regardless of actual revenue performance during the applicable annual period.

2.20 "Operating Season" means the period from the first San Francisco Giants
home game of each calendar year through the last San Francisco Giants home game
of such year, including any postseason games played at Oracle Park.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide exclusive food and
beverage catering and concession services for Oracle Park, a premier Major
League Baseball venue with a seating capacity of 41,915, located at 24 Willie
Mays Plaza, San Francisco, CA 94107. The Service Provider shall be the sole
provider of all food and beverage services at the Venue during all Events,
including all San Francisco Giants home games and private venue events
(approximately 85 events per year). The scope of services includes, without
limitation, premium suite catering, club level dining, general concession
stands, portable concession carts, beverage service (alcoholic and
non-alcoholic), in-seat delivery service, private event catering, and novelty
and merchandise food items.

3.2 Premium Suite Catering. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive
catering services for all luxury suites and premium seating areas at the Venue,
including but not limited to: seasonal suite catering menus with a minimum of
three (3) tiered package options per game; pre-game and in-game food service;
dedicated beverage service including a full bar program; custom menu
development for suite holders with dietary restrictions; and post-game catering
upon request.

3.3 Club Level Dining. The Service Provider shall operate all club level dining
facilities at the Venue, including but not limited to: all-inclusive buffet and
food station service for club level ticket holders; themed dining experiences
aligned with promotional game nights; craft beer and wine programming; and
dedicated wait staff service for all club level seating sections.

3.4 General Concession Stands. The Service Provider shall operate all permanent
concession stands located throughout the Venue concourses, including but not
limited to: traditional ballpark fare concession stands; specialty food stands
featuring San Francisco and Bay Area culinary themes; vegetarian, vegan, and
gluten-free concession options at a minimum of four (4) locations; international
food stands representing the diverse culinary traditions of the Bay Area; and
dessert and novelty food stands.

3.5 Portable Concession Carts. The Service Provider shall deploy and operate a
fleet of portable concession carts throughout the Venue, including but not
limited to: mobile beer and wine carts on all concourse levels; specialty
item carts featuring items such as garlic fries, churros, and branded food
offerings; quick-service beverage carts for high-traffic areas; and portable
food carts positioned near entry gates for pre-game service.

3.6 Beverage Service. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive beverage
service throughout the Venue, encompassing both alcoholic and non-alcoholic
beverages, including but not limited to: domestic and craft beer service at all
concession stands and portable carts; wine service by the glass and bottle;
full cocktail and spirits service at designated bar locations; fountain
beverages, bottled water, and specialty non-alcoholic drinks; and coffee, tea,
and specialty beverage service.

3.7 In-Seat Delivery Service. The Service Provider shall offer in-seat food
and beverage delivery service to all premium seating areas and, where
operationally feasible, to select general seating sections, utilizing mobile
ordering technology, dedicated delivery staff, and expedited preparation
protocols to ensure timely delivery.

3.8 Private Event Catering. The Service Provider shall provide full-service
catering for all private events held at the Venue, including but not limited
to: corporate hospitality events, meetings, and conferences; wedding receptions
and private celebrations; charity galas and fundraising events; holiday parties
and seasonal celebrations; and community and civic events.

3.9 Novelty and Merchandise Food Items. The Service Provider shall develop and
offer novelty and branded food items exclusive to the Venue, including but not
limited to: signature dishes associated with the San Francisco Giants brand;
limited-time seasonal offerings; promotional food items tied to special events
and giveaways; and collectible or branded food packaging and merchandise.

4. MENU DEVELOPMENT

4.1 Seasonal Menu Rotation. The Service Provider shall develop and implement a
seasonal menu rotation program, introducing new menu items and retiring
underperforming items at the beginning of each baseball season, at the All-Star
break, and at the start of any postseason play. Each seasonal menu cycle shall
include a minimum of ten (10) new or revised menu items across all service
categories.

4.2 Dietary Accommodations. The Service Provider shall maintain comprehensive
dietary accommodation options at all times, including but not limited to:
vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, nut-free, dairy-free, kosher, and halal
options. All concession stands shall clearly label dietary options, and the
Service Provider shall maintain a current allergen matrix for all menu items.

4.3 Local Sourcing. The Service Provider shall prioritize the sourcing of
ingredients from local and regional producers, with a target of sourcing a
minimum of thirty percent (30%) of all food ingredients from producers located
within one hundred fifty (150) miles of the Venue.

4.4 Price Points. The Service Provider shall offer menu items across a range of
price points designed to serve all attendee demographics, with value-priced
options, mid-range selections, and premium offerings available at every
concession location.

5. OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Staffing Ratios. The Service Provider shall maintain staffing levels
sufficient to ensure that average customer wait times at concession stands do
not exceed five (5) minutes during peak service periods, and that all concession
stands are fully operational no later than ninety (90) minutes prior to the
scheduled start of each Event.

5.2 Uniform Standards. All Service Provider personnel shall wear clean,
professional uniforms that comply with health department requirements and
reflect positively on the Venue brand. Uniform standards shall be submitted to
the Client for approval prior to the start of each Operating Season.

5.3 Service Speed Targets. The Service Provider shall implement operational
procedures and training programs designed to achieve the following service speed
targets: maximum transaction time of sixty (60) seconds per concession customer;
maximum delivery time of fifteen (15) minutes for in-seat orders; and full
suite catering setup completion no later than sixty (60) minutes prior to first
pitch.

5.4 Queue Management. The Service Provider shall implement queue management
systems at all high-volume concession locations, including digital queue
monitoring, express lanes for beverage-only orders, and mobile ordering
pick-up stations to minimize wait times and maximize throughput.

6. FOOD SAFETY AND SANITATION

6.1 HACCP Plan. The Service Provider shall develop, implement, and maintain a
comprehensive Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points (HACCP) plan covering all
food preparation, storage, cooking, holding, and service operations at the
Venue, in accordance with applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

6.2 Health Department Permits. The Service Provider shall obtain and maintain
all required health permits, food facility permits, and food handler
certifications for all food service operations at the Venue, and shall ensure
that such permits and certifications remain current throughout the Term.

6.3 Temperature Monitoring. The Service Provider shall implement continuous
temperature monitoring systems for all refrigeration, freezing, and hot-holding
equipment, with automated alerts for any temperature deviations outside of
acceptable ranges, and shall maintain temperature logs for a minimum of two (2)
years.

6.4 Allergen Protocols. The Service Provider shall implement comprehensive
allergen management protocols, including staff training on the Big Nine
allergens, separate preparation areas and equipment for allergen-free items,
clear labeling of all menu items with allergen information, and documented
procedures for responding to allergen-related customer inquiries and incidents.

6.5 Recall Procedures. The Service Provider shall maintain documented food
recall procedures, including supplier notification protocols, product
identification and segregation procedures, and customer notification processes,
and shall conduct at least one (1) food recall simulation exercise per year.

7. COMPENSATION

7.1 Revenue Sharing — General Concession Sales. The Client shall receive
forty-five percent (45%) of the Gross Sales from all general concession stands,
portable concession carts, and novelty food items. The Service Provider shall
retain fifty-five percent (55%) of such Gross Sales.

7.2 Revenue Sharing — Premium Catering Sales. The Client shall receive
fifty-two percent (52%) of all Premium Catering Sales from luxury suite
catering, club level dining, private dining rooms, and other premium hospitality
services. The Service Provider shall retain forty-eight percent (48%) of such
Premium Catering Sales.

7.3 Revenue Sharing — Alcoholic Beverage Sales. The Client shall receive
thirty-five percent (35%) of all Alcoholic Beverage Sales at the Venue. The
Service Provider shall retain sixty-five percent (65%) of such Alcoholic
Beverage Sales.

7.4 Minimum Annual Guarantee. Regardless of actual revenue performance, the
Service Provider shall guarantee to the Client a minimum annual payment of
two million eight hundred thousand dollars ($2,800,000.00) per Operating Season
(the "Minimum Annual Guarantee" or "MAG"). If the Client's aggregate revenue
share under Sections 7.1, 7.2, and 7.3 for any Operating Season is less than
the MAG, the Service Provider shall pay the difference to the Client within
thirty (30) days following the conclusion of such Operating Season. If the
Client's aggregate revenue share for any Operating Season exceeds the MAG, the
Service Provider shall retain all revenues above the MAG after payment of the
Client's share.

7.5 Monthly Settlement. Revenue sharing payments shall be settled on a monthly
basis. Within fifteen (15) days following the end of each calendar month, the
Service Provider shall submit to the Client a detailed monthly revenue report
itemizing Gross Sales, Premium Catering Sales, Alcoholic Beverage Sales, and
the Client's share of each category. The Client's revenue share payment shall be
due and payable within thirty (30) days of receipt of the monthly revenue
report.

7.6 Service Charges. Any service charges, gratuities, or administrative fees
added by the Service Provider to the price of catering services, suite
catering packages, or private event catering shall be retained in full by the
Service Provider and shall not be included in the calculation of Gross Sales or
the Client's revenue share.

8. ALCOHOL SERVICE

8.1 ABC Licensing. The Service Provider shall obtain and maintain all required
ABC licenses, permits, and authorizations for the sale and service of alcoholic
beverages at the Venue, and shall comply with all applicable California Alcoholic
Beverage Control laws and regulations at all times.

8.2 Responsible Service Training. All Service Provider personnel who serve or
sell alcoholic beverages shall complete a certified responsible beverage service
training program prior to their first day of service and shall complete annual
refresher training thereafter.

8.3 Intoxication Prevention. The Service Provider shall implement comprehensive
intoxication prevention protocols, including but not limited to: mandatory ID
verification for all patrons appearing to be under the age of thirty (30);
refusal of service to visibly intoxicated patrons; cutoff policies for alcohol
service during the later innings of baseball games; and coordination with Venue
security for the removal of disorderly patrons.

8.4 Minor ID Verification. The Service Provider shall implement strict minor
identification verification procedures at all points of alcoholic beverage sale,
using electronic ID scanners or other reliable verification methods, and shall
ensure that no alcoholic beverages are sold to any person under the age of
twenty-one (21).

9. TECHNOLOGY AND POS SYSTEMS

9.1 Point of Sale Systems. The Service Provider shall deploy and maintain
modern, integrated point of sale systems at all concession stands, portable
carts, premium service locations, and in-seat delivery stations throughout the
Venue. The POS system shall support real-time sales reporting, inventory
tracking, payment processing, and integration with the Venue's ticketing and
access control systems.

9.2 Inventory Management. The Service Provider shall implement a comprehensive
inventory management system integrated with the POS system, providing real-time
inventory visibility, automated reorder alerts, waste tracking, and variance
reporting for all food, beverage, and supply items.

9.3 Mobile Ordering. The Service Provider shall offer a mobile ordering
platform accessible via smartphone application and web browser, allowing patrons
to place food and beverage orders for pickup at designated locations or for
in-seat delivery, with integrated payment processing and order status tracking.

9.4 Payment Processing. The POS system shall accept all major credit and debit
cards, mobile payment methods (including Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay),
and contactless payment technologies. Cash payment acceptance shall be maintained
at a minimum of twenty percent (20%) of all concession locations.

10. FINANCIAL CONTROLS

10.1 Cash Handling. The Service Provider shall implement and maintain rigorous
cash handling procedures, including but not limited to: dual-control cash drawers;
regular cash pick-ups throughout each Event; daily cash reconciliation; and
secure cash transport and deposit procedures.

10.2 Revenue Reconciliation. The Service Provider shall reconcile all revenue
streams following each Event, comparing POS system sales records with actual cash
and credit card receipts, and shall investigate and resolve any discrepancies
within seventy-two (72) hours.

10.3 Audit Rights. The Client shall have the right to audit the Service
Provider's financial records related to food and beverage operations at the Venue
no more than twice (2) per year, upon at least ten (10) business days' prior
written notice. The Service Provider shall cooperate fully with any such audit
and shall provide the Client or its designated auditors with access to all
relevant financial records, POS data, and supporting documentation.

10.4 Per Capita Reporting. The Service Provider shall calculate and report per
capita spending for each Event, broken down by category (general concessions,
premium catering, alcoholic beverages), and shall provide comparative per capita
analysis on a monthly and annual basis.

11. SUSTAINABILITY

11.1 Waste Reduction. The Service Provider shall implement comprehensive waste
reduction programs, with a target of diverting a minimum of sixty percent (60%)
of all food and beverage waste from landfill disposal through composting,
recycling, and food donation programs.

11.2 Composting. The Service Provider shall establish a composting program for
all food waste generated at the Venue, including back-of-house prep waste, unsold
perishable food, and post-consumer food waste from compostable service ware.

11.3 Recyclable Materials. The Service Provider shall use recyclable or
compostable service ware (plates, cups, utensils, and packaging) to the maximum
extent practicable, with a target of achieving one hundred percent (100%)
recyclable or compostable service ware within the first Operating Season.

11.4 Local Sourcing Targets. In addition to the local sourcing requirements set
forth in Section 4.3, the Service Provider shall establish and report on annual
local sourcing targets, with the goal of increasing the percentage of locally
sourced ingredients by at least five percent (5%) per year during the Term.

12. STAFFING

12.1 Background Checks. The Service Provider shall conduct comprehensive
background checks on all personnel who will work at the Venue, including
criminal history checks, employment verification, and reference checks, in
accordance with applicable law.

12.2 Food Handler Certifications. All Service Provider personnel who handle,
prepare, or serve food or beverages at the Venue shall possess a valid California
Food Handler Card, and such certifications shall be maintained current throughout
the Term.

12.3 Training Programs. The Service Provider shall implement comprehensive
training programs for all Venue personnel, covering but not limited to: food
safety and sanitation; responsible alcohol service; customer service excellence;
POS system operation; allergen awareness and response; emergency procedures; and
Venue-specific policies and procedures.

12.4 Game-Day Staffing. The Service Provider shall employ or engage a sufficient
number of trained personnel for each Event to meet the staffing ratios and
service speed targets set forth in Section 5, and shall provide the Client with
a proposed game-day staffing plan no later than forty-eight (48) hours prior to
each Event.

13. COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

13.1 Local Vendor Participation. The Service Provider shall make commercially
reasonable efforts to include local and minority-owned businesses, women-owned
businesses, and small businesses as vendors, suppliers, and subcontractors in
connection with food and beverage operations at the Venue.

13.2 Charitable Donations. The Service Provider shall donate unsold, wholesome
prepared food from Venue events to local charitable organizations through
established food rescue programs, to the extent permitted by applicable health
and safety regulations.

13.3 Youth Programs. The Service Provider shall participate in and support at
least two (2) community youth programs per year related to culinary education,
hospitality training, or food security, in coordination with the Client's
community relations initiatives.

14. DELIVERABLES AND REPORTING

14.1 Season Menu and Pricing Plan. Within forty-five (45) days of the Effective
Date, and no later than January 15 of each subsequent year, the Service Provider
shall deliver to the Client a comprehensive season menu and pricing plan for the
upcoming Operating Season, including all proposed menu items, pricing, dietary
accommodations, and promotional offerings.

14.2 POS System Deployment. Within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date, the
Service Provider shall complete the deployment and testing of all POS systems,
inventory management systems, mobile ordering platforms, and payment processing
systems at the Venue, and shall provide the Client with documentation confirming
full operational readiness.

14.3 Staff Training and Health Certifications. Within seventy-five (75) days of
the Effective Date, and prior to the first Event, the Service Provider shall
complete all required staff training programs and shall provide the Client with
certification records confirming that all personnel have obtained the required
food handler certifications, responsible beverage service training, and
background clearances.

14.4 First Event Execution. The Service Provider shall provide full food and
beverage services for Opening Day of the San Francisco Giants regular season
(the "First Event"), and shall ensure that all concession outlets, premium
services, and support operations are fully operational and staffed in accordance
with the standards set forth in this Agreement.

14.5 Monthly Revenue and Attendance Reports. Within fifteen (15) days following
the end of each calendar month, the Service Provider shall deliver to the Client
a detailed monthly revenue and attendance report, including total Gross Sales,
Premium Catering Sales, Alcoholic Beverage Sales, per capita spending by
category, attendance figures for each Event, and variance analysis comparing
actual performance to budgeted projections.

15. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

15.1 Client Ownership. The Client shall own all right, title, and interest in
the Oracle Park brand, logos, trademarks, service marks, trade dress, and all
other intellectual property associated with the Venue and the San Francisco
Giants (the "Venue IP").

15.2 Service Provider Property. The Service Provider shall retain all right,
title, and interest in its menu concepts, recipes, culinary techniques,
operational systems, training materials, POS system configurations, proprietary
vendor relationships, and proprietary management methodologies, tools, and
processes (the "Caterer IP").

15.3 License Grant. The Service Provider hereby grants the Client a
non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Service Provider's branding,
trade name, and logos on Venue materials, marketing collateral, and digital
platforms in connection with the promotion and operation of food and beverage
services at the Venue during the Term.

16. CONFIDENTIALITY

16.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all
Confidential Information of the other party disclosed in connection with this
Agreement. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, financial data
and attendance figures, revenue reports, per capita spending data, menu
development plans, vendor pricing, proprietary recipes and operational systems,
and any other non-public information concerning the Venue or either party's
business operations.

16.2 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section 16
shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of
five (5) years, provided that financial data and attendance figures shall remain
confidential for a period of five (5) years following the expiration or
termination of this Agreement.

17. TERMINATION

17.1 Termination Without Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement
without cause upon one hundred eighty (180) calendar days' prior written notice
to the other party.

17.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause
if the other party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement
and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving
written notice specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

17.3 Termination for Health Code Violations. Notwithstanding the cure period set
forth in Section 17.2, the Client may terminate this Agreement immediately upon
fifteen (15) calendar days' prior written notice if the Service Provider
receives a notice of violation from any health department authority relating to
food safety conditions at the Venue that, in the Client's reasonable judgment,
poses a risk to public health or safety, provided that the Service Provider has
not remediated such violation within the fifteen (15) day notice period.

17.4 Non-Renewal. Either party may decline to renew this Agreement at the end
of the Initial Term or any Renewal Term by providing the other party with at
least one hundred eighty (180) calendar days' prior written notice of its intent
not to renew.

17.5 Transition Obligations. Upon receipt of notice of termination or
non-renewal, the Service Provider shall cooperate fully in transitioning food
and beverage operations to the Client's designated successor caterer, including
the transfer of all recipes, vendor contacts, inventory, equipment, health
permits, and ABC licenses as permitted by applicable law.

18. LIABILITY

18.1 Limitation of Liability. TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE
LAW, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT,
INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT
OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE PERFORMANCE OF SERVICES HEREUNDER,
REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES ARE BASED ON CONTRACT, TORT, STRICT LIABILITY,
ANY OTHER LEGAL THEORY, AND EVEN IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY
OF SUCH DAMAGES.

18.2 Liability Cap. The Service Provider's total aggregate liability for all
claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed five million
dollars ($5,000,000.00) per individual incident or occurrence and ten million
dollars ($10,000,000.00) in the aggregate for all claims arising during any
twelve (12) month period.

19. INSURANCE

19.1 General Liability Insurance. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the
Service Provider shall maintain commercial general liability insurance in an
amount not less than five million dollars ($5,000,000.00) per occurrence, covering
bodily injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury arising from
the Service Provider's food and beverage operations at the Venue.

19.2 Liquor Liability Insurance. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the
Service Provider shall maintain liquor liability insurance in an amount not less
than three million dollars ($3,000,000.00) per occurrence, covering claims arising
from the sale, service, or furnishing of alcoholic beverages at the Venue.

19.3 Workers' Compensation Insurance. Throughout the term of this Agreement,
the Service Provider shall maintain workers' compensation insurance in accordance
with California statutory requirements, with employer's liability coverage in an
amount not less than two million dollars ($2,000,000.00).

19.4 Evidence of Coverage. The Service Provider shall provide certificates of
insurance evidencing the required coverage to the Client upon execution of this
Agreement and upon each renewal of such policies. All insurance policies shall
name the Client as an additional insured with respect to the general liability
coverage, and shall provide the Client with at least thirty (30) calendar days'
prior written notice of cancellation or material modification.

20. TERM

20.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall
continue for a period of thirty-six (36) months, concluding on March 14, 2028
(the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 17.

20.2 Auto-Renewal. Upon expiration of the Initial Term and any subsequent

Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve such dispute through
mediation administered by a mutually agreed-upon mediator in San Francisco,

21.2 Arbitration. If the parties are unable to resolve a dispute through
mediation within sixty (60) days of initiating the mediation process, such

in accordance with the commercial arbitration rules of the American Arbitration
Association (AAA) then in effect. The decision of the arbitrator(s) shall be
final and binding, and judgment upon the award may be entered in any court of
competent jurisdiction.

ARCHITECTURAL AND ENGINEERING DESIGN SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Architectural and Engineering Design Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number AED-2024-9021, is entered into as of September 1, 2024
(the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the advancement of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)
education has been recognized as a critical national priority, with educators,
policymakers, and industry leaders alike emphasizing the need for modern,
well-equipped learning environments that foster hands-on experimentation,
interdisciplinary collaboration, and innovation-driven pedagogy;

WHEREAS, the design of educational facilities has undergone a significant
transformation over the past two decades, moving away from traditional
lecture-hall-centric models toward integrated learning environments that
incorporate flexible classroom configurations, technology-rich maker spaces,
specialized laboratory facilities, and communal gathering areas designed to
encourage spontaneous interaction and cross-disciplinary exchange among students,
faculty, researchers, and industry partners;

WHEREAS, the Gulf Coast region of Texas has experienced substantial population
growth and increasing demand for higher education and workforce development
programs, particularly in the fields of energy technology, biomedical engineering,
advanced manufacturing, and computational sciences, creating an urgent need for
institutional infrastructure capable of supporting cutting-edge STEM education and
research programs;

WHEREAS, Gulf Coast Educational Foundation, a nonprofit organization dedicated to
expanding access to quality STEM education in the greater Houston metropolitan
area, has undertaken a capital project to construct a new state-of-the-art STEM
education center and has determined that professional architectural and engineering
design services are required for the planning, design, and construction
administration of such facility;

WHEREAS, Horizons Design Collaborative PLLC is a professional design firm with
demonstrated expertise in the architectural and engineering design of educational
and institutional facilities, including higher education science buildings, K-12
STEM academies, research laboratories, and community technical training centers,
and has been selected by the Client to provide comprehensive design services for
the Project;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements
set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and
sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1. PARTIES

1.1 Service Provider. Horizons Design Collaborative PLLC, a professional limited

"Design Team").

1.2 Client. Gulf Coast Educational Foundation, a nonprofit corporation duly
organized and existing under the laws of the State of Texas, with its principal
offices located at 3400 Memorial Drive, Houston, TX 77024 (hereinafter referred
to as "Client," "Owner," or "Foundation").

1.3 Individual Capacity. Each signatory hereto represents and warrants that they
have the full legal authority to bind their respective organization to the terms
and conditions of this Agreement, and that the execution of this Agreement has been
duly authorized by all necessary corporate or organizational approvals.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Addendum" means a written or graphic instrument issued by the Architect prior
to the opening of bids that modifies or interprets the bidding documents by adding
to, deleting from, or clarifying the requirements contained therein.

2.2 "Alternate" means an amount proposed for an alternate to the base bid that
involves a change in the scope of work, materials, equipment, or systems
specified in the construction documents.

2.3 "Bid Set" means the set of construction documents, specifications, and addenda
issued to bidders for the purpose of obtaining competitive bids for the
construction of the Project.

2.4 "BIM" or "Building Information Modeling" means a digital representation of
physical and functional characteristics of the Project, used for design,
coordination, documentation, and construction management purposes.

2.5 "Change Order" means a written instrument prepared by the Architect and signed
by the Owner, Contractor, and Architect authorizing a change in the work or an
adjustment in the contract sum or contract time.

2.6 "Construction Administration" means the architectural and engineering services
performed during the construction phase of the Project, including but not limited
to submittal review, site observations, response to requests for information, and
evaluation of payment applications.

2.7 "Construction Documents" means the drawings and specifications that set forth
in detail the requirements for the construction of the Project, including
architectural, structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, civil, and landscape
drawings and specifications.

2.8 "Design Development" means the phase of design services following schematic
design during which the design is refined and elaborated, including development of
floor plans, elevations, building sections, outline specifications, and
preliminary structural, mechanical, and electrical system designs.

2.9 "Estimated Construction Cost" means the Architect's best estimate of the total
cost of constructing the Project based on current market conditions, materials
pricing, and labor rates at the time of estimation, exclusive of the design fees,
land costs, financing costs, and owner-furnished equipment and furnishings.

2.10 "LEED" means Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design, a green building
certification system developed by the U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC).

2.11 "MEP" means mechanical, electrical, and plumbing engineering systems,
including heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC), electrical power
distribution and lighting, plumbing and sanitary systems, and fire protection
systems.

2.12 "Milestone" means a significant point or event in the progress of the design
or construction of the Project, as identified in the schedule of deliverables.

2.13 "Program" or "Space Program" means a document prepared by or on behalf of the
Client describing the functional requirements of the Project, including the types
and sizes of spaces, adjacency requirements, equipment needs, and performance
criteria for each space or functional area.

2.14 "RFI" means a Request for Information, a written inquiry submitted by the
Contractor to the Architect seeking clarification or interpretation of the
construction documents.

2.15 "Schematic Design" means the initial phase of design services during which
the Architect develops conceptual design alternatives illustrating the scale and
relationship of the Project components, including preliminary floor plans,
building massing, site layout, and conceptual exterior character studies.

2.16 "Shop Drawing" means a drawing, diagram, schedule, or other document
specifically prepared or assembled by or for the Contractor to illustrate a
specific portion of the work, including the fabrication or installation of
materials, equipment, or systems.

2.17 "Specification" means a written document prepared by the Architect or
consulting engineers that prescribes the quality standards, performance criteria,
and installation methods for materials, equipment, and systems incorporated into
the Project.

2.18 "Statute of Repose" means the statutory time period after substantial
completion of the Project within which a claim for architectural or engineering
professional negligence may be brought, regardless of when the claim is
discovered.

2.19 "Substantial Completion" means the stage in the progress of the work when the
Project or designated portion thereof is sufficiently complete, in accordance with
the construction documents, so that the Owner can occupy or utilize the facility
for its intended purpose.

2.20 "Value Engineering" means an organized approach to providing the necessary
functions of the Project at the lowest total cost over the life of the facility,
without compromising quality, performance, or reliability.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive architectural
and engineering design services for the Client's new STEM education center (the
"Project"), a new approximately 85,000 square foot facility to be constructed on
property owned or controlled by the Client. The Project encompasses laboratories,
classrooms, a 400-seat auditorium, maker spaces, faculty offices, and a central
atrium.

3.2 Architectural Design Services. The Service Provider shall provide full
architectural design services including schematic design, design development,
construction documents, and bidding assistance for the Project, encompassing all
building enclosure, interior architecture, and site design elements.

3.3 Engineering Design Services. The Service Provider shall provide engineering
design services in the following disciplines: structural engineering, mechanical
engineering (HVAC systems, plumbing systems, fire protection), electrical
engineering (power distribution, lighting, technology infrastructure), and civil
engineering (site grading, stormwater management, utility connections, paving).

3.4 Construction Administration Services. The Service Provider shall provide
construction administration services throughout the construction phase of the
Project, including periodic site observations, submittal review, response to
requests for information, evaluation of contractor payment applications, and
review of shop drawings.

4. DESIGN PROCESS

4.1 Schematic Design Phase. During the Schematic Design Phase, the Service
Provider shall prepare schematic design studies consistent with the approved Space
Program, including preliminary floor plans, building sections, exterior elevation
studies, site plan, and conceptual structural, mechanical, and electrical system
layouts. The Service Provider shall present the schematic design to the Client
for review and approval before proceeding to the Design Development Phase.

4.2 Design Development Phase. During the Design Development Phase, the Service
Provider shall develop the approved schematic design into a more detailed design
including refined floor plans, building sections, exterior elevations, interior
elevations of key spaces, outline specifications, preliminary structural design,
preliminary MEP system layouts, and updated cost estimates. The Service Provider
shall conduct a minimum of two (2) formal review cycles with the Client during
this phase.

4.3 Construction Documents Phase. During the Construction Documents Phase, the
Service Provider shall prepare complete construction documents and specifications
suitable for bidding and construction, including detailed architectural drawings,
structural engineering drawings and calculations, MEP engineering drawings and
specifications, civil engineering drawings, and all required technical
specifications. The Service Provider shall conduct a minimum of two (2) formal
review cycles with the Client and shall coordinate with the Client's independent
code consultant.

4.4 Bidding and Negotiation Phase. During the Bidding and Negotiation Phase, the
Service Provider shall assist the Client in obtaining competitive bids or
negotiated proposals from qualified contractors, including the issuance of addenda
as necessary, responses to bidder inquiries, evaluation of bids, and preparation
of a contractor recommendation report.

5. SUSTAINABILITY REQUIREMENTS

5.1 LEED Certification. The Project shall be designed to achieve a minimum of
LEED Silver certification under the LEED v4.1 Building Design and Construction
rating system. The Service Provider shall incorporate sustainable design strategies
including energy-efficient building envelope design, high-performance HVAC systems,
daylight harvesting, water conservation fixtures, and the use of materials with
recycled content and low volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions.

5.2 Energy Modeling. The Service Provider shall perform whole-building energy
modeling in accordance with ASHRAE Standard 90.1 to demonstrate that the proposed
design achieves a minimum of twenty percent (20%) improvement in energy performance
over the baseline building performance rating.

5.3 Materials Selection. The Service Provider shall specify materials and
finishes that contribute to LEED credit achievement, including materials with
recycled content, regionally sourced materials, certified wood products, and low-
emitting materials for adhesives, sealants, paints, coatings, and flooring systems.

6. CODE COMPLIANCE

6.1 Building Code. The Project shall be designed in compliance with the
International Building Code (IBC) as adopted and amended by the State of Texas,
and all applicable local building codes, zoning ordinances, and development
regulations of the jurisdiction in which the Project is located.

6.2 Accessibility. The Project shall comply with the Texas Accessibility Standards
(TAS) and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Accessibility Guidelines,
ensuring that all spaces, circulation paths, and building elements are accessible
to persons with disabilities.

6.3 Fire and Life Safety. The Service Provider shall design the Project to comply
with all applicable fire and life safety requirements, including but not limited
to occupancy classification, fire-resistance ratings, means of egress, fire alarm
and detection systems, automatic sprinkler systems, and emergency lighting.

7. STRUCTURAL ENGINEERING

7.1 Foundation Design. The Service Provider shall design the structural foundation
system for the Project based on a geotechnical investigation to be performed by a
licensed geotechnical engineer retained by the Client. The foundation design shall
account for the subsurface soil conditions, groundwater levels, and any site-
specific geotechnical recommendations.

7.2 Wind Load Requirements. Given the Project's location within the Gulf Coast
hurricane zone, the structural design shall conform to the wind load requirements
of ASCE 7-22 for Risk Category III facilities and shall incorporate design wind
speeds appropriate for the Houston metropolitan area as determined by the
applicable building code.

8. MEP ENGINEERING

8.1 HVAC Systems. The Service Provider shall design heating, ventilation, and air
conditioning systems appropriate for the Project's diverse space types, including
laboratory spaces requiring specialized ventilation and exhaust systems, general
classroom spaces, the auditorium, faculty offices, and common areas. Laboratory
ventilation systems shall comply with ASHRAE Standard 110 for laboratory fume hood
performance.

8.2 Plumbing Systems. The Service Provider shall design domestic water supply,
sanitary waste and vent, stormwater drainage, and natural gas distribution systems
for the Project. Laboratory plumbing shall include acid waste drainage, compressed
air, vacuum, and specialty gas distribution as required by the laboratory program.

8.3 Electrical Systems. The Service Provider shall design electrical power
distribution, emergency and standby power systems, interior and exterior lighting
systems, and technology infrastructure including structured cabling, wireless
access point infrastructure, and audiovisual systems for classrooms, laboratories,
and the auditorium.

8.4 Fire Protection. The Service Provider shall design automatic fire sprinkler
systems, fire alarm and detection systems, and fire department connection and
access provisions in accordance with NFPA 13, NFPA 72, and applicable building
code requirements.

9. CIVIL ENGINEERING

9.1 Site Work. The Service Provider shall provide civil engineering design for the
Project site, including grading and drainage plans, utility connection plans,
stormwater management design, parking lot design, and site accessibility
improvements.

9.2 Stormwater Management. The stormwater management system shall be designed in
compliance with the Harris County Flood Control District requirements and the
City of Houston stormwater management regulations, including on-site detention and
water quality treatment as required.

9.3 Utilities. The Service Provider shall design utility connections for municipal
water supply, sanitary sewer, electric service, natural gas, and
telecommunications, and shall coordinate with the respective utility providers for
service availability and connection requirements.

10. BIM REQUIREMENTS

10.1 Level of Development. The Service Provider shall develop Building Information
Models for the Project to a minimum Level of Development (LOD) of 300 for all
architectural, structural, and MEP disciplines at the completion of the
Construction Documents Phase.

10.2 Clash Detection. The Service Provider shall perform inter-disciplinary clash
detection using the BIM models at a minimum of three (3) stages during the
Construction Documents Phase: at the completion of the 50% construction documents
milestone, at the completion of the 90% construction documents milestone, and
prior to issuance of the final Bid Set.

10.3 Coordination Meetings. The Service Provider shall conduct regular BIM
coordination meetings with all engineering disciplines and the Client's
representatives at intervals not exceeding two (2) weeks during the Design
Development and Construction Documents Phases.

11. CONSTRUCTION ADMINISTRATION

11.1 Submittal Review. The Service Provider shall review and approve or reject
shop drawings, product data, samples, and other submittals from the Contractor in
accordance with the schedule established at the pre-construction meeting. The
Service Provider shall respond to submittals within ten (10) business days of
receipt.

11.2 Site Visits. The Service Provider shall conduct periodic site visits at
intervals appropriate to the stage of construction, generally not less than once
every two (2) weeks during active construction, to observe the progress and
quality of the work and to evaluate general conformance with the construction
documents.

11.3 RFI Response. The Service Provider shall respond to Requests for Information
from the Contractor within five (5) business days of receipt. RFIs requiring
coordination with consulting engineers may require up to ten (10) business days.

11.4 Payment Application Review. The Service Provider shall review Contractor
payment applications for conformance with the progress of the work and shall issue
a certification of payment to the Client recommending payment, partial withholding,
or rejection of the application within ten (10) business days of receipt.

12. BIDDING ASSISTANCE

12.1 Pre-Qualification. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in
establishing contractor pre-qualification criteria and shall evaluate contractor
pre-qualification applications for compliance with the established criteria.

12.2 Bid Evaluation. The Service Provider shall analyze bids received, identify
any significant deviations or qualifications in the bids, prepare a bid
comparison matrix, and provide a written recommendation to the Client regarding
the selection of the Contractor.

12.3 Contract Recommendation. Following bid evaluation, the Service Provider shall
assist the Client in preparing the construction contract and shall review the
contractor's proposed construction schedule for reasonableness and conformance
with the Project requirements.

13. OWNER-ARCHITECT-CONTRACTOR RELATIONS

13.1 Communication Protocols. All communications among the Owner, Architect, and
Contractor regarding the Project shall be documented in writing. The Architect
shall serve as the primary point of communication between the Owner and Contractor
on all matters relating to the design and construction documents.

13.2 Decision Authority. The Owner shall be the sole authority for decisions
regarding changes to the scope of the Project, the construction budget, and the
construction schedule. The Architect shall provide professional recommendations
and evaluations to assist the Owner in making informed decisions.

13.3 Schedule Impacts. The Architect shall promptly notify the Owner of any
schedule impacts resulting from design changes, code compliance requirements, or
unforeseen conditions discovered during construction, and shall recommend
appropriate corrective actions.

14. TERM

14.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall
continue for a period of thirty (30) months, concluding on February 28, 2027 (the
"Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 20. The Term
encompasses the full design, bidding assistance, and construction administration
phases for the Project.

14.2 No Auto-Renewal. This Agreement shall not automatically renew upon
expiration. Any extension or new engagement following the expiration of the Term
shall require the execution of a new agreement or written amendment signed by both
parties.

14.3 Effect of Termination. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall not
affect any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination,
including without limitation Sections 16 (Intellectual Property), 17
(Confidentiality), 19 (Liability), 20 (Termination), 21 (Insurance), 22 (Dispute
Resolution), and 23 (Governing Law).

15. COMPENSATION

15.1 Basis of Compensation. The professional fees for architectural and engineering
design services shall be based on a percentage of the Estimated Construction Cost
of the Project. The Estimated Construction Cost for purposes of this Agreement is
thirty-two million dollars ($32,000,000.00). In the event that the actual
construction cost differs from the Estimated Construction Cost by more than five
percent (5%), the fees shall be adjusted proportionally based on the actual
construction cost.

15.2 Architectural Design Fees. The Client shall pay the Service Provider
architectural design fees equal to eight percent (8%) of the Estimated Construction
Cost, totaling two million five hundred sixty thousand dollars ($2,560,000.00),
for all architectural design services described in this Agreement.

15.3 Engineering Design Fees. The Client shall pay the Service Provider engineering
design fees equal to four percent (4%) of the Estimated Construction Cost,
totaling one million two hundred eighty thousand dollars ($1,280,000.00), for all
structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire protection, and civil
engineering design services described in this Agreement.

15.4 Total Design Fees. The total professional fees for all design phases
(schematic design, design development, and construction documents) shall be three
million eight hundred forty thousand dollars ($3,840,000.00).

15.5 Construction Administration Fees. In addition to the design fees described
above, the Client shall pay the Service Provider construction administration fees
equal to two percent (2%) of the Estimated Construction Cost, totaling six hundred
forty thousand dollars ($640,000.00), for all construction administration services
described in this Agreement.

15.6 Total Professional Fees. The total professional fees under this Agreement
shall not exceed four million four hundred eighty thousand dollars ($4,480,000.00),
comprising design fees of $3,840,000.00 and construction administration fees of
$640,000.00, subject to adjustment as provided in Section 15.1.

15.7 Payment Terms. Payments shall be made based on monthly progress billing in
accordance with completion percentages for each design phase. The Service Provider
shall submit monthly invoices documenting the percentage of completion of each
design phase and the corresponding fee amount. The Client shall pay all undisputed
invoices within thirty (30) days of receipt.

15.8 Retainage. The Client shall withhold ten percent (10%) of each progress
payment as retainage. All retainage shall be released upon substantial completion
of the Project and the Service Provider's delivery of all final design documents,
record drawings, and closeout materials.

16. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

16.1 Client Ownership. The Client shall own all drawings, specifications, and
design documents produced under this Agreement for the specific Project (the
"Project Documents"). The Client shall have the right to use, reproduce, modify,
and permit others to use the Project Documents for the construction, maintenance,
operation, alteration, and addition to the Project.

16.2 Copyright Retention. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Service Provider
shall retain copyright in all Project Documents and design instruments produced
under this Agreement. The Service Provider shall have a perpetual, non-exclusive
license to use the designs, drawings, and images of the Project in the Service
Provider's portfolio, marketing materials, professional publications, and
competition submissions.

16.3 Restrictions. The Client shall not use the Project Documents for any project
other than the Project without the prior written consent of the Service Provider,
and shall not permit the Project Documents to be used by others for any project
other than the Project without such consent.

17. CONFIDENTIALITY

17.1 Obligations. Each party agrees to hold in strict confidence all Confidential
Information of the other party disclosed in connection with this Agreement,
including without limitation financial information, donor information (in the case
of the Client), and proprietary design methodologies (in the case of the Service
Provider).

17.2 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section 17
shall survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for a period of five
(5) years.

18. TERMINATION

18.1 General Termination. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon ninety
(90) calendar days' prior written notice to the other party, with the Client
obligated to pay the Service Provider for all work completed through the effective
date of termination.

18.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause if
the other party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement and
fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving written
notice specifying the nature of the breach in reasonable detail.

18.3 Payment Upon Termination. Upon termination for any reason, the Client shall
pay the Service Provider for all services satisfactorily performed through the
effective date of termination, plus reimbursement for any non-cancelable expenses
incurred on behalf of the Project, within thirty (30) days of the effective date
of termination.

19. LIABILITY

19.1 Limitation of Liability. TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW,
NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL,
CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED
TO LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF USE, BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, OR LOSS OF PROGRAM
FUNDING, ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE PERFORMANCE OF
SERVICES HEREUNDER, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES ARE BASED ON CONTRACT,
TORT, STRICT LIABILITY, ANY OTHER LEGAL THEORY, AND EVEN IF THE PARTY HAS BEEN
ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

19.2 Liability Cap. The Service Provider's total aggregate liability for all
claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed the total
professional fees payable under this Agreement, which shall not exceed four
million four hundred eighty thousand dollars ($4,480,000.00), regardless of the
form of action or legal theory.

19.3 Statute of Repose. No claim or cause of action arising out of or relating to
the professional services provided under this Agreement may be brought more than
ten (10) years after the date of Substantial Completion of the Project, regardless
of when such claim is discovered or when the act or omission giving rise to such
claim occurred.

20. INSURANCE

20.1 Professional Liability. Throughout the term of this Agreement and for a
period of five (5) years following substantial completion of the Project, the
Service Provider shall maintain professional liability (errors and omissions)
insurance in an amount not less than two million dollars ($2,000,000.00) per
claim, with an aggregate limit of not less than five million dollars
($5,000,000.00), covering claims arising from negligent acts, errors, or
omissions in the provision of architectural and engineering design services under
this Agreement.

20.2 General Liability. Throughout the term of this Agreement, the Service
Provider shall maintain commercial general liability insurance in an amount not
less than two million dollars ($2,000,000.00) per occurrence, covering bodily
injury, property damage, and personal and advertising injury arising from the
Service Provider's operations in connection with the Project.

20.3 Evidence of Coverage. The Service Provider shall provide certificates of
insurance evidencing the required coverage to the Client upon execution of this
Agreement and upon each renewal of such policies.

21. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

21.1 Negotiation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to this
Agreement, the parties shall first attempt to resolve such dispute through good
faith negotiations between senior executives of each party for a period of not
less than thirty (30) calendar days.

21.2 Mediation. If the parties are unable to resolve a dispute through
negotiation, such dispute shall be submitted to non-binding mediation administered
by a mutually agreed-upon mediator in Houston, Texas. Each party shall bear its
own costs of mediation.

21.3 Arbitration. If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be submitted to
binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in
accordance with its Construction Industry Arbitration Rules then in effect. The
arbitration shall be conducted in Houston, Texas. The decision of the arbitrator
shall be final and binding.

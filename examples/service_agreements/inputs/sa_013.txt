DATA ANALYTICS AND BUSINESS INTELLIGENCE SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Data Analytics and Business Intelligence Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number DAB-2025-0334, is entered into as of May 1, 2025 (the
"Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the grocery industry in the United States has experienced a period of
unprecedented data-driven transformation over the past decade, fueled by the rapid
proliferation of digital commerce platforms, the expansion of loyalty program
membership databases, the deployment of Internet of Things (IoT) sensors across
supply chain and in-store operations, and the growing consumer expectation for
personalized shopping experiences tailored to individual purchasing patterns and
dietary preferences. The modern grocery enterprise generates, processes, and stores
vast quantities of transactional, behavioral, and operational data on a daily basis,
creating both significant competitive opportunities and substantial analytical
challenges for organizations seeking to translate raw data into actionable business
intelligence;

WHEREAS, the competitive landscape of the Pacific Northwest grocery market has
intensified considerably in recent years, with national chains, regional players,
discount operators, and emerging e-commerce grocery platforms all vying for market
share in a region characterized by a growing population, elevated consumer
expectations for product quality and sustainability, and a regulatory environment
that increasingly emphasizes food safety traceability, environmental stewardship,
and transparent supply chain practices. In this environment, the ability to leverage
data analytics for strategic decision-making has become a critical differentiator
between high-performing and underperforming grocery retailers;

WHEREAS, the evolution of customer analytics within the grocery sector has progressed
from rudimentary point-of-sale transaction analysis to sophisticated multi-channel
customer intelligence platforms that integrate purchase history, digital engagement
metrics, demographic data, geospatial information, and real-time behavioral signals
to enable predictive modeling of customer preferences, churn risk assessment, basket
optimization, and targeted promotional effectiveness measurement. Organizations that
have invested in comprehensive business intelligence capabilities have demonstrated
measurable improvements in customer retention rates, average transaction values, and
promotional return on investment relative to peers relying on traditional
intuition-based decision-making approaches;

WHEREAS, the operational complexity of managing a regional grocery chain with
multiple store locations across diverse market areas creates substantial demand for
data-driven approaches to inventory optimization, labor scheduling, assortment
planning, pricing strategy, and supply chain efficiency. The interplay between
perishable product dynamics, seasonal demand fluctuations, competitive pricing
pressures, and consumer preference evolution requires analytical frameworks capable
of processing large volumes of multi-dimensional data in near-real-time to support
timely operational and strategic decisions;

WHEREAS, InsightMatrix Analytics Corp. is a leading provider of data analytics,
business intelligence, and machine learning consulting services with extensive
experience serving clients in the retail, consumer packaged goods, and grocery
sectors, and maintains a team of data scientists, data engineers, visualization
specialists, and analytics strategists with deep domain expertise in the analytical
challenges unique to multi-location grocery operations;

WHEREAS, FreshMart Grocers Inc. operates a chain of eighty-five (85) grocery stores
across the Pacific Northwest region and has determined that the development of a
comprehensive data analytics and business intelligence capability is essential to
supporting its growth strategy, improving operational efficiency, enhancing customer
experience, and maintaining competitive positioning in an increasingly data-driven
market environment;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements
set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and
sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "A/B Test" means a controlled experiment in which two or more variants of a
business variable (such as pricing, promotional placement, store layout, or product
assortment) are tested simultaneously with statistically equivalent customer
populations to determine which variant produces superior performance against a
predefined success metric, using appropriate randomization, sample size
determination, and statistical significance testing methodologies.

2.2 "Big Data" means datasets characterized by high volume, velocity, variety, and
veracity that require specialized data processing technologies, distributed
computing architectures, and advanced analytical techniques beyond those applicable
to traditional relational database environments.

2.3 "Business Intelligence" or "BI" means the technologies, applications, and
practices for the collection, integration, analysis, and presentation of business
information to support fact-based decision-making, encompassing reporting,
dashboards, ad-hoc analysis, data visualization, and self-service analytics
capabilities.

2.4 "Dashboard" means a visual display interface that consolidates and presents
key performance indicators, metrics, and data points in an organized, interactive
format designed to enable rapid comprehension of business performance and facilitate
data-driven decision-making at various organizational levels.

2.5 "Data Governance" means the framework of policies, processes, standards, and
roles that ensures the availability, usability, integrity, and security of the
data employed in the enterprise, including data quality management, metadata
management, data lineage tracking, access control administration, and regulatory
compliance monitoring.

2.6 "Data Lake" means a centralized data repository that stores structured,
semi-structured, and unstructured data at scale in its native format, enabling
flexible data processing, exploratory analysis, and machine learning model training
without the constraints of a predefined schema.

2.7 "Data Mart" means a subset of a data warehouse that is oriented toward a
specific business line, department, or analytical domain, containing consolidated
and aggregated data optimized for the analytical requirements of its intended user
community.

2.8 "Data Pipeline" means an automated series of data processing steps—including
ingestion, cleansing, transformation, validation, enrichment, and loading—that
moves data from source systems to target analytical environments in a reliable,
repeatable, and auditable manner.

2.9 "Data Warehouse" means a centralized, subject-oriented, time-variant, and
non-volatile data repository designed to support enterprise-wide reporting,
analysis, and decision-making, typically structured using dimensional modeling
techniques such as star schemas and snowflake schemas.

2.10 "ETL" means Extract, Transform, Load—the process of extracting data from
source systems, transforming the data into an appropriate format and structure for
analysis, and loading the transformed data into target data storage systems such
as data warehouses or data lakes.

2.11 "KPI" or "Key Performance Indicator" means a quantifiable metric used to
evaluate the success of an organization, department, project, or activity against
strategic and operational objectives, providing a measurable basis for performance
assessment and progress tracking.

2.12 "Machine Learning" means a subset of artificial intelligence in which
computational algorithms learn patterns and relationships from data without being
explicitly programmed for each specific task, enabling predictive modeling,
classification, clustering, anomaly detection, and recommendation generation.

2.13 "Predictive Model" means a mathematical or computational model, developed
using statistical or machine learning techniques, that estimates the probability
or expected value of a future outcome based on historical data and identified
patterns, subject to appropriate validation and performance monitoring.

2.14 "Statistical Significance" means the probability that an observed effect or
difference in an A/B test or analytical result is not attributable to random
variation, typically assessed using a predefined significance level (alpha) and
reported as a p-value, with results below the alpha threshold considered
statistically significant.

2.15 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by
either party to the other in connection with this Agreement, including but not
limited to business strategies, financial data, customer information, sales
figures, vendor relationships, operational procedures, and proprietary analytical
methodologies.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive data analytics
and business intelligence services to the Client, including but not limited to the
following service categories:

    (a) Data Warehouse Architecture Design. The Service Provider shall design and
    document a scalable data warehouse architecture to serve as the centralized
    analytical data repository for the Client's eighty-five (85) store operation,
    encompassing cloud platform selection and configuration, dimensional data
    modeling, schema design optimized for analytical query performance, and a
    comprehensive data architecture document describing the target-state
    infrastructure.

    (b) ETL Pipeline Development. The Service Provider shall develop, test, and
    deploy automated ETL pipelines for the ingestion, transformation, and loading
    of data from the Client's point-of-sale systems, inventory management
    platforms, customer loyalty databases, supply chain management systems, and
    other relevant source systems into the data warehouse. ETL development shall
    include error handling, data quality validation, scheduling, logging, and
    alerting mechanisms.

    (c) Dashboard and Visualization Creation. The Service Provider shall design
    and develop an executive dashboard suite providing interactive visualizations
    of key performance indicators across sales performance, inventory health,
    customer behavior, promotional effectiveness, and operational efficiency for
    the Client's store network. Dashboard design shall adhere to established
    visualization standards including accessibility compliance, responsive layout
    design, and drill-down interactivity.

    (d) Predictive Analytics Modeling. The Service Provider shall develop
    predictive analytics models for inventory optimization (including demand
    forecasting for perishable and non-perishable product categories, stockout
    risk prediction, and optimal replenishment timing) and customer behavior
    analysis (including customer lifetime value prediction, churn risk scoring,
and purchase propensity modeling). Model development shall encompass feature
    engineering, model selection, training, validation, deployment, and ongoing
    performance monitoring.

    (e) A/B Testing Framework Design. The Service Provider shall design and
    implement a structured A/B testing framework enabling the Client to conduct
    controlled experiments across its store network, including test design
    methodology, sample size calculation tools, statistical significance testing
    protocols, and a decision framework for interpreting results and implementing
    winning variations.

    (f) Executive Reporting. The Service Provider shall develop automated
    executive reporting capabilities providing periodic performance summaries,
    trend analyses, and actionable insights to support strategic decision-making
    by the Client's senior leadership team.

3.2 Data Requirements. The Client acknowledges that the predictive analytics
models described in Section 3.1(d) require a minimum of twelve (12) months of
historical data of sufficient quality and granularity to produce reliable model
outputs. If the Client is unable to provide the required historical data within
the timeframes specified in Section 7, the delivery timeline for the predictive
model deliverable shall be extended proportionally until adequate training data
becomes available. The Service Provider shall notify the Client in writing of
any data quality or completeness issues that may impact model development.

3.3 Anonymized Data Usage. The Service Provider may use patterns, trends, and
insights derived from anonymized and aggregated Client data to improve its
general analytical frameworks, methodologies, and industry benchmarks, provided
that no individually identifiable Client data, store-level data, or competitively
sensitive information is disclosed or retained by the Service Provider outside the
scope of this engagement.

4. DATA ARCHITECTURE

4.1 Cloud Platform. The Service Provider shall evaluate and recommend a cloud
data platform suitable for the Client's analytical workload requirements, data
volume projections, security and compliance requirements, and budget constraints.
The Service Provider shall assist the Client with platform provisioning,
configuration, and initial deployment.

4.2 Schema Design. The Service Provider shall design the target data warehouse
schema using dimensional modeling best practices, including the creation of fact
tables for sales transactions, inventory movements, and customer interactions,
and dimension tables for products, stores, customers, time periods, and
promotional campaigns.

4.3 Data Modeling. The Service Provider shall develop conceptual, logical, and
physical data models documenting the structure, relationships, and attributes of
the data warehouse, including data lineage mapping from source systems to
analytical outputs.

5. ETL DEVELOPMENT

5.1 Data Ingestion. The Service Provider shall implement data ingestion processes
for all identified source systems, supporting both batch and near-real-time
ingestion patterns as appropriate for each data source and analytical use case.

5.2 Data Transformation. The Service Provider shall develop transformation logic
for data cleansing, standardization, deduplication, enrichment, and conformance
to target schema requirements, including handling of data type conversions,
null value management, and business rule application.

5.3 Data Loading. The Service Provider shall implement efficient data loading
processes, including incremental loading strategies for high-volume transactional
data and full refresh processes for reference and dimension data.

5.4 Scheduling and Orchestration. The Service Provider shall implement scheduling
and orchestration of all ETL pipelines, including dependency management, retry
logic, and failure notification mechanisms.

5.5 Error Handling. The Service Provider shall implement comprehensive error
handling for all ETL processes, including data quality exception logging, dead
letter queues for unprocessable records, and escalation procedures for critical
pipeline failures.

6. DASHBOARD DESIGN

6.1 Visualization Standards. All dashboards and visualizations developed under this
Agreement shall adhere to industry-standard visualization best practices,
including appropriate chart type selection, color palette design optimized for
accessibility (including color-blind accessibility), consistent formatting, and
clear labeling of axes, legends, and data points.

6.2 Interactivity. Dashboards shall support interactive filtering, drill-down
capabilities, and parameter-driven analysis to enable users at various
organizational levels to explore data at the appropriate level of granularity.

6.3 Accessibility. Dashboard design shall comply with applicable accessibility
standards to ensure usability by individuals with visual impairments or other
disabilities.

7. PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS

7.1 Model Selection. The Service Provider shall evaluate and select appropriate
modeling techniques for each predictive use case, considering the nature of the
prediction target, available feature data, performance requirements, and
interpretability needs of the Client's business users.

7.2 Model Training. The Service Provider shall train predictive models using
historical Client data, employing appropriate train/validation/test split
methodologies and cross-validation techniques to ensure robust model performance
and generalizability.

7.3 Model Validation. The Service Provider shall validate all predictive models
against held-out test data and relevant business performance benchmarks prior to
deployment, documenting model performance metrics including accuracy, precision,
recall, and area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUC-ROC) as
applicable to each model type.

7.4 Model Deployment. The Service Provider shall deploy validated models to the
Client's production environment, including the development of scoring pipelines,
batch prediction jobs, and integration with relevant downstream systems.

7.5 Model Monitoring. The Service Provider shall implement model performance
monitoring to detect concept drift, data quality degradation, and performance
decline over time, and shall recommend model retraining schedules based on
observed monitoring results.

8. A/B TESTING

8.1 Test Design. The Service Provider shall develop standardized A/B test design
templates and protocols, including hypothesis formulation, primary and secondary
metric selection, randomization methodology, and test duration estimation.

8.2 Sample Size Calculation. The Service Provider shall provide sample size
calculation tools and guidance to ensure that A/B tests are adequately powered
to detect practically meaningful effect sizes at the desired statistical
significance level.

8.3 Statistical Methods. The Service Provider shall implement appropriate
statistical testing methods for A/B test analysis, including frequentist
hypothesis testing, confidence interval estimation, and sequential testing
procedures where appropriate.

8.4 Decision Framework. The Service Provider shall develop a decision framework
for interpreting A/B test results and translating statistical findings into
actionable business recommendations, including criteria for test continuation,
variant selection, and rollout strategy.

9. DATA GOVERNANCE

9.1 Data Quality. The Service Provider shall implement data quality monitoring
and validation processes to ensure the accuracy, completeness, consistency, and
timeliness of data flowing through the analytical infrastructure.

9.2 Data Lineage. The Service Provider shall document data lineage from source
systems through transformation processes to analytical outputs, enabling traceability
and auditability of reported metrics and model inputs.

9.3 Data Cataloging. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in establishing
a data catalog documenting the available data assets, their definitions, quality
profiles, ownership, and authorized usage.

9.4 Privacy. The Service Provider shall implement data privacy controls in
accordance with applicable data protection regulations and the Client's data
governance policies, including data anonymization and pseudonymization techniques
for analytical datasets.

10. SECURITY AND PRIVACY

10.1 Data Encryption. The Service Provider shall ensure that all Client data is
encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher and encrypted at rest using AES-256
or equivalent encryption standards within the analytical infrastructure.

10.2 Access Controls. The Service Provider shall implement role-based access
controls for all analytical systems, ensuring that users have access only to the
data and functionality required for their designated roles and responsibilities.

10.3 PCI Compliance. To the extent that the analytical infrastructure processes
payment card data, the Service Provider shall implement controls consistent with
the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) requirements applicable
to the Client's PCI validation level.

10.4 PII Handling. The Service Provider shall implement policies and technical
controls for the handling of personally identifiable information (PII) in
analytical datasets, including data minimization, purpose limitation, and
appropriate access restrictions.

12.3 Effect of Termination. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall not
affect any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination,
including without limitation Sections 14 (Intellectual Property), 15

13. COMPENSATION

13.1 Monthly Retainer. The Client shall pay the Service Provider a monthly
retainer fee of twenty-two thousand dollars ($22,000.00) per month (the "Monthly
Retainer"). The Monthly Retainer shall cover up to two hundred (200) hours of
professional services per calendar month.

13.2 Overtime. For any services rendered in excess of two hundred (200) hours
in a given calendar month, the Client shall pay the Service Provider an overtime
rate of one hundred seventy-five dollars ($175.00) per hour. The Service Provider
shall notify the Client in writing when projected monthly hours are expected to
exceed one hundred sixty (160) hours to allow the Client an opportunity to adjust
service priorities.

13.3 Data Infrastructure Setup Fee. In addition to the Monthly Retainer, the
Client shall pay the Service Provider a one-time data infrastructure setup fee
of forty-five thousand dollars ($45,000.00), payable in full within thirty (30)
calendar days of the Effective Date. This fee covers the initial cloud platform
provisioning, environment configuration, security baseline implementation, and
foundational infrastructure components required to support the analytics
environment.

13.4 Payment Terms. Invoices for the Monthly Retainer and any applicable overtime
charges shall be submitted monthly and shall be payable by the Client within
thirty (30) calendar days of receipt. All fees are quoted in United States dollars.

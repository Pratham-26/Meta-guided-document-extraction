CONSTRUCTION PROJECT MANAGEMENT SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Construction Project Management Services Agreement (the "Agreement"), bearing
reference number CPM-2024-5507, is entered into as of April 15, 2024 (the "Effective
Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, urban development in the Pacific Northwest has experienced unprecedented growth
over the past two decades, driven by population influx, economic diversification, and
the expansion of technology and healthcare sectors in the Portland metropolitan area.
The construction industry has responded to this demand with increasingly complex and
ambitious building projects that integrate residential, commercial, and public spaces
into cohesive mixed-use developments. These projects require sophisticated project
management expertise to navigate the intricate web of design coordination, regulatory
compliance, contractor management, and stakeholder communication that characterizes
modern urban construction;

WHEREAS, sustainable building practices have become a central consideration in
contemporary construction, with the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED)
certification framework serving as the predominant standard for measuring environmental
performance in the built environment. LEED-certified buildings demonstrate reduced energy
consumption, lower water usage, improved indoor air quality, and minimized construction
waste, contributing to both environmental sustainability and long-term operational cost
savings. The pursuit of LEED Gold certification, as contemplated for the project described
herein, requires meticulous planning, material selection, and construction process
management to satisfy the rigorous prerequisites and credit requirements established by
the United States Green Building Council (USGBC);

WHEREAS, the construction industry has witnessed significant transformation in recent
years through the adoption of Building Information Modeling (BIM), advanced project
scheduling tools, lean construction methodologies, and integrated project delivery
approaches. These innovations have enhanced the ability of project management professionals
to coordinate complex design processes, manage multi-trade construction sequences, control
project costs, and maintain schedule adherence across large-scale developments. The
application of Critical Path Method (CPM) scheduling, Gantt Chart visualization, and
Earned Value Management (EVM) analysis enables project managers to identify schedule
bottlenecks, allocate resources efficiently, and provide stakeholders with transparent
and data-driven progress reporting;

WHEREAS, the management of construction projects involving multiple subcontractors,
specialized trade contractors, and regulatory agencies requires a comprehensive
understanding of construction law, safety regulations, environmental compliance
requirements, and community engagement practices. The construction project management
services contemplated by this Agreement are designed to provide the Client with
experienced professional oversight throughout all phases of the project, from pre-
construction planning through substantial completion and closeout;

WHEREAS, the parties desire to engage in a collaborative relationship wherein the
Service Provider shall furnish professional construction project management services
for the development of a twelve-story mixed-use building in Portland, Oregon, and the
Client shall retain the Service Provider to coordinate and oversee all aspects of the
construction process as more fully described herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set
forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency
of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Building Information Modeling" or "BIM" means a digital representation of physical
and functional characteristics of the Project, used for design coordination, clash
detection, quantity takeoff, and construction sequencing throughout the project lifecycle.

2.2 "Change Order" means a written document, approved by both the Client and the Service
Provider, that authorizes a modification to the original scope of work, schedule, or
budget for the Project, including additions, deletions, or revisions to the construction
documents, specifications, or contract requirements.

2.3 "Critical Path" means the sequence of activities in the project schedule that
determines the minimum project duration, where any delay to a critical path activity
will directly delay the overall project completion date unless recovered through schedule
acceleration measures.

2.4 "Earned Value Management" or "EVM" means a project management methodology that
integrates scope, schedule, and cost baselines to objectively measure project performance
and progress through the calculation of key performance indicators including Schedule
Performance Index (SPI) and Cost Performance Index (CPI).

2.5 "General Contractor" or "GC" means the entity selected by the Client, with input
from the Service Provider, to serve as the primary contractor responsible for the overall
construction of the Project, including the management and coordination of all
Subcontractors and trade contractors.

2.6 "Gantt Chart" means a type of bar chart that illustrates a project schedule, showing
the start and finish dates of the various elements and activities of the Project,
along with dependencies between activities and the current status of each work item.

2.7 "LEED" means the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design green building rating
system developed by the United States Green Building Council (USGBC), providing a
framework for identifying and implementing practical and measurable green building design,
construction, operations, and maintenance solutions.

2.8 "Milestone" means a significant point or event in the Project schedule that marks
the completion of a major phase of work, a key deliverable, or a critical decision point,
as identified in the Project Management Plan.

2.9 "Permit" means any license, certificate, approval, authorization, or consent issued
by a governmental authority having jurisdiction over the Project that is required for the
design, construction, operation, or occupancy of the Project, including without limitation
building permits, grading permits, environmental permits, utility connection permits, and
certificates of occupancy.

2.10 "Punch List" means a list of incomplete or non-conforming work items identified
during the final inspection of the Project that must be corrected or completed by the
General Contractor or applicable Subcontractors prior to the issuance of the Certificate
of Substantial Completion.

2.11 "RFI" or "Request for Information" means a formal written request submitted by the
General Contractor, a Subcontractor, or the Service Provider to the design team seeking
clarification, interpretation, or additional information regarding the construction
documents, specifications, or contract requirements.

2.12 "Subcontractor" means any entity, other than the General Contractor, engaged to
perform a portion of the construction work on the Project, whether directly by the Client,
by the General Contractor, or by another Subcontractor.

2.13 "Value Engineering" or "VE" means a systematic and organized approach to providing
the necessary functions of the Project at the lowest total cost over the life of the
Project, without compromising quality, reliability, or performance requirements, through
the analysis of proposed materials, systems, and construction methods.

2.14 "Substantial Completion" means the stage in the progress of the Project when the
work is sufficiently complete, in accordance with the construction documents, so that the
Client can occupy or utilize the Project for its intended purpose, notwithstanding the
existence of minor items of work to be completed or corrected as set forth on the
Punch List.

2.15 "Project" means the design, development, and construction of a twelve-story
mixed-use building located in Portland, Oregon, as more fully described in Section 4
of this Agreement.

2.16 "Project Management Plan" means the comprehensive planning document prepared by
the Service Provider that establishes the project objectives, scope, schedule, budget,
organizational structure, communication protocols, risk management strategies, quality
standards, and coordination procedures for the Project.

2.17 "Critical Path Method" or "CPM" means a project scheduling algorithm for planning
and controlling a project that determines the longest sequence of dependent activities
and identifies the minimum time needed to complete the Project.

2.18 "Schedule Performance Index" or "SPI" means the ratio of earned value to planned
value, used as a measure of schedule efficiency on the Project, where an SPI of less
than 1.0 indicates that the Project is behind schedule.

2.19 "Cost Performance Index" or "CPI" means the ratio of earned value to actual cost,
used as a measure of cost efficiency on the Project, where a CPI of less than 1.0
indicates that the Project is over budget.

2.20 "Certificate of Substantial Completion" means the formal written document, prepared
by the Service Provider and approved by the Client and the General Contractor, certifying
that the Project has reached the stage of Substantial Completion as defined herein.

2.21 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by either
party to the other, whether orally, in writing, by electronic transmission, or by
inspection, including but not limited to project plans, financial data, contractor
bids, proprietary methodologies, and business operations information.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 Construction Project Management Services. The Service Provider shall provide
comprehensive construction project management services for the Project, encompassing
the following core service areas:

    (a) Design Coordination. The Service Provider shall coordinate all design
    disciplines, including architectural, structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing,
    fire protection, and civil engineering, to ensure design integrity, constructability,
    and compliance with applicable building codes and LEED Gold certification requirements.
    The Service Provider shall facilitate regular design coordination meetings, manage
    the RFI process, review shop drawings and submittals, and resolve design conflicts
    through Building Information Modeling (BIM) coordination and clash detection.

    (b) Contractor Management. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in the
    selection, evaluation, and management of the General Contractor and key Subcontractors.
    Contractor management services shall include the development of contractor
    qualification criteria, bid evaluation support, contract negotiation assistance,
    performance monitoring, and dispute resolution facilitation.

    (c) Budget Tracking and Cost Control. The Service Provider shall establish and
    maintain a comprehensive project budget, track all project costs against the approved
    budget baseline, perform monthly cost forecasting using Earned Value Management (EVM)
    methodologies, review and approve contractor payment applications, and identify
    cost-saving opportunities through Value Engineering analysis.

    (d) Schedule Oversight. The Service Provider shall develop and maintain a detailed
    project schedule using Critical Path Method (CPM) scheduling techniques, monitor
    schedule performance through regular updates and Schedule Performance Index (SPI)
    calculations, identify schedule risks and recovery strategies, and report schedule
    status to the Client and project stakeholders.

    (e) Quality Assurance Inspections. The Service Provider shall implement a
    comprehensive quality assurance program for the Project, including the development
    of inspection checklists, coordination of third-party testing and inspections,
    review of testing and inspection reports, management of the Punch List process,
    and verification of compliance with construction documents and specifications.

    (f) Permit Management. The Service Provider shall manage all aspects of the
    permitting process for the Project, including the preparation and submission of
    permit applications, coordination with regulatory agencies, tracking of permit
    status, and resolution of plan review comments. The Service Provider shall maintain
    a comprehensive permit tracking log and ensure that all required permits are obtained
    prior to the commencement of related construction activities.

    (g) Stakeholder Reporting. The Service Provider shall provide regular project
    status reports to the Client and other designated stakeholders, including monthly
    progress reports, budget status reports, schedule updates, and ad-hoc reports as
    requested by the Client. Reporting shall include narrative descriptions of project
    progress, Gantt Chart visualizations, cost performance summaries, risk assessments,
    and photographs documenting construction progress.

4. PROJECT DESCRIPTION

4.1 Building Specifications. The Project consists of the design, development, and
construction of an approximately 185,000 gross square foot, twelve-story mixed-use
building located at a site to be identified by the Client in the Portland, Oregon
metropolitan area. The building program shall include:

    (a) Floors 1 through 3: Approximately 45,000 square feet of ground-floor retail
    and commercial space, including restaurant space with associated kitchen ventilation
    systems, and two additional floors of Class A commercial office space.

    (b) Floors 4 through 12: Approximately 120,000 square feet of residential space
    comprising one hundred twenty (120) residential units, including a mix of studio,
    one-bedroom, and two-bedroom apartments, with common amenity spaces including a
    fitness center, resident lounge, rooftop terrace, and indoor/outdoor common areas.

    (c) Below-Grade: Approximately 20,000 square feet of below-grade parking and
    building services, providing approximately sixty (60) parking spaces, mechanical
    rooms, electrical rooms, storage areas, and building maintenance facilities.

4.2 Sustainability Targets. The Project shall be designed and constructed to achieve
LEED Gold certification under the LEED BD+C: New Construction rating system. Key
sustainability features shall include high-performance building envelope with enhanced
insulation values, energy-efficient HVAC systems, water-conserving fixtures and
landscaping, on-site stormwater management, construction waste diversion targeting
seventy-five percent (75%) or greater, and the use of low-emitting materials for
adhesives, sealants, paints, coatings, and flooring systems.

4.3 Floor Plans. Detailed floor plans, elevations, sections, and construction details
shall be developed as part of the design process and incorporated into the construction
documents. The Service Provider shall coordinate the review and approval of floor plans
with the Client and all design consultants.

5. DESIGN COORDINATION PROCEDURES

5.1 BIM Coordination. The Service Provider shall require the General Contractor and
all major Subcontractors to participate in a Building Information Modeling (BIM)
coordination process. BIM models shall be used for spatial coordination, clash detection,
quantity takeoff, and construction sequencing. The Service Provider shall chair weekly
BIM coordination meetings and maintain a clash detection log documenting identified
conflicts and their resolution status.

5.2 Design Review. The Service Provider shall conduct regular design review sessions
with the design team to review construction documents for completeness, constructability,
code compliance, and coordination between design disciplines. The Service Provider shall
maintain a design review log documenting all comments, responses, and resolutions.

5.3 RFI Management. The Service Provider shall manage the Request for Information (RFI)
process, including receipt, logging, distribution, tracking, and closure of all RFIs.
The Service Provider shall establish target response times for RFIs and monitor
compliance with established response timeframes.

6. CONTRACTOR SELECTION AND MANAGEMENT

6.1 Contractor Qualification. The Service Provider shall develop contractor qualification
criteria for the General Contractor and key Subcontractors, including evaluation of
financial stability, relevant experience, safety record, bonding capacity, references,
and proposed project team composition. A Contractor Short List of pre-qualified
contractors shall be maintained by the Service Provider and provided to the Client as
set forth in Exhibit B (Contractor Short List, attached hereto but not included).

6.2 Bid Evaluation. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in the evaluation of
contractor bids and proposals, including the development of bid evaluation criteria,
the conduct of bid interviews, and the preparation of bid evaluation reports with
recommendations for contractor selection.

6.3 Performance Monitoring. The Service Provider shall monitor the performance of the
General Contractor and Subcontractors throughout the construction process, including
the evaluation of schedule adherence, quality of work, safety compliance, and
responsiveness to project requirements.

7. BUDGET AND COST CONTROL

7.1 Budget Baseline. The Service Provider shall establish a project budget baseline in
consultation with the Client, incorporating all project costs including construction
costs, soft costs, contingency allowances, and owner-furnished items. The budget
baseline shall serve as the basis for all cost tracking and reporting activities.

7.2 Cost Tracking. The Service Provider shall maintain a detailed cost tracking system
that records all project expenditures, compares actual costs to the budget baseline,
and forecasts costs to complete. Cost tracking reports shall be updated monthly and
included in the monthly progress reports provided to the Client.

7.3 Value Engineering. The Service Provider shall conduct Value Engineering reviews at
key project milestones to identify opportunities for cost reduction without compromising
quality, performance, or sustainability objectives. Value Engineering recommendations
shall be documented and submitted to the Client for review and approval.

8. SCHEDULE MANAGEMENT

8.1 Schedule Development. The Service Provider shall develop a detailed project schedule
using Critical Path Method (CPM) scheduling techniques. The schedule shall identify all
major project activities, milestones, and dependencies, and shall establish target start
and finish dates for each activity. The schedule shall be maintained in a Gantt Chart
format for ease of visualization and communication.

8.2 Schedule Updates. The Service Provider shall update the project schedule on a monthly
basis, incorporating actual progress data, revised activity durations, and any approved
Change Orders that affect the schedule. Schedule Performance Index (SPI) and Cost
Performance Index (CPI) metrics shall be calculated and reported to the Client as part
of the monthly progress reports.

8.3 Schedule Recovery. In the event that the project schedule falls behind the approved
baseline, the Service Provider shall develop and present to the Client a schedule recovery
plan identifying the measures necessary to return the project to the baseline schedule.

9. QUALITY ASSURANCE AND INSPECTIONS

9.1 Quality Assurance Program. The Service Provider shall develop and implement a
comprehensive quality assurance program for the Project, including the preparation of
quality assurance plans, inspection checklists, testing protocols, and documentation
requirements for all major construction activities and building systems.

9.2 Third-Party Inspections. The Service Provider shall coordinate all third-party
testing and inspections required by the construction documents, applicable building
codes, and the LEED certification process, including structural steel inspections,
concrete testing, fire protection system testing, and environmental monitoring.

9.3 Punch List Management. The Service Provider shall manage the Punch List process,
including the identification and documentation of incomplete or non-conforming work
items, coordination of corrective work with the General Contractor, verification of
completed corrections, and maintenance of the Punch List log through to final completion.

10. SAFETY MANAGEMENT

10.1 Safety Program Oversight. The Service Provider shall review and approve the General
Contractor's project-specific safety plan, monitor safety compliance through regular site
inspections, and coordinate safety meetings with the General Contractor and Subcontractors.
The Service Provider shall maintain a safety incident log and report safety performance
to the Client on a monthly basis.

10.2 Regulatory Compliance. The Service Provider shall ensure that all construction
activities comply with applicable Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)
regulations, Oregon OSHA requirements, and local safety ordinances.

11. PERMITTING AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

11.1 Permit Management. The Service Provider shall manage all aspects of the permitting
process for the Project, including the identification of all required permits, preparation
and submission of permit applications, coordination with the City of Portland Bureau of
Development Services and other regulatory agencies, tracking of permit status, and
resolution of plan review comments. A LEED Checklist documenting the targeted LEED Gold
certification credits is attached hereto as Exhibit A (LEED Checklist, attached hereto
but not included).

11.2 Regulatory Compliance. The Service Provider shall ensure that the Project complies
with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, codes, and ordinances,
including without limitation the Oregon Structural Specialty Code, Portland Zoning Code,
Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and environmental regulations.

12. ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE

12.1 Construction Waste Management. The Service Provider shall develop and implement a
construction waste management plan targeting a minimum of seventy-five percent (75%)
diversion of construction waste from landfills through recycling, reuse, and salvage.

12.2 Environmental Monitoring. The Service Provider shall coordinate environmental
monitoring activities as required by the Project permits, including stormwater management,
erosion control, dust mitigation, noise monitoring, and protection of adjacent properties
and natural resources.

13. COMMUNITY RELATIONS

13.1 Community Engagement. The Service Provider shall assist the Client in managing
community relations throughout the construction process, including coordination with
neighbors regarding construction activities, management of noise and vibration impacts,
and response to community inquiries and complaints.

13.2 Construction Impact Mitigation. The Service Provider shall develop and implement
a construction impact mitigation plan addressing traffic management, pedestrian safety,
parking impacts, dust and noise control, and protection of adjacent properties during
construction operations.

14. MONTHLY REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

14.1 Monthly Progress Reports. The Service Provider shall deliver comprehensive monthly
progress reports to the Client no later than the tenth (10th) business day of each
month. Each monthly progress report shall include:

    (a) A narrative description of construction activities completed during the
    reporting period and planned for the upcoming period;

    (b) An updated project schedule (Gantt Chart format) with actual and forecast
    completion dates for all major activities and milestones;

    (c) A budget status report comparing actual costs to the budget baseline, including
    Schedule Performance Index (SPI) and Cost Performance Index (CPI) calculations;

    (d) A summary of RFIs submitted, responded to, and pending;

    (e) A Change Order status report summarizing proposed, approved, and executed
    Change Orders;

    (f) A safety performance summary, including any incidents, near-misses, and
    corrective actions taken;

    (g) Photographs documenting construction progress at key locations on the Project.

14.2 Executive Summaries. In addition to the detailed monthly progress reports, the
Service Provider shall prepare quarterly executive summaries suitable for presentation
to the Client's investors, board members, and other senior stakeholders.

15. CHANGE ORDER PROCEDURES

15.1 Change Order Initiation. Any proposed change to the Project scope, schedule, or
budget shall be documented on a Change Order Request form prepared by the Service
Provider or the General Contractor, identifying the nature of the change, the reason
for the change, the estimated cost impact, and the estimated schedule impact.

15.2 Change Order Review and Approval. The Service Provider shall review all Change
Order Requests for completeness, accuracy, and consistency with the construction
documents, and shall provide a written recommendation to the Client regarding each
proposed change. No Change Order shall be executed without the prior written approval
of the Client.

15.3 Change Order Log. The Service Provider shall maintain a Change Order log tracking
all proposed, approved, and executed Change Orders, including their cost and schedule
impacts, for inclusion in the monthly progress reports.

16.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue
for a period of thirty (30) months, concluding on October 15, 2026 (the "Initial Term"),
unless earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions of Section 22.

16.2 No Automatic Renewal. This Agreement shall not automatically renew upon expiration
of the Initial Term. Any extension of this Agreement beyond the Initial Term shall require

16.3 Effect of Termination. Expiration or termination of this Agreement shall not affect
any provisions that by their nature are intended to survive termination, including without
limitation Sections 19 (Confidentiality), 21 (Liability), 24 (Dispute Resolution), 25

17. COMPENSATION

17.1 Fixed Project Fee. In consideration of the construction project management services
described in Section 3, the Client shall pay the Service Provider a fixed project fee in
the amount of four hundred twenty thousand dollars ($420,000.00) for the full project
duration.

17.2 Monthly Installments. The fixed project fee of four hundred twenty thousand dollars
($420,000.00) shall be payable in equal monthly installments of fourteen thousand dollars
($14,000.00) over the thirty (30) month term of this Agreement. Monthly invoices shall be
submitted by the Service Provider on the first business day of each calendar month and
shall be payable by the Client within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt.

17.3 Success Bonus. In addition to the fixed project fee, the Client shall pay the Service
Provider a success bonus in the amount of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) if the
Project achieves Substantial Completion on or before the target completion date and the
final project cost is at or below the approved budget baseline. The success bonus shall
be payable within thirty (30) calendar days of the issuance of the Certificate of
Substantial Completion.

17.4 Taxes. All fees payable under this Agreement are exclusive of applicable taxes. The
Client shall be responsible for any sales tax, use tax, or similar taxes imposed on the
services provided under this Agreement, except for taxes based on the Service Provider's
net income.

23. TERMINATION

23.1 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience
upon ninety (90) calendar days' prior written notice to the other party.

23.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for cause if the other
party commits a material breach of any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such
breach within thirty (30) calendar days after receiving written notice specifying the nature
of the breach in reasonable detail.

23.3 Termination upon Insolvency. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon
written notice if the other party becomes insolvent, files a petition in bankruptcy, is
adjudicated as bankrupt, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a receiver
appointed for a substantial portion of its assets.

23.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Service
Provider shall (a) deliver all completed and in-progress project documentation to the
Client, (b) return or securely destroy all Client Confidential Information in its
possession, and (c) provide reasonable transition assistance to facilitate the Client's
engagement of a successor project management firm, all at the Client's expense.

24. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

24.1 Mediation. In the event of any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement,
the parties shall first attempt to resolve such dispute through good faith mediation
administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in accordance with its Commercial
Mediation Procedures. The mediation shall be held in Portland, Oregon, and the parties shall
share equally the costs of the mediation.

24.2 Arbitration. If the dispute is not resolved through mediation within sixty (60) calendar
days of the initiation of mediation, either party may submit the dispute to binding
arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in accordance with
its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in Portland, Oregon,
before a single arbitrator with experience in construction law and project management.

not constitute a waiver of such party's right to enforce such provision or any other

FINANCIAL AUDIT AND ACCOUNTING SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Financial Audit and Accounting Services Agreement (the "Agreement"),
bearing reference number FAS-2025-0178, is entered into as of February 15, 2025
(the "Effective Date"), by and between the parties identified in Section 1 below.

WHEREAS, the agricultural sector of the United States economy represents a
critical component of the nation's food supply chain, contributing approximately
five percent of the gross domestic product and employing millions of workers
across farming, processing, distribution, and retail operations. The financial
complexity inherent in modern agribusiness operations has grown substantially in
recent decades, driven by commodity price volatility, escalating input costs,
increasingly sophisticated risk management instruments including futures, options,
and crop insurance products, expanding international trade relationships, and the
growing influence of environmental, social, and governance considerations on
lending and investment decisions within the agricultural finance community;

WHEREAS, the regulatory environment governing financial statement audits has
undergone significant transformation in the aftermath of corporate accounting
scandals and the subsequent enactment of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, the
Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2010, and the
establishment of the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board (PCAOB) as the
primary regulatory body overseeing the audits of public companies and the firms
that perform those audits. PCAOB inspection findings have consistently emphasized
the need for improved audit quality in areas including revenue recognition,
internal controls testing, fair value measurements, and the evaluation of going
concern assumptions, resulting in increasingly rigorous standards for audit
engagement planning, risk assessment, and substantive procedures;

WHEREAS, the application of generally accepted auditing standards (GAAS),
including the Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS) issued by the Auditing
Standards Board of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants
(AICPA) and, where applicable, the standards issued by the PCAOB, requires
auditors to exercise professional judgment in the planning and performance of
audit engagements, including the assessment of materiality, the identification
and assessment of risks of material misstatement, and the design and execution
of audit procedures responsive to those assessed risks;

WHEREAS, Sterling & Associates CPAs PLLC is a certified public accounting firm
with over thirty years of experience providing independent audit, tax, and
advisory services to middle-market companies across the agricultural,
manufacturing, and distribution sectors, and maintains professional liability
insurance coverage and adheres to the quality control standards set forth in
Statement on Quality Control Standards No. 8 (SQCS 8);

WHEREAS, Midwest Agribusiness Holdings Inc. is a leading agribusiness
conglomerate with annual revenues approximating one hundred eighty million
dollars ($180,000,000), operating grain elevator facilities, processing plants,
and distribution networks across the Midwestern United States, and has
determined that comprehensive independent audit, tax, and advisory services are
required to meet its financial reporting obligations, regulatory compliance
requirements, and stakeholder expectations;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and
agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the
receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as
follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Auditor Independence" means the condition in which the auditor is free
from personal, financial, or other relationships that may impair, or reasonably
appear to impair, the auditor's objectivity and integrity in the performance
of the audit engagement, as required by the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct,
SEC rules, and PCAOB standards.

2.2 "GAAP" means Generally Accepted Accounting Principles, as established by
the Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB) and applicable to the Client's
financial statements, including Accounting Standards Codification (ASC)
pronouncements, SEC Staff Accounting Bulletins, and related guidance.

2.3 "GAAS" means Generally Accepted Auditing Standards, as established by the
Auditing Standards Board of the AICPA and, where applicable, the PCAOB,
including the Statements on Auditing Standards and related interpretive guidance.

2.4 "Internal Control Deficiency" means a deficiency in internal control over
financial reporting that exists when the design or operation of a control does
not allow management or employees, in the normal course of performing their
assigned functions, to prevent, or detect and correct, misstatements on a
timely basis.

2.5 "Material Weakness" means a deficiency, or combination of deficiencies, in
internal control over financial reporting such that there is a reasonable
possibility that a material misstatement of the annual or interim financial
statements will not be prevented, or detected and corrected, on a timely basis.

2.6 "PCAOB" means the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, established
by the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 to oversee the audits of public companies
and related matters, including the registration, inspection, and discipline of
public accounting firms.

2.7 "Review Engagement" means a professional engagement in which the auditor
performs analytical procedures and makes inquiries to provide limited assurance
that no material modifications should be made to the financial statements in
accordance with applicable attestation standards.

2.8 "Significant Deficiency" means a deficiency, or combination of
deficiencies, in internal control over financial reporting that is less severe
than a material weakness, yet important enough to merit attention by those
charged with governance.

2.9 "SOX 404" means Section 404 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, which
requires management's assessment of internal controls over financial reporting
and, for accelerated filers, an auditor's attestation on the effectiveness of
internal controls over financial reporting.

2.10 "Unqualified Opinion" means an audit opinion in which the auditor
concludes that the financial statements present fairly, in all material
respects, the financial position, results of operations, and cash flows of the
entity in conformity with GAAP, without any material modifications or
qualifications.

2.11 "Going Concern" means the fundamental assumption in financial reporting
that an entity will continue to operate for a reasonable period of time,
generally at least twelve months from the date of the financial statements,
and will not be compelled to liquidate its assets or curtail the scope of its
operations at an unfavorable price.

2.12 "Related Party Transaction" means a transfer of resources, services, or
obligations between the entity and its affiliates, principal owners, management,
or members of their immediate families, regardless of whether a price is
charged.

2.13 "Management Letter" means a communication from the auditor to those
charged with governance and management that describes the auditor's
responsibilities under GAAS, the planned scope and timing of the audit, and
significant findings arising from the audit, including material weaknesses,
significant deficiencies, and other matters required or recommended to be
communicated.

2.14 "Engagement Letter" means a written agreement between the auditor and the
client that sets forth the terms of the audit engagement, the respective
responsibilities of management and the auditor, the scope of the engagement,
and the applicable reporting framework, prepared in accordance with AICPA
professional standards.

2.15 "Audit Work Papers" means the documented record of the auditor's
procedures performed, evidence obtained, and conclusions reached during the
course of the audit engagement, maintained in accordance with AICPA and PCAOB
record retention requirements.

2.16 "COSO Framework" means the Internal Control—Integrated Framework
published by the Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway
Commission, which provides a comprehensive framework for the evaluation of
internal control over financial reporting.

2.17 "Substantive Procedures" means audit procedures performed to detect
material misstatements at the assertion level, including tests of details of
classes of transactions, account balances, and disclosures, and substantive
analytical procedures.

2.18 "Materiality" means the magnitude of an omission or misstatement of
accounting information that, in light of surrounding circumstances, makes it
probable that the judgment of a reasonable person relying on the information
would have been changed or influenced by the omission or misstatement.

2.19 "Sampling Risk" means the risk that the auditor's conclusion based on a
sample may be different from the conclusion that would be reached if the
entire population were subjected to the same audit procedure.

2.20 "Analytical Procedures" means evaluations of financial information through
analysis of plausible relationships among both financial and, where
appropriate, non-financial data, including the investigation of identified
fluctuations and relationships that are inconsistent with other relevant
information or that deviate from expected values by a significant amount.

2.21 "Confidential Information" means all non-public financial data, business
information, trade secrets, tax returns, audit findings, management
representations, and other sensitive information disclosed by either party in
connection with this Agreement.

3. SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 General Scope. The Service Provider shall provide comprehensive financial
audit, tax, and advisory services to the Client, including but not limited to
the following service categories:

    (a) Independent Financial Statement Audits. Conduct independent audits of
    the Client's consolidated financial statements for fiscal years ending in
    2025 and 2026, in accordance with generally accepted auditing standards
    (GAAS) and, where applicable, PCAOB standards, to express an opinion on
    whether the consolidated financial statements present fairly, in all
    material respects, the financial position of the Client as of the
    respective fiscal year end dates and the results of operations and cash
    flows for each respective fiscal year, in conformity with GAAP.

    (b) Internal Controls Over Financial Reporting. Perform an assessment of
    the Client's internal controls over financial reporting, including the
    evaluation of the design and operating effectiveness of key controls
    across significant accounts and assertions, in accordance with the COSO
    Internal Control—Integrated Framework (2013) and applicable auditing
    standards.

    (c) SOX 404 Compliance Assessment. If and to the extent the Client is
    subject to Section 404 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002, the Service
    Provider shall perform an integrated audit encompassing both the audit of
    internal controls over financial reporting and the audit of the financial
    statements, and shall issue an attestation report on management's
    assessment of the effectiveness of internal controls over financial
    reporting. The scope and nature of SOX 404 compliance services shall be
    determined based on the Client's public filing status and applicable SEC
    requirements.

    (d) Tax Preparation and Advisory Services. Prepare all federal, state, and
    multi-state income tax returns for the Client and its subsidiaries,
    including corporate income tax returns (Form 1120), state income and
    franchise tax returns, and applicable multi-state tax apportionment
    schedules. Tax advisory services shall include tax planning consultation,
    analysis of tax positions and uncertain tax positions under ASC 740, and
    review of estimated tax payment schedules and tax provision calculations.

    (e) Quarterly Financial Reviews. Perform quarterly reviews of the Client's
    condensed consolidated financial statements for each quarter of fiscal
    years 2025 and 2026, in accordance with applicable review engagement
    standards, including the performance of analytical procedures and
    inquiries of management to provide limited assurance that no material
    modifications should be made to the interim financial statements.

4. AUDIT METHODOLOGY

4.1 Risk Assessment. The Service Provider shall conduct a comprehensive risk
assessment at the beginning of each audit engagement in accordance with AU-C
315 (or PCAOB AS 1101, as applicable), including the identification and
assessment of risks of material misstatement at the financial statement level
and the assertion level, consideration of fraud risks in accordance with
AU-C 240 (or PCAOB AS 2401), and evaluation of the Client's business
environment, internal control, and risk management processes.

4.2 Materiality Determination. The Service Provider shall determine overall
materiality and performance materiality for each audit engagement using
quantitative benchmarks appropriate for the Client's industry and
circumstances, including consideration of revenues, assets, pre-tax income,
and other relevant financial metrics. Materiality shall be documented in the
audit planning memorandum and shall be reviewed and, if necessary, revised
during the course of the audit as additional information becomes available.

4.3 Sampling. Where sampling is used as an audit procedure, the Service
Provider shall design and execute samples in accordance with AU-C 530 (or
PCAOB AS 2301, as applicable), including consideration of sampling risk, the
selection method (statistical or non-statistical), sample size, and the
evaluation of sample results. The Service Provider shall document the sampling
methodology, sample selection, testing performed, and conclusions reached.

4.4 Substantive Procedures. The Service Provider shall design and perform
substantive procedures responsive to the assessed risks of material
misstatement, including tests of details of transactions, account balances,
and disclosures, and substantive analytical procedures. The nature, timing,
and extent of substantive procedures shall be determined based on the
Service Provider's professional judgment, considering the assessed level of
risk, the nature of the Client's business and industry, and the results of
risk assessment procedures and tests of controls.

5. INTERNAL CONTROLS ASSESSMENT

5.1 COSO Framework Application. The Service Provider shall evaluate the
Client's internal controls over financial reporting using the COSO Internal
Control—Integrated Framework (2013), which encompasses five interrelated
components: control environment, risk assessment, control activities,
information and communication, and monitoring activities.

5.2 Control Testing. The Service Provider shall test the operating
effectiveness of key controls identified through the walkthrough process and
risk assessment, including the evaluation of the manner in which management
implemented the control, the consistency with which the control was applied,
and the manner in which the control was documented by the person performing
the control.

5.3 Deficiency Evaluation. The Service Provider shall evaluate identified
control deficiencies to determine whether they constitute significant
deficiencies or material weaknesses, in accordance with AU-C 265 (or PCAOB
AS 1301, as applicable), and shall communicate all identified deficiencies,
significant deficiencies, and material weaknesses to management and those
charged with governance in accordance with AU-C 265 and AU-C 260.

6. SOX 404 COMPLIANCE

6.1 Scoping. If the Client is subject to Section 404(b) of the Sarbanes-Oxley
Act, the Service Provider shall perform an integrated audit in accordance
with PCAOB AS 2201, encompassing both the audit of internal controls over
financial reporting and the audit of the financial statements. The scope of
the internal control audit shall include all significant accounts, classes of
transactions, disclosures, and relevant assertions, as determined through the
Service Provider's risk assessment procedures.

6.2 Testing. The Service Provider shall test the operating effectiveness of
controls that the Service Provider has determined are sufficiently important
to the audit of internal controls over financial reporting, considering the
nature of the Client's business, the assessed risk of material misstatement,
and the results of prior-year testing.

6.3 Reporting. If the Client is subject to Section 404(b), the Service
Provider shall issue an attestation report on management's assessment of the
effectiveness of internal controls over financial reporting in accordance with
PCAOB AS 2201 and PCAOB AT-C 205.

7. TAX SERVICES

7.1 Federal Tax Compliance. The Service Provider shall prepare the Client's
consolidated federal corporate income tax return (Form 1120) for each taxable
year ending during the term of this Agreement, including all required
schedules, elections, and supporting work papers.

7.2 State and Multi-State Compliance. The Service Provider shall prepare
state income and franchise tax returns for all jurisdictions in which the
Client is required to file, including multi-state tax apportionment
calculations, state modifications, and credits. The Service Provider shall
also prepare any required state composite returns, partnership returns, and
other entity-level returns for the Client's subsidiaries and affiliated
entities.

7.3 Tax Planning and Advisory. The Service Provider shall provide tax planning
consultation services, including analysis of the tax implications of proposed
business transactions, evaluation of tax positions and uncertain tax
positions under ASC 740 (FIN 48), review of estimated tax payment schedules,
tax provision calculations, and analysis of tax-efficient structures for the
Client's operations.

8. QUARTERLY REVIEWS

8.1 Analytical Procedures. The Service Provider shall perform analytical
procedures at the commencement of each quarterly review engagement to identify
any significant fluctuations or relationships in the Client's financial data
that may indicate the existence of material misstatements requiring further
investigation.

8.2 Inquiry. The Service Provider shall make inquiries of management and, as
appropriate, others within the Client's organization regarding matters
relevant to the Client's financial statements, including accounting policies
and practices, significant transactions and events, changes in business
conditions, and any matters that may affect the Client's ability to continue
as a going concern.

8.3 Limited Assurance. The quarterly review engagement is intended to provide
limited assurance that no material modifications should be made to the
condensed consolidated financial statements, in accordance with AR-C 100
(or PCAOB standards, as applicable). A review engagement is substantially
less in scope than an audit engagement and does not enable the Service
Provider to obtain assurance that the financial statements are free of
material misstatement.

9. INDEPENDENCE REQUIREMENTS

9.1 SEC Independence Rules. If the Client is subject to SEC reporting
requirements, the Service Provider shall comply with all applicable SEC
independence rules, including Rule 2-01 of Regulation S-X and related
interpretive guidance, which restrict the types of non-audit services that
may be provided to an audit client and prohibit certain financial
relationships between the auditor and the audit client.

9.2 AICPA Code of Conduct. The Service Provider shall comply with the AICPA
Code of Professional Conduct, including the conceptual framework approach to
evaluating threats to independence and the application of safeguards to
eliminate or reduce identified threats to an acceptable level.

9.3 Rotation Requirements. If applicable under PCAOB rules or SEC
regulations, the Service Provider shall comply with auditor and engagement
partner rotation requirements, including the rotation of the lead and
concurring audit partners after specified periods of service on the engagement.

10. COMMUNICATION WITH AUDIT COMMITTEE

10.1 Required Communications. The Service Provider shall communicate with the
Client's audit committee (or those charged with governance in the absence of
an audit committee) in accordance with AU-C 260 (or PCAOB AS 1301, as
applicable), including communications regarding the planned scope and timing
of the audit, significant findings from the audit, material weaknesses and
significant deficiencies in internal control, management integrity issues,
uncorrected misstatements, disagreements with management, and other matters
required to be communicated.

10.2 Management Letter. The Service Provider shall prepare and deliver a
management letter to the Client's audit committee and management at the
conclusion of each annual audit engagement, summarizing the audit scope and
findings, identified deficiencies in internal controls, recommended
improvements to financial reporting processes, and any other matters that the
Service Provider considers appropriate to communicate.

10.3 Other Matters. The Service Provider shall communicate to the audit
committee all other matters required by AU-C 260, including information about
the firm's quality control procedures, significant audit adjustments,
uncorrected misstatements, and the Service Provider's views about significant
accounting policies and practices.

15. COMPENSATION

15.1 Annual Audit Fee. The Client shall pay the Service Provider a fixed fee
of one hundred eighty-five thousand dollars ($185,000.00) per fiscal year for
the independent audit of the Client's consolidated financial statements and
assessment of internal controls over financial reporting, inclusive of all
fieldwork, reporting, management letter preparation, audit committee
presentations, and other costs associated with the completion of the annual
audit engagement.

15.2 Quarterly Review Fee. The Client shall pay the Service Provider a fee of
twenty-two thousand dollars ($22,000.00) per quarter for each quarterly review
engagement, totaling eighty-eight thousand dollars ($88,000.00) per fiscal
year, inclusive of all analytical procedures, inquiries, reporting, and other
costs associated with the completion of each quarterly review.

15.3 Tax Services Fee. The Client shall pay the Service Provider a fee of
thirty-five thousand dollars ($35,000.00) per fiscal year for federal, state,
and multi-state tax return preparation and tax advisory services described in
Section 7.

15.4 Total Annual Value. The aggregate annual value of services under this
Agreement shall approximate three hundred eight thousand dollars ($308,000.00),
comprising the annual audit fee of $185,000, the quarterly review fees of
$88,000, and the tax services fee of $35,000. These fees are subject to
adjustment in the event of significant changes in the scope of services
agreed upon by the parties in writing.

15.5 Payment Terms. Invoices shall be submitted upon the delivery of each
deliverable or upon the completion of each service milestone, and shall be
payable by the Client within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt. All fees
are quoted in United States dollars.
